# Pentagon Claims Transparency In New UAP Files Release

A newly released image shows a "football-shaped" object near Japan in 2024.

The Pentagon, in concert with NASA and the FBI, on May 8 released scores of reports, images and videos of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) in what the Trump administration says is a new push for transparency.

The latest batch of files builds on previously released information from the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), with varying degrees of redactions on documents wrriten as recently as early this year. Some of the files are repeats of previously disclosed images.

"These files, hidden behind classifications, have long fueled justified speculation—and it's time the American people see it for themselves," Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said in a statement.

Released as part of the Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (Pursue) initiative, the initial batch of 162 total records includes both historical and recent sightings. While the bulk of the reports, some 82 events, are related to U.S. military sources, 12 are from NASA files dating back to the Gemini, Apollo, and Skylab programs.

The initial release includes 28 video clips, most of them recorded as "unresolved UAP" events covering a vast geographic spread ranging from the Mediterranean and Middle East to Africa, Asia and North America. Around 50% of the reports involve mission reports, while 93 are linked to submissions to the AARO.

Of the reports from recent military operations across the globe, the bulk of the incidents involve General Atomics ASI MQ-9 Reapers operated by the U.S. Air Force. These reports largely reference individual UAPs observed through the MQ-9s full-motion video and other sensors. Pilots and sensor operators refer to the UAPs they identify in a series of different descriptions. For example, a special operations MQ-9 operating out of the United Arab Emirates in June 2024 reported a "hot, spherical object" with a vertical unwavering probe.

One such report submitted to AARO by the United States Central Command from an MQ-9 is typical of the material and includes a 1 min. 5 sec. video clip captured by an unidentified U.S. aircraft of a "diamond-shaped" object moving at 434 kt. (500 mph). The sighting, which was over Greece, is also notable as it reveals the UAP was only detectable via short-wave infrared (SWIR) sensor.

Multiple crewed aircraft report spotting UAPs during operations in the Middle East. For example, F-16s operating in Iraq in March 2023 reported seeing several bright objects maneuvering quickly through the aircraft's targeting pods. In August 2020, a U.S. Navy MH-60 operating in the Persian Gulf reported three small contacts appearing with a wing and airframe structure. In November 2016, a U.S. Navy P-8 observed a UAP acting similar to a sea-skimming missile, flying at a rate of almost 500 kt. above the ocean.

While the reports focus on UAP, they provide some insights into the state of military operations in the region. In February 2023, Air Force F-15Es operating in Syria spotted a UAP that appeared similar to a balloon near al-Shaddadi in northeastern Syria. The pilots in the report say they experienced X-band radar jamming from the Turkish border. In May 2022, an MQ-9 operating near western Syria reported a UAP flying north-northeast. During the mission, the MQ-9 was spotted and approached by a Russian Su-27 or Su-35 that flew close underneath the drone before the fighters returned to their operating base.

Other AARO files have been submitted by the FBI that account for 56 of the overall reports included in the initial batch. A proportion of these are historic, including well-known events within the UFO community such as the April 1964 "Socorro saucer" incident involving a UAP sighting by a New Mexico State Police officer. But the FBI submissions also include more recent events with more than 30 photo records from late 2025 which are described as still images "derived from U.S. government or military systems."

Many of the reports, including those from the FBI, are associated with sightings of orb-shaped objects. One heavily redacted FBI report submitted in 2025 to AARO covers a firsthand account of a UAP encounter at an unidentified U.S. military facility. In the report, an individual relayed to FBI agents that "he and other federal and state personnel conducted searches to where orbs had been previously seen. After searching the area with a helicopter, they found a "super-hot" orb hovering over the ground. The report goes on to say the orb was seen to travel for 20 mi. at a speed too fast for the helicopter in pursuit. An additional "swarm" of lights were seen moving in all directions.

Although further reports are due to be made public by the Pursue office, it remains unclear whether these will include more information about where and how information was collected, how the events have been classified and analyzed, and whether future releases will include full-resolution sensor data.
