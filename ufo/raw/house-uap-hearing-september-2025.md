# Military Whistleblowers Share New Evidence of Alleged UAP at September 2025 House Hearing
**Source:** https://defensescoop.com/2025/09/09/military-whistleblowers-share-new-evidence-alleged-uap-ufo-hearing/
**Author:** DefenseScoop staff
**Date:** September 9, 2025

## Overview

On **September 9, 2025**, the **House Oversight and Government Reform Committee's Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets** convened its first UAP-focused hearing, marking a significant new chapter in congressional UAP oversight. The hearing featured testimony from current and former U.S. military officials who presented detailed accounts of encounters with unidentified anomalous phenomena, including what may be the first public showing of military footage depicting an engagement with a UAP.

## Key Witnesses and Their Testimony

### Jeffrey Nuccetelli -- Air Force Veteran

**Jeffrey Nuccetelli**, an Air Force veteran with **16 years of service**, provided testimony about a series of UAP incidents that occurred between **2003 and 2005** near **Vandenberg Space Force Base** in California. His testimony centered on observations made during **National Reconnaissance Office (NRO)** satellite launches, which are among the most sensitive and closely monitored military operations.

Nuccetelli described massive objects that appeared "like flying buildings" and moved in ways that defied conventional aerodynamic explanation. These objects were reportedly observed in proximity to critical national security launch operations, raising both safety and security concerns.

Crucially, Nuccetelli emphasized that these were not isolated or unverified sightings: **"Each incident was witnessed by multiple personnel, documented, investigated and reported up the chain of command."** This claim of multiple-witness, formally documented encounters represents a stronger evidentiary foundation than single-observer reports. The fact that these incidents occurred at a military installation responsible for launching classified reconnaissance satellites adds a significant national security dimension.

### Dylan Borland -- Air Force Veteran

**Dylan Borland**, another Air Force veteran, made his **first public disclosure** about a **2012 UAP incident at Langley Air Force Base** in Virginia. Langley is home to Air Combat Command headquarters and is one of the most important Air Force installations in the country.

Beyond the incident itself, Borland's testimony focused on the **retaliation he experienced** after reporting his UAP encounter through official channels. He described **sustained harassment and obstruction of his professional career spanning over a decade**, providing a concrete case study of the chilling effect that reporting UAP encounters has had on military personnel. His account underscored why Congress has been pursuing enhanced whistleblower protections specifically for UAP-related disclosures.

### Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins -- Active-Duty U.S. Navy

Perhaps the most significant testimony came from **U.S. Navy Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins**, who became the **first active-duty Navy official to testify publicly about UAP** before Congress. With **23 years of active service**, Wiggins brought current operational credibility to the hearing.

Wiggins described a **2023 incident aboard the USS Jackson** (a littoral combat ship) involving a **"self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped" object** that emerged from the ocean. The object then linked up with three similar objects before the group disappeared, exhibiting **"high, synchronized, near-instantaneous acceleration."** Key technical details included:

- Detection on **multiple sensor systems including radar**
- **No sonic boom** despite apparent high-speed movement
- **No conventional propulsion signatures** detected
- **Self-luminous** characteristics (the object generated its own light)
- **Trans-medium travel** -- the object moved from underwater to airborne

The "Tic Tac" description directly echoes the famous **2004 USS Nimitz encounter** described by Commander David Fravor, suggesting either the same type of phenomena or a consistent pattern of observations across nearly two decades of Navy operations.

## Unprecedented Video Evidence

In a dramatic moment during the hearing, **Representative Eric Burlison** unveiled video footage showing a **U.S. military MQ-9 Reaper drone firing a Hellfire missile at a high-speed orb** off the coast of Yemen in **October 2024**. This appears to be the first publicly shown footage of a U.S. military engagement with an unidentified aerial object.

The footage reportedly showed the missile appearing to impact or reach the object, yet the orb **continued moving** without apparent damage or change in trajectory. The implications of this footage are significant: it suggests either that the object possessed extraordinary durability or defensive capabilities, or that the engagement dynamics were more complex than a simple intercept.

The Yemen context is also noteworthy, as the region has been an active combat zone due to Houthi rebel activities, meaning U.S. military assets in the area were operating under heightened alert with sophisticated tracking and weapons systems active.

## Expert and Advocacy Testimony

The hearing also featured testimony from supporting witnesses:

- **George Knapp**, veteran investigative journalist who has covered UAP topics for decades, provided context on the history of government secrecy around the phenomena.
- **Joe Spielberger** from the **Project on Government Oversight (POGO)**, a nonpartisan government accountability organization, addressed the structural and institutional barriers to transparency.

## Legislative Priorities Discussed

**Representative Anna Paulina Luna**, who chairs the Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, emphasized several legislative priorities emerging from the hearing:

- **Stronger whistleblower protections** specifically tailored to UAP disclosures, recognizing that existing protections have proven inadequate given the classified nature of the programs involved
- **Confidential reporting channels** for military personnel to report UAP encounters without fear of career retaliation
- **Declassification initiatives** to make UAP-related data available to Congress and, where appropriate, to the public

## Significance

This hearing represented several firsts: the first hearing by this specific task force focused on UAP, the first public testimony by an active-duty Navy official about UAP encounters, and the first public showing of military footage depicting a weapons engagement with an unidentified object. The combination of multiple credentialed military witnesses, sensor-corroborated evidence, and video footage marked an escalation in the quality and specificity of UAP-related congressional testimony compared to earlier hearings.

The hearing also demonstrated that congressional interest in UAP has survived the transition from the 118th to the 119th Congress, with new institutional structures (the declassification task force) taking up the issue alongside established oversight mechanisms.
