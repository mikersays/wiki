# AARO Functioning at Full Operational Capability as Lawmakers Prep for Classified UAP Briefing

**Author:** Brandi Vincent
**Date:** December 5, 2024
**Publication:** DefenseScoop
**URL:** https://defensescoop.com/2024/12/05/aaro-full-operational-capability-lawmakers-prep-classified-uap-briefing/

## Operational Milestone

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has achieved full operational status as of October 1, 2024. According to DOD spokesperson Sue Gough, this means the office now possesses "the requisite personnel, expertise, facilities and tools to carry out its core mission."

## What This Means in Practice

The achievement culminates a two-year development effort. AARO, formally established in mid-2022 by Deputy Defense Secretary Kathleen Hicks, was designed to investigate reports of unidentified anomalous phenomena that could impact national security. The office now has the infrastructure to systematically identify and assess incidents across military domains.

## Congressional Briefing

On December 6, 2024, AARO director Jon Kosloski — a former National Security Agency physics and engineering leader — was scheduled to participate in a classified briefing with House Oversight and Accountability Committee members. The session focused on reviewing AARO's annual report and addressing follow-up questions from previous hearings.

## Ongoing Context

Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin emphasized the department's commitment to investigating unexplainable phenomena, noting: "whether it's some really unexplained phenomena or it's just something that is explainable — we just have to get to the root cause."
