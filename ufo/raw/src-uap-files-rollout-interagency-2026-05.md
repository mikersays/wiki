# Pentagon releases 'never-before-seen' files detailing UFOs

The Pentagon on Friday, May 8, 2026, announced that it had released a first batch of "never-before-seen" files on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) after President Donald Trump directed the agency to do so earlier this year. The release marks the launch of an interagency declassification effort billed by officials as the most extensive UAP transparency push in U.S. history.

## What was released

A total of 162 files were posted to a new Pentagon UAP website at WAR.GOV/UFO. The release comprised:

- 120 PDFs
- 28 videos
- 14 images

The materials were drawn from the FBI, the Department of Defense (rebranded by the administration as the "Department of War"), NASA, and the State Department, and document eyewitness testimony, photos, and reports of UAP dating back decades and originating from incidents around the world.

The image set is a mix of suspicious aerial objects filmed by military aircraft and photographs taken on the lunar surface by NASA Apollo astronauts. Six of the photos come from the Apollo 12 and Apollo 17 missions and depict phenomena observed during those missions; one Apollo 17 surface photograph shows three dots in the sky above the moon. Apollo 17 voice transcripts included in the release describe operators observing "very bright particles or fragments" drifting past during maneuvers, with one crew member reporting "big ones on my window" that looked "like the Fourth of July," and another describing "very jagged, angular fragments that are tumbling."

The release also includes an FBI photo composite paired with an eyewitness sketch describing an "apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object" estimated at between 130 and 195 feet in length, and FBI photographs from New Year's Eve 1999 depicting purported UAP alongside U.S. aircraft. A core document in the trove is FBI case file 62-HQ-83894, covering the years 1947 to 1968 and concerning unidentified objects and so-called "flying discs," with 18 separate documents inside, including reports from Oak Ridge, Tennessee. Some cases acknowledged in the release remain unresolved, with the government noting it cannot make definitive determinations.

## Redactions

108 of the 162 files contain redactions. Officials said the redactions protect eyewitness identities, the locations of government facilities, and information about sensitive military sites. The Pentagon stated that no redaction removed information about the nature or existence of any UAP encounter itself.

## Rollout schedule

Officials said additional documents will be released on a rolling basis every few weeks as further records are declassified. The effort is being run as the Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (PURSUE) program, with the Department of War in the lead and the Office of the Director of National Intelligence coordinating the broader Intelligence Community contribution.

## Trump directive and political backdrop

The release follows a February 19, 2026, directive from President Trump, posted to Truth Social, instructing "the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies" to "begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs)" and "any and all other information connected to these highly complex" matters.

That directive itself came after former President Barack Obama, in a February 2026 podcast appearance, said "aliens are real." Asked about Obama's remark, Trump said Obama "gave classified information" and "he's not supposed to be doing that — he made a big mistake."

The Pentagon's most recent comprehensive review, the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) historical report issued in 2024, found no evidence that the U.S. government has ever recovered extraterrestrial technology or biological material. Trump himself has said publicly he is uncertain whether aliens exist. AARO, established in 2022 and at full operational capability since 2024, continues to analyze incoming UAP reports.

## Officials' statements

**Secretary of War Pete Hegseth**, in a written statement accompanying the release: "These files, hidden behind classifications, have long fueled justified speculation — and it's time the American people see it for themselves." Hegseth added that the Department of War is "in lockstep with President Trump" to bring "unprecedented transparency regarding our government's understanding of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena," calling the rollout a demonstration of "the Trump Administration's earnest commitment to unprecedented transparency."

**FBI Director Kash Patel** called the release "landmark," saying: "For the first time in history, the American people have unfettered access to declassified government files on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomenon — a level of transparency that no prior administration has delivered." Patel said the rollout was being led by the Department of War and Secretary Hegseth pursuant to Trump's February 19 order. Patel also said that he personally did not have any information indicating that the U.S. had ever recovered alien bodies from crashed UFOs.

**Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard**: "The American people have long sought transparency about the government's knowledge of unidentified anomalous phenomena." Gabbard said the Office of the Director of National Intelligence is "actively coordinating the Intelligence Community's declassification efforts with the Department of War to ensure a careful, comprehensive, and unprecedented review."

**NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman** applauded "President Trump's whole-of-government effort to bring greater transparency to the American people on unidentified anomalous phenomena," and committed NASA to following "the data" while remaining "candid about what we know to be true, what we have yet to understand, and all that remains to be discovered."
