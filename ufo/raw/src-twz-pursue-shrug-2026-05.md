# The Newly Released Government UFO Archives Will Leave You Shrugging

## Summary

The U.S. government has released 162 declassified videos, pictures, and documents
related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), spanning from the 1940s to the
2020s. The records originate from multiple agencies and include material on
claimed UAP sightings domestically, abroad, and on the surface of the moon. On
initial review, the author argues there is nothing groundbreaking in the release.

The release is the first batch under a new initiative called the Presidential
Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (PURSUE), described as an
interagency effort involving:

- The White House
- Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI)
- Department of Energy (DOE)
- Department of War (DOW), including the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)
- NASA
- FBI
- Other components of the U.S. intelligence community

The records released so far come from the U.S. military, the FBI, NASA, and the
State Department. Materials include videos, pictures of unidentified objects,
intelligence reports, statements regarding sightings, and diplomatic cables. Many
documents are partially redacted to omit names and other privacy-protected
information. Some records, including ones from the FBI and NASA, are reported to
have been released, at least in part, in the past.

## Origin of PURSUE

PURSUE follows a directive from President Donald Trump issued earlier in 2026.
In February, Trump announced on Truth Social that he would direct the Secretary
of War and other relevant agencies to identify and release government files
related to alien and extraterrestrial life, UAP, and UFOs.

The White House told TWZ that Trump directed the effort because he is "the most
transparent president in history" and that the administration is focused on
providing "maximum disclosure" so that the public "can ultimately make up their
own minds."

## Statements from officials

- **Secretary of War Pete Hegseth** said the Department of War is "in lockstep"
  with Trump to bring "unprecedented transparency" on UAP, noting that classified
  files have "long fueled justified speculation."
- **Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard** said ODNI is coordinating
  declassification across the intelligence community with the Department of War,
  describing the release as "the first in what will be an ongoing joint
  declassification and release effort."
- **FBI Director Kash Patel** described the release as a "landmark" giving the
  American people "unfettered access to declassified government files."
- **NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman** said NASA's job is to "follow the data
  and share what we learn" and to remain candid about what is known, unknown,
  and yet to be discovered.

A War Department official told TWZ that no media engagement is planned and that
the agency is not providing comment or assessment on the files overall or any
specific file.

## Background on AARO

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) had previously released multiple
tranches of declassified UAP-related records on a limited and inconsistent basis,
including imagery of unresolved sightings. Established in 2022, AARO acts as a
central manager within the U.S. military for tracking, reporting, and analyzing
UAP incidents, and as a repository for relevant intelligence assessments. The
office has been criticized by members of Congress on both sides of the aisle for
alleged stonewalling on UAP matters.

## Initial assessment of the contents

- **Apollo lunar imagery:** Images from several Apollo missions are described as
  standouts, but it is unclear what they show. An Apollo 17 (1972) image is
  already noted as likely a visual artifact rather than an object.
- **"Football-shaped" object near Japan:** An image purported to show a UAP
  resembling a football-shaped body near Japan looks consistent with a balloon.
  TWZ notes Chinese use of high-altitude balloons for intelligence-gathering is
  well established, including reported sightings around Japan and other Pacific
  countries, and references the 2023 Chinese spy balloon shot down over the U.S.
- **Unresolved imagery:** Questions are emerging about what is actually depicted
  in other "unresolved" images in the PURSUE archive.

Overall, little context is provided for the records — including prior
investigations and any conclusions drawn. Some investigations are reportedly
still ongoing.

## Author's critique

Trevithick reiterates a long-standing TWZ argument: the U.S. military can rapidly
release post-strike and incident video/imagery from advanced sensors when it fits
a desired narrative, but disclosures on UAP take far longer, if they come at all.
He notes American authorities still have not released imagery from the 2023
shootdowns of three still-unidentified objects over the U.S. and Canada despite
prior pledges, while the Canadian government has released some material.

He also flags broader concerns TWZ has raised for years: that adversary
intelligence-gathering activity (drones, balloons, etc.) is increasingly
muddled with the UAP topic, raising real national security stakes.

The author concludes that the full scale and scope of new details in PURSUE
remains to be seen, more releases are pledged, but at this point "there does not
look to be anything really revelatory" and readers should not get their hopes up.
TWZ plans further coverage as more information is released.
