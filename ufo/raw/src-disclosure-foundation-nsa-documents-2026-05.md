# UFO files released: New UAP documents from NSA obtained by Disclosure Foundation

The Disclosure Foundation, an advocacy group for unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), released more than 300 pages of intelligence reports from the National Security Agency (NSA) on UFO sightings following a Freedom of Information Act request. It follows the Trump administration's release of more than 160 files relating to UFOs earlier this month.

The Disclosure Foundation said many of the records were once classified as "TOP SECRET UMBRA" — a designation used for highly sensitive signals intelligence.

## How the Records Were Obtained

The Disclosure Foundation said the records were obtained through a Freedom of Information Act request and subsequent appeal, after the NSA initially denied access to the materials. According to the group, its legal team sought underlying documents tied to a previously known classified NSA memo.

The documents are connected to a 1980 FOIA lawsuit, during which the NSA's chief policy officer, Eugene Yeates, submitted classified affidavits in response to the request for documents. Though the "Yeates Memo" was declassified in 2009, supporting material remained withheld until this appeal succeeded.

Following a prolonged challenge, the agency's appeals authority determined that the blanket denial was improper, leading to the release of hundreds of pages of records.

Hunt Willis, the organization's chief legal officer, stated: "Despite this historic document release, the NSA still asserts considerable redactions and exemptions for UAP-related material that dates back to the 1960s."

Willis continued: "It is simply unacceptable for security classification exemptions to remain on government documents that predate the Civil Rights Act. We are committed to having the courts review the legitimacy of these redactions and holding these agencies accountable to the public transparency that Congress intended."

## What the Documents Contain

In one of the files — which were heavily redacted — a witness described a mysterious star-shaped object as "impossible to be an aircraft." The report said the object moved quickly in vertical motions at high altitude and "kept turning around." The object was described as having "white luminous light" and being slightly "bluish" in color.

Other documents detail an object with two yellow lights seen flying at a low altitude, silently changing directions from north to west, and another with "luminous radiation of 22 meters extending in a spiral pattern from a black center."

Another file bearing the "TOP SECRET UMBRA" marking detailed a "spherical or disc-like" UFO described as brighter than the sun and about half the moon's diameter.

The files also describe incidents involving military intercepts, including one report in which 13 MiG fighter jets were scrambled after military radar detected a single unidentified object. Another report mentioned six MiGs "attacking said UFO."

The documents include radar tracking of unidentified flying objects, visual sightings with altitude and heading data, multi-witness corroborated sightings, and objects displaying controlled behavior and anomalous characteristics. Some objects were assessed as "probably balloons," while others defied conventional explanation.

## What Was in the Pentagon's UFO Files?

Earlier in May, the Defense Department published more than 160 UFO-related files after President Donald Trump announced in February that he would direct Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to do so.

"President Trump directed the Department of War to identify and release government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena, and unidentified flying objects because he is the most transparent president in history," White House spokesperson Anna Kelly stated.

These documents included numerous reported sightings of UFOs in the sky, including accounts from several Apollo missions. One file outlines a technical crew debrief from Apollo 11, the mission that first landed humans on the moon in 1969. Others included transcripts of astronauts reporting seeing unexplained lights, particles and flashes during flights to the moon.

The Disclosure Foundation's legal team continues reviewing NSA redactions and has filed Mandatory Declassification Review requests for congressional briefings.
