# Anna Paulina Luna Says 'We Will Be Getting Those' UFO Files

Representative Anna Paulina Luna stated that the Pentagon missed an April 14 deadline to provide over 45 videos documenting alleged encounters with unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP). A March letter signed by Luna, a Florida Republican, requested videos reportedly showing spherical, cigar-shaped, and "Tic Tac"-like objects recorded by U.S. military platforms over war zones, oceans, and restricted airspace. Incidents cited include activity near Iran, Syria, U.S. bases and airports, and a 2023 incident over Lake Huron. The letter was addressed to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth.

Luna chairs the Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets. She previously criticized the Pentagon on social media, noting that no one had responded until her office reached out, suggesting someone failed to pass the letter to appropriate authorities.

The Pentagon responded with a statement to Newsweek, indicating that the Department of War's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) is working with the White House and federal agencies to consolidate existing UAP records and facilitate expeditious release of previously unseen UAP information.

According to a 2021 Pew Research survey, approximately two-thirds of Americans believe intelligent life likely exists elsewhere. President Donald Trump announced in February that he would direct his administration to identify and release government files concerning UFOs and extraterrestrial life, though Luna stated this directive has not been upheld.

Speaking to former Representative Matt Gaetz on his One America News Network show, Luna explained that she had her staff inquire about Pentagon responses and subsequently contacted the White House after the Pentagon refused to respond. She indicated the Pentagon confirmed receipt and offered to brief the House Oversight Committee at a future date, but added that such timing decisions were not the Pentagon's to make.

Luna expressed confidence in Secretary Hegseth's support for the president's declassification directive, stating: "So whoever's trying to be cute at the Pentagon is about to get rolled because a) that's not up to them; b) Pete would not undermine the president; and c) this stuff has been ordered for release."

She concluded her remarks by emphasizing: "And so we will be getting those files, whether or not they agree with us."

Defense Secretary Hegseth told reporters in February that he lacked a specific timeline for releasing files ordered by Trump but stated the department would be "in full compliance with that executive order."
