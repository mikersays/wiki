# UFO files show Buzz Aldrin saw a 'sizeable' object close to the moon and a 'fairly bright light source' that the Apollo 11 crew felt could be a laser

The Pentagon released a new batch of UFO files on Friday as President Donald Trump renewed public interest in "unidentified anomalous phenomena." Though declassification efforts spanned years, Trump previously teased the release, and he encouraged public interpretation with a Truth Social post: "Whereas previous Administrations have failed to be transparent on this subject, with these new Documents and Videos, the people can decide for themselves, 'WHAT THE HELL IS GOING ON?'"

The files comprise State Department cables, FBI documents, and NASA transcripts from crewed space flights. They document cases deemed unresolved by the government. The Pentagon describes them as "never-before-seen," though some had previously been made public. Experts caution that UAP videos are frequently misinterpreted by those unfamiliar with military technology. A 2024 Pentagon report found no evidence the U.S. recovered alien technology or confirmed alien life.

In a 1969 Apollo 11 debriefing, astronaut Aldrin described spotting "a 'sizeable' object close to the moon and a 'fairly bright light source'" that crew members believed "could be a laser."

Other notable incidents include a 1994 State Department cable describing a Tajik pilot and three Americans witnessing a brightly lit UAP "making 90 degree turns, doing corkscrews and maneuvering in circles at great rates of speed" over Kazakhstan.

The collection includes more than 20 military videos from locations including Syria, Japan, and North America. A 2023 Aegean Sea report documented a UAP making "multiple 90-degree turns at an estimated 80 mph." An intelligence official described encountering a "super-hot" orb traveling approximately 20 miles before spotting four or five additional orbs that "flared up and down."

An FBI interview with a drone pilot documented a "linear object" with light bright enough to "see bands within the light" that vanished after five to ten seconds.

A 1972 NASA photograph from Apollo 17 shows three dots in triangular formation. Pentagon analysis suggests it could be a "physical object," though no consensus exists regarding its nature.

Historical documents include a 1948 report from U.S. airmen in the Netherlands regarding recurring flying saucer sightings, with Swedish observers believing they originated from "no presently known culture on earth."

Sean Kirkpatrick, former director of the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office, identified a 2013 Middle East video showing an eight-pointed star-shaped aircraft as likely "a hot jet engine producing a diffraction pattern in the camera," cautioning that unsupported releases "only serve to fuel more speculation, conspiracy and arm-chair pseudoscience."

Some congressional Republicans demanded greater transparency. Representative Anna Paulina Luna stated additional videos would be released later. Representative Tim Burchett thanked Trump for "keeping his word" on disclosure while noting transparency "won't all happen at once."

The Sol Foundation called for legislation mandating "thorough" review of classified UAP records "with the aim of providing Americans with the full truth about longstanding government knowledge and programs concerning technologies and vehicles not of human origin."
