# UFO Files: Trump Admin's New Release Reveals Object Shot Down

President Donald Trump's administration has released a second batch of files relating to unidentified flying objects (UFOs), just two weeks after it released more than 160 documents.

"The Department of War is in lockstep with President Trump to bring unprecedented transparency regarding our government's understanding of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP)," Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said.

Hegseth added that the newly declassified material had "long fueled justified speculation" and said the administration wanted Americans to "see it for themselves."

The second release follows an earlier document dump ordered by Trump, who said the files were being released because of "the tremendous interest shown."

The Pentagon's public UAP archive has already drawn more than 1 billion hits since launching earlier this month.

### Lake Huron Shootdown Video Released

One of the most notable additions is Pentagon footage appearing to show the 2023 shootdown of an unidentified object over Lake Huron by a U.S. fighter jet.

The footage was included in materials assembled by the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) after lawmakers requested additional UAP records earlier this year.

The incident took place during heightened national concern over aerial intrusions shortly after a Chinese surveillance balloon crossed the U.S.

Though later reporting suggested the object may have been linked to a civilian hobby balloon group, the video remains one of the clearest military engagement clips included in the archive.

### Intelligence Officer Describes 'Two Large Orbs'

One of the most striking new documents comes from a U.S. intelligence officer, who reported seeing "two large orbs flare up" near his helicopter in late 2025.

The objects were described as oval-shaped, orange with a white or yellow center and emitting light in all directions.

The officer wrote that the orbs later appeared over nearby fighter jets.

"I remarked to the pilots that it seemed the same orbs we had encountered were now 'chasing' the fighters," he said.

He added that the orbs continued to flare around the aircraft for several minutes, forming a distinct triangle before disappearing.

### New Mexico 'Green Orb' Files Released

Another major release is a 116-page file documenting a cluster of sightings near Sandia, New Mexico, between 1948 and 1950. According to the Pentagon, the archive includes:

- 209 reports of "green orbs," "discs," and "fireballs."
- Accounts of objects maneuvering, vanishing or exploding.
- Investigations into residual copper powder found at several sites.

These cases contributed to Project Grudge, a short-lived U.S. Air Force program in 1949 that attempted to evaluate UFO sightings during the early Cold War.

### Apollo 12 Crew Audio Describes 'Streaks of Light'

The second batch also contains Apollo 12 audio recordings in which astronauts described seeing "streaks of lights" while attempting to sleep in darkness during the 1969 mission.

In the post-mission medical debrief, astronauts Pete Conrad, Richard Gordon and Alan Bean discussed flashes appearing even with their eyes closed.

NASA later concluded the phenomenon was caused by internal visual effects rather than external light sources.

The account closely mirrors material included in the Pentagon's first release earlier this month, including Apollo 11 debrief transcripts where Buzz Aldrin described several "unusual" observations in space.

### Additional Videos and Files Released

The new release also includes more than 50 videos and additional military encounter reports spanning multiple decades.

One infrared clip reportedly shows four unidentified objects moving over water near Iran in 2022, while another Coast Guard recording captures an object flying near an aircraft over the southeastern United States in 2024.

Other files detail encounters across U.S. Central Command's area of responsibility between 2018 and 2023, including incidents over the Persian Gulf.

One 2022 video reportedly shows several spherical objects repeatedly entering and exiting the water near a submarine, an event AARO flagged for further analysis.

The Pentagon stressed that the files vary significantly in quality, sourcing and verification standards, but said they were released as part of a broader transparency effort surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Lawmakers from both parties have continued pushing for greater disclosure around government-held UAP records and military encounter reports.
