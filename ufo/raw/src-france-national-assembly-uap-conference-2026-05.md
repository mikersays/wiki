# France's National Assembly to Host UAP Conference

France's National Assembly will host a conference on unidentified aerospace phenomena on June 29, 2026. MP Arnaud Saint-Martin (LFI) announced the event will take place in the largest hall of the National Assembly, accommodating 250 attendees, with support from the Space Studies National Center (CNES) and GEIPAN.

Pierre Henriet, MP (HOR) and vice president of the Parliamentary Office for the Evaluation of Scientific and Technological Choices (OPECST), will join Saint-Martin in organizing the symposium. The OPECST serves as a joint advisory body to the National Assembly and Senate, comprising 36 members tasked with informing Parliament about scientific and technological implications.

GEIPAN, established in 1977, operates under oversight of the Ministries of the Economy and Finance, Higher Education and Research, and the Armed Forces. The organization "collection, analysis, investigation, publication and archiving of reports of UAP sightings." Despite common misconceptions, "GEIPAN is not a research group focusing on UFOs, extraterrestrial life or other paranormal phenomena."

The group classifies sightings by abnormality and reliability levels following investigation. Saint-Martin described the symposium as "Research into unidentified aerospace phenomena beyond fantasy," noting it represents "the first symposium of its kind in the history of Parliament, featuring authoritative experts and specialists." Registration will open soon with seating limited to 250 places.
