# Hegseth FY27 Posture Testimony (House Armed Services, April 29, 2026)

**Document title:** RECORD VERSION — STATEMENT BY THE HONORABLE PETE HEGSETH, Secretary of War, BEFORE THE COMMITTEE ON ARMED SERVICES, UNITED STATES HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, SECOND SESSION, 119TH CONGRESS, ON DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE FISCAL YEAR 2027 BUDGET REQUEST, WEDNESDAY, April 29, 2026.

Hegseth testifies under the new "Department of War (Defense)" / "DoW" branding, alongside Chairman of the Joint Chiefs Gen. Dan Caine (the 22nd Chairman) and Jay Hurst (performing the duties of Under Secretary of War (Comptroller)/CFO). The President's proposed FY27 topline is $1.5 trillion, up from the FY26 $1 trillion topline.

## Opening framing

Three priorities: "Restore the warrior ethos; rebuild our military; and re-establish deterrence." Calls the FY27 request a generational investment that will "shift the entire military acquisition paradigm." Specifically calls out development of "Golden Dome for America," revitalization of shipbuilding, aviation, and drones, the next-generation F-47 fighter, and end-strength shaping. Touts savings achieved with the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE). Commits to passing a financial audit by 2028.

Continuing Resolutions are described as degrading deterrence and readiness; the Aviation Mishap Task Force has identified CRs as a major contributor to aviation mishaps.

## National Defense Strategy / four lines of effort

> "That unipolar moment is over – and we have an opportunity to define what comes next."

Four lines of effort:

1. Defend the U.S. Homeland
2. Deter China in the Indo-Pacific Through Strength, Not Confrontation
3. Increase Burden-Sharing with U.S. Allies and Partners
4. Supercharge the U.S. Defense Industrial Base

The first two are the "primary operational focus of the Joint Force." Hegseth says the War Department "will not be distracted by democracy-building, interventionism, undefined wars, regime change, climate change, woke moralizing, and feckless nation-building."

He cites recent operations as proof of "second-to-none operational flexibility": **Operations ROUGH RIDER, MIDNIGHT HAMMER, ABSOLUTE RESOLVE, and EPIC FURY.**

## Defend the U.S. Homeland

- Border: claims "the number of illegals crossing into our country is zero" thanks to surge of forces partnered with DHS/CBP.
- Cartels described as "the Al Qaeda of our hemisphere"; DoW is "tracking them. We are killing them. And we will keep killing them." A **Counter Cartel Coalition of the Americas** of 18 hemispheric partners has been established.
- **Golden Dome for America**: created by one of Trump's first executive orders, framed as "a revolutionary approach to defending our nation from advanced aerial threats."
- **CYBERCOM 2.0**: described as the most comprehensive overhaul of U.S. Cyber Command since its founding 15 years ago.
- Nuclear posture: "we will modernize our nation's nuclear triad. We will develop additional options to support deterrence and escalation management... And we will test nuclear weapons and nuclear delivery systems on an equal basis as others." (Notable: explicit reference to nuclear *testing*.)
- Western Hemisphere: invokes the **Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine**, codified in the new National Security Strategy. Names Panama Canal, Gulf of America, and Greenland as key terrain.

## Deter China in the Indo-Pacific Through Strength, Not Confrontation

- Says U.S.–China relations are "better and stronger than they've been in many years" after a Trump–Xi trade breakthrough in November 2025; reciprocal state visits planned in 2026.
- Mil-to-mil dialogue with the PLA expanded; groundwork laid at ASEAN in Malaysia "months ago."
- Quotes "speak softly but carry a big stick"; says U.S. is not trying to "strangle" China's growth, dominate or humiliate it, or change the Taiwan status quo.
- Strategy: "deterrence by denial" along the **First Island Chain (FIC)**.
- Acknowledges China's "rapid, formidable, and holistic" military buildup.

## Burden-Sharing

- NATO framing: "**NATO 1.0**" (Cold War, capable), "**NATO 2.0**" (post-Cold War, freeriding/diminished), and a desired "**NATO 3.0**" returning to warfighting roots.
- U.S. forces redeployed from **Romania** "last year without increasing risk to NATO's deterrent."
- Sharp criticism of allies on Iran:

> "In recent weeks, for instance, far too many of our NATO allies showed that they could not be relied upon to support our nation's operations against Iran—despite the fact that they themselves are far more reliant on Middle East energy markets than we are and also share our interest in denying Iran a nuclear weapons and otherwise degrading Iran's power projection capabilities. This is unconscionable—and we will remember."

- Republic of Korea named "a model ally"; Japan signaling raised defense spending; Australia released a new National Defense Strategy and AUKUS continues "full steam ahead."
- Middle East: "the Joint Force will always maintain the ability to take decisive action, as we are demonstrating now in **Operation EPIC FURY**." Israel called "a model ally."
- Model allies named: Israel, South Korea, Poland, Finland, the Baltics.

## Supercharging the Defense Industrial Base

- 23 Portfolio Acquisition Executives (PAEs) stood up: 6 Navy, 5 Air Force, 6 Space Force, 6 Army.
- Multi-year procurement authority for critical munitions emphasized.
- **Industry Equity** model: DoW takes equity stakes "where commercial markets are unwilling or unable" (e.g., critical minerals).
- America First Arms Transfer Strategy consolidates FMS under USW for Acquisition & Sustainment.

## Research and Engineering

- AI strategy: COTS AI tools deployed broadly to warfighters/civilians; "Pace Setting Projects" via the Under Secretary of War for Research and Engineering (USW(R&E)) replace traditional Programs of Record for AI.
- **Office of Strategic Capital (OSC)**: ~$210 billion lending authority; advisory services; catalyst for private investment.
- **Directed Energy**: DIB only postured to produce a "limited number of prototypes"; goal is to scale to "tens to hundreds of units" through demand-signaling.

### Anomalous Health Incidents (AHIs)

> "Under this Administration, we are fully meeting the intent of Congress on Anomalous Health Incidents, or AHIs. We have added a Navy Rear Admiral who is both a SEAL and an MD to the leadership team... we have approved the first HAVANA Act payment to victims, with the DoW disbursing nearly $3 million in compensation since President Trump's Inauguration."

The AHI Cross-Functional Team (CFT) has been realigned to OUSW(R&E). Goal: "get ahead of any potential threat and eliminate the potential for adversarial surprise."

### Biotechnology

Bioindustrial Commercialization Program; emphasis on domestic biomanufacturing and reducing foreign-source reliance for biological materials.

## Personnel and Readiness

- Military Health System (MHS) reform; transition of MTF administration to the Defense Health Agency (DHA) "conditions-based."
- DoW + VA + HHS combining $120 billion in pharmaceutical purchasing power to onshore key starting materials and active pharmaceutical ingredients.
- **Pacific Multi-Domain Training and Experimentation Capability (PMTEC)** highlighted as key for Indo-Pacific deterrence.
- "Department of War Education Activity (DoWEA)" (formerly DoDEA): teacher-hiring overhaul projects 100% classroom fill at start of next school year; parental access policies expanded; EO 14191 school choice.
- Recruiting: "strongest performance in three decades"; new Military Service Recruitment Task Force (June 2025); Army National Guard's "Uncommon is Calling" campaign.
- TBI / Blast Overpressure: Cognitive Monitoring Program with baselines for all new service members; future fielding of wearable blast sensors; Army ALARACT 022/2022 set lower PSI thresholds for explosive breaching.

## Intelligence and Security

- Space-based **Air Moving Target Indicator (AMTI)** capability under development.
- **MQ-9 Reaper** production restarted.
- Counterintelligence partnership with FBI emphasized.
- Strong support for **FISA Section 702** reauthorization.

### All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office and Unidentified Anomalous Phenomenon (verbatim)

This is the UAP/AARO section in full:

> "In recent years, the issue of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) has garnered significant public attention. The U.S. government has a responsibility to investigate these phenomena to determine whether they pose a threat to national security, and, at the President's direction, to be as transparent as possible with the American people about its findings. The All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) is the focal point for the government's efforts to detect, track, identify, and mitigate UAP. The AARO's mission is to bring a rigorous scientific and intelligence-based approach to the study of these reported phenomena. To further transparency, AARO regularly releases UAP documents of public interest, and will continue to brief Congress at all levels of classification."

> "The modernization of our intelligence and security capabilities is not a choice; it is a necessity. By investing in resilient space-based systems, sustaining our airborne platforms, proactively countering foreign intelligence threats, reauthorizing critical surveillance authorities, and maintaining transparency in the investigation of UAP, we can ensure that the United States remains the world's preeminent intelligence power."

## Fiscal and Budgetary Priorities

- **$1.5 trillion FY27 budget**, framed as a generational reindustrialization of the DIB.
- Specific investment areas: "space dominance, hypersonic weapons, autonomous warfare, nuclear deterrence, and conventional capabilities" — all called out as areas where strategic competitors (i.e., China, Russia) are investing.
- FY27 budget invests in "space superiority, establishing **drone dominance**, increasing magazine depth, and maintaining superiority across all warfighting domains."
- **Working Families Tax Cut Act (WFTCA)**: $153 billion in mandatory funding for defense; called a "down payment" on Peace Through Strength.
- Audit goal: clean opinion on consolidated DoW Working Capital Fund in 2027, consolidated DoW General Fund in 2028. AI to automate reconciliations and anomaly detection.

## Conclusion (key quote)

> "The threat environment we face is significant, complex, and rising. Conflict is not inevitable and let me stress once again that our goal is to prevent war through increasing U.S. strength. We long for peace, but we must prepare for war."

## Notable items relevant to UAP/drone-defense vault

- AARO is treated as a permanent, formal element of the intelligence enterprise — Hegseth commits to continued classified and unclassified briefings to Congress and to public document releases.
- "Anomalous" appears in two distinct contexts in the testimony: the AARO/UAP mission, and Anomalous Health Incidents (AHIs, i.e., Havana Syndrome). AHI CFT was moved under USW(R&E).
- "**Drone dominance**" is named as a top FY27 investment priority (alongside space superiority and magazine depth).
- "**Autonomous warfare**" is explicitly called out as a domain where strategic competitors are investing and which the U.S. must counter.
- **Operation EPIC FURY** is acknowledged as ongoing in the Middle East.
- **Golden Dome for America** is the named homeland aerial-threat-defense program.
- **NORTHCOM, NORAD, JIATF-401**: not named explicitly in this written testimony (drone-incursion / counter-drone homeland mission is implied via Golden Dome and CYBERCOM 2.0 framing but not detailed by command).
- The testimony uses "Department of War" / "DoW" throughout — consistent with the Trump-era rebrand referenced elsewhere in the vault.
