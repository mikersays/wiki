## Overview

As of 2025, the landscape of claims regarding non-human technology and reverse engineering programs has grown substantially since David Grusch's initial congressional testimony in July 2023. Multiple whistleblowers, congressional investigations, and investigative journalism efforts have added layers of detail to allegations that the United States government has recovered and attempted to reverse-engineer craft of non-human origin. This document synthesizes the current state of these claims, the evidence cited, official responses, and the key figures involved.

## The David Grusch Foundation

**David Charles Grusch**, a former U.S. Air Force intelligence officer and National Reconnaissance Office (NRO) representative to the UAP Task Force, remains the most prominent whistleblower in this space. In June 2023, Grusch went public with claims that:

- The U.S. government operates a **"multi-decade UAP crash retrieval and reverse engineering program"**
- The government has recovered **"non-human origin technical vehicles"** -- intact and partially intact craft
- **"Non-human biologics"** were recovered from crash sites (testified under oath before Congress in July 2023)
- A covert program has existed for decades, deliberately hidden from congressional oversight
- **"Quite a number"** of craft have been recovered, spanning decades
- Both the U.S. and other nations are in possession of recovered materials

Grusch filed a formal whistleblower complaint with the Intelligence Community Inspector General (ICIG), who found his complaint **"credible and urgent."** He was subsequently granted whistleblower protections and testified before the House Oversight Committee on July 26, 2023.

### Grusch's 2025 Activity

In 2025, Grusch has continued making public appearances, including a notable interview on **The Megyn Kelly Show** where he addressed topics including crash retrievals, legacy programs, misinformation campaigns, and what he described as deep-state dynamics surrounding the concealment of UAP programs. Elizondo publicly vouched for Grusch, calling him **"a patriot... a damn fine human being."**

## The Kona Blue Program

One of the more concrete revelations to emerge from government records involves **Kona Blue**, a proposed Department of Homeland Security program that was developed specifically to **reverse-engineer any alien technology recovered by the government**. Unlike many other alleged programs, Kona Blue's existence has been **officially acknowledged** by the Department of Defense.

However, the DoD has stated that Kona Blue **"never got off the ground"** because **"no such technology was ever located."** Skeptics see this as evidence against the existence of recovered non-human materials, while proponents argue that Kona Blue may have been a cover story or that materials are held within separate, more deeply classified programs that Kona Blue was not granted access to.

## Historical Reverse Engineering Claims

### Bob Lazar

The longest-standing claims of reverse engineering come from **Bob Lazar**, who in **1989** alleged that he worked as an engineer at a facility called **S-4 near Area 51** in Nevada. Lazar's key claims include:

- He personally observed **nine UFOs** undergoing reverse-engineering efforts at S-4
- The craft used propulsion systems capable of producing **gravity and warping the fabric of space**
- The propulsion relied on an exotic fuel source identified as **Element 115** (moscovium, which was later synthesized in 2003, lending some retroactive plausibility to his claims about its existence)
- Researchers were unable to fully understand or replicate the technology

Lazar's claims remain deeply controversial. He lacks verifiable employment records at Los Alamos National Laboratory (though some evidence suggests he did work there), and his educational credentials have not been confirmed by the institutions he claims to have attended.

### Alleged Derivative Technologies

Some proponents have claimed that certain modern technologies were derived from reverse-engineering efforts, including:

- **Fiber optics**
- **Microchips / integrated circuits**
- **Lasers**
- **Night vision technology**
- **Kevlar**

These claims are generally not supported by mainstream historical evidence, as documented development histories exist for each of these technologies tracing them to human researchers and incremental scientific progress. The claims persist in popular culture and among some UAP researchers.

## The AARO Report and Official Denial

The **All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)**, established by Congress in 2022 to investigate UAP, published a historical review report that concluded:

- **No evidence** exists that the U.S. government has recovered alien material
- **No evidence** of reverse-engineered UAP technology within U.S. government programs
- Many reported cases can be attributed to misidentifications of conventional objects or classified programs

The Pentagon's position, as articulated through AARO and official spokespeople, remains that there is no verifiable evidence of non-human technology in government possession. However, critics of the AARO report -- including several members of Congress -- have argued that the office lacked adequate access to the most sensitive compartmented programs and that its investigation was constrained by the very secrecy mechanisms it was tasked with penetrating.

## The Arms Race Dimension

Several former officials have alleged that recovered UAP technology has fueled a **"secretive arms race"** among nations. This framing positions the reverse engineering not merely as a scientific curiosity but as a matter of national security competition, where the first nation to successfully replicate UAP capabilities would gain decisive military advantage.

**Christopher Mellon** and others have argued that the secrecy surrounding these programs is driven not by concern about public panic but by the strategic value of any technological breakthroughs that might emerge from reverse engineering efforts. This framing has resonated with national security-minded lawmakers who might otherwise be skeptical of UAP claims.

## The Credibility Landscape

The credibility of reverse engineering claims as of 2025 rests on several pillars:

**Supporting factors:**
- Multiple independent whistleblowers making consistent claims
- Congressional willingness to take claims seriously and legislate around them
- The ICIG's finding that Grusch's complaint was "credible and urgent"
- Official acknowledgment of programs like Kona Blue
- Multiple sensor-corroborated military encounters (Nimitz 2004, Roosevelt 2014-2015, etc.)
- Growing number of military personnel willing to testify publicly

**Undermining factors:**
- No physical evidence has been publicly presented
- AARO's official finding of no evidence
- Claims rely primarily on testimony rather than testable evidence
- The scientific community broadly considers claims of extraterrestrial visitation unproven
- Historical precedent of UFO claims that later received conventional explanations
- Some key figures (e.g., Lazar) have credibility issues

## Current Status

As of late 2025, the situation remains in tension between:
1. An increasingly organized and legislatively supported disclosure movement
2. Official Pentagon denials and an AARO report finding no evidence
3. New whistleblowers continuing to emerge with supporting claims
4. Congressional provisions in the FY2026 NDAA requiring new briefings and classification reviews
5. The prospect of Elizondo's second book ("Reckoning") potentially introducing new claims in 2026

The debate has shifted from whether UAP phenomena deserve serious investigation (now broadly accepted) to whether the U.S. government possesses recovered materials of non-human origin (still deeply contested). The next major inflection points are likely to be the NDAA-mandated briefings on NORAD/NORTHCOM intercepts and any testimony from the alleged crash-retrieval program whistleblower referenced by Rep. Luna.
