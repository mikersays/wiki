# UFO File Release: What's Inside the 162 Secret Pentagon UAP Files? From Apollo Moon Photos to FBI's 'Mysterious' 62-HQ-83894 Case File

The United States Department of Defence has officially opened public access to a massive new collection of declassified UFO-related records, offering people around the world a rare look into decades of mysterious sightings, unexplained aerial encounters and classified investigations once hidden from public view.

The release comes under a new transparency initiative called the Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (PURSUE), announced on May 8. Through the new online archive, citizens can now access hundreds of previously restricted documents, videos and images connected to Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP), more commonly known as UFOs.

According to officials, this is only the beginning, with additional files expected to be uploaded in phases over the coming weeks and months.

## What is Included in the First UFO File Release?

The first batch, labelled "Release 01," contains 162 newly declassified files collected from agencies including the Federal Bureau of Investigation, National Aeronautics and Space Administration, the State Department and the Pentagon.

Officials said the collection includes:

- Around 120 PDF documents
- 28 classified or surveillance-style videos
- Approximately 14 photographs

Many of the cases remain officially "unresolved," meaning investigators could not determine what the observed objects actually were.

The Pentagon stated that some sections were still blacked out to protect witness identities, military facility locations and unrelated national security information. However, the agency added: "No redactions have been made to any files released under President Trump's directive concerning information about the nature or existence of any encounter reported as a UAP or related phenomena."

## Where Can People Watch or Download the UFO Files?

The newly released material is available through the Pentagon's dedicated UFO archive portal.

Users can access the database through the PURSUE UFO Archive Portal at www.war.gov/ufo/.

The website allows the public to browse videos, documents, infrared imagery and historical investigation records without requiring security clearance.

## UFO Files PDF

The newly released UFO Files PDF collection contains more than 160 declassified documents, videos and images shared by the United States Department of Defence under its new PURSUE archive initiative. The files include eyewitness accounts, FBI investigation records, military reports, infrared images and unexplained UAP sightings dating back several decades.

Several documents also feature previously restricted material linked to National Aeronautics and Space Administration missions, unidentified aerial encounters near military zones and historical FBI UFO case files.

## What Are the Most Shocking UFO Cases in the Files?

Several newly released files have already sparked major discussion online because of the unusual details described by investigators and witnesses.

One report includes infrared images captured over the western United States in late 2025, showing unidentified flying objects moving at unusual speeds.

Another file details a 2024 sighting near Japan reported by US Indo-Pacific Command personnel. Witnesses described seeing a football-shaped airborne object that investigators could not identify.

A separate video recorded near Greece in October 2023 reportedly showed an unidentified object making sudden "90-degree turns" while travelling at nearly 80 miles per hour.

The archive also includes footage from Syria showing two orange semi-transparent aerial formations appearing briefly before disappearing seconds later.

## Do the Files Include Apollo Moon Mission UFO Photos?

Yes. One of the biggest revelations involves photographs taken during the Apollo 12 Moon Landing and the Apollo 17 Moon Landing. According to the released documents, astronauts captured several unexplained lights and unidentified objects above the Moon's surface during the missions.

The Pentagon has not confirmed whether the objects were spacecraft, camera anomalies or other phenomena.

## What is the FBI UFO File 62-HQ-83894?

One of the most discussed inclusions is the FBI's long-running UFO investigation archive, known as File 62-HQ-83894.

The historical case file covers reports of "flying discs" and unidentified objects between 1947 and 1968. It contains 18 separate reports, including eyewitness testimony, technical analysis, photographs and media coverage from that era.

Among the stranger entries is a 1967 report from a man in Chesapeake who claimed he encountered a transparent craft occupied by "undersized creatures" wearing "regular trouser pants and T-Shirts."

The FBI memo stated the witness "spoke coherently" but appeared to be under "emotional strain."

## Does the Pentagon Confirm Alien Life Exists?

Despite the unusual material, the Pentagon has repeated that no official investigation has confirmed extraterrestrial life.

Officials continue to describe the cases as unexplained aerial events rather than confirmed alien encounters. However, the scale of the new release has intensified public curiosity and renewed debate over what governments may know about unexplained objects seen in Earth's skies and space.
