# Japan: Lawmakers Push for UAP Investigation Body

Japan could be set to take a new step forward in organizing its response to unidentified aerospace phenomena.

On March 24, 2026, the Japanese parliamentary group dedicated to UAPs announced its intention to propose the creation of a government body to oversee efforts relating to this issue. The non-partisan Parliamentary Group for the Study of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena stated it will "formally propose the establishment of a specialized government body to oversee UAP intelligence."

This proposal is scheduled for consideration at a meeting in Tokyo on March 30, 2026. The proposed body would fall under the Deputy Chief Cabinet Secretary for Crisis Management, suggesting direct integration into national security mechanisms.

Former MP Yoshiharu Asakawa has been instrumental in this effort. He previously submitted a proposal to the Defense Minister in May 2025 to establish a specialized department but received no positive response. This time, the group proposes placing the organization within the Cabinet Secretariat, viewing "the government as a whole should address the UAP issue, not just from a national security standpoint."

The Japanese Defense Ministry shifted its position in 2024, emphasizing that unidentified objects should be treated as security issues. Defense Minister Minoru Kihara stressed the importance of analyzing and responding to potential unidentified incursions into Japanese airspace.

The proposal connects to recent U.S. declassification efforts under President Trump. Japan's focus remains on detection, identification, and analysis, with hypotheses centered on human adversary technologies rather than alternative explanations.

A significant factor supporting this proposal involves the ongoing investigation into the "Genkai Nuclear Power Plant UAP Incident." The parliamentary group identified "irreconcilable discrepancies between Kyushu Electric Power Company's records and the Saga Prefectural Police's official explanation."

If adopted, this proposal would represent further institutionalization of UAP handling in Japan's public policy framework.
