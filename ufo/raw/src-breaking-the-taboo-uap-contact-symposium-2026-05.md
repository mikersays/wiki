## Speaker Bios & Abstracts

### Karin Austin

"Experiencer to Researcher: Examining the Evidence for Contact" Austin, a former participant in John Mack's research and later his personal assistant, brings both first-person experience of the contact phenomenon and decades of engagement with its study. She addresses the persistent marginalization of experiencers — even within the UAP community — and, building on Michael Bohlander's case for legal-standard witness credibility, argues for rigorous case-by-case assessment of contact testimony. She then presents the Center for the Impossible's metadata analysis of experiencer reports across multiple archival collections — guided by the phenomenological framework provided by Kimberly Engels — a first-of-its-kind effort to bridge experiencer testimony and academic research, drawing on thousands of convergent accounts and the consistent worldview transformations they describe.

Karin Austin is Managing Director of the John Mack Institute and Center for the Impossible at Rice University. A former participant in Dr. Mack's "alien abduction" research, she served as his personal assistant in the final years of his life. Following his death in 2004, she helped the Mack family preserve his archives for future public and researcher access. In 2022, she facilitated a transfer of those archives to Harvard University and the Center where she, alongside Amanda Focke — Head of Special Collections in the Woodson Research Center at Rice — is currently leading a multidisciplinary team of academic researchers in a groundbreaking metadata analysis of subject-related materials across multiple archival collections. Karin is a public educator and advocate for the necessary inclusion of experiencer knowledge in institutional efforts to accept, understand, and disclose the existence of advanced non-human intelligence who are apparently monitoring human activity on Earth.

### Michael Bohlander

"'Not without my consent!' – The Human Ethics View on the use of Force by Alien Intelligence in Encounters with Humans" A standard part of the narratives about abductions or other encounters of humans with alien intelligence is the use of force in the wider sense in overcoming human resistance. This paper argues that disregarding human autonomy makes these interactions into an exercise of violence on the part of the alien intelligence which can rise in severity to the level of what these days are called core crimes under international criminal law.

Michael Bohlander holds the Chair in Global Law and SETI Policy at Durham University. He has been the International Co-Investigating Judge in the Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia since 2015, of which nearly four years full-time in Phnom Penh. From 2017 until 2022, he was also an international judge of the Kosovo Specialist Chambers. He joined Durham University in 2004 as a full professor, having previously been a life-tenured member of the German judiciary since 1991, where he heard civil and criminal cases at trial and appellate level. He has published widely on German law, English and Welsh criminal law, comparative and international criminal law, and Islamic law. As of 2020, his main research focus has been on the consequences of contact with extraterrestrial intelligence for human law and society in the wider sense.

### Simon Bresler

"After The Phenomenon: Mental Health and Meaning Making" What happens after an encounter with the unknown? This talk explores the psychological terrain that follows anomalous experiences with UAP or NHI. We will examine key features of the unknown and then differentiate psychopathology from transformative anomalous encounters. Finally we will address important ways to support Experiencers in their healing journey, outlining the stages of integration, and highlighting how ontological shock can evolve into existential growth, expanded identity, and renewed participation in life.

Simon Bresler is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker, Certified Group Psychotherapist, and Clinical Director of the John Mack Institute. With over a decade of experience working in Hospitals, Community Mental Health Clinics, and in Private Practice, Simon has been interested in trauma and the mechanisms that foster resilience. In addition to leading several psychodynamic therapy groups in his private practice, he is also the Clinical Director of the John Mack Institute, a not-for-profit organization focused on helping those who have faced unconventional, isolating, and anomalous experiences. His regard for the individuality of every person's journey, the healing power of groups, and his openness to various worldviews, has made him a compassionate guide for those navigating unusual emotional landscapes.

### Kimberly S. Engels

"The Limitations of Anthropocentric Ethics: Navigating Morality amidst Epistemic Gaps" What happens when an ethical encounter unfolds across a profound epistemic divide? This talk examines the challenges of evaluating moral responsibility in interactions where one party possesses forms of knowledge unavailable to the other. Drawing on experiencer testimony suggesting that the UAP intelligence may be situated differently in time and possess insight into the nature and purpose of contact that humans lack, I argue that anthropocentric ethical frameworks are insufficient to capture the moral complexity of such encounters. When the terms of the interaction itself exceed human conceptual grasp, conventional appeals to consent, autonomy, and harm require re-examination.

Kimberly S. Engels is Associate Professor of Philosophy at Molloy University and Research Director of the John Mack Institute. Her philosophical research centers on existentialism, ethics, and phenomenology as tools for examining our lived experience. Her UAP research focuses on the lived conscious experience of people who report contact with UAP, and how such accounts engender new frameworks for our conceptions of self, other, and world. She is the author of Contact and Multidimensional Subjectivity: Towards a Phenomenology of Disclosure (College Publications, 2026).

### Beth H. Glick

"Transformed Into What? Measuring Identity and Action After Exceptional Experience" Across decades of experiencer testimony, a pattern emerges: encounters with non-human intelligence, near-death experiences, and other exceptional experiences frequently catalyze a shift toward interconnectedness, ecological concern, and expanded moral consideration. But the opposite also occurs: ego inflation, grandiosity, rigidification of belief. What moderates these divergent outcomes? The question is complicated by the fact that exceptional experiences can be multiplex, co-occurring and recurring and layered across a life, and isolating any one encounter from the larger pattern may distort the picture. This talk introduces the Exceptional Experience Integration & Impact Measure (EXIM), a multidimensional instrument assessing present-state integration across identity, worldview, relationships, and behavior. EXIM is designed to surface what shapes how these experiences reorganize a life, for individual experiencers and for understanding what these encounters may be producing in us collectively.

Beth H. Glick is an exceptional experiences researcher, writer, and field-builder. Her doctoral work at California Institute for Human Science investigates how contact encounters, near-death experiences, and mystical states reshape people's lives — and why they do so in radically different ways. She has published on trance channeling, noetic experience, and non-physical entity phenomena, and collaborates with IONS, UVA's Division of Perceptual Studies, and the Worldwide Initiative for Super Experiencer Research. With Kimberly S. Engels, she is co-investigator of "Epistemic Repair, Meaning-Making and Experiencer Worldviews: A Phenomenological Study" — a project that seeks to measure how contact experiences with non-human intelligence have affected individuals over decades of life. She co-founded ChangeCraft, spending twenty years in human rights and atrocity prevention, and co-leads The Synapse: Where Women Connect Mind, Machine, and What Comes Next. She holds a master's degree from Harvard.

### Debra Jordan Kauble

"Extraordinary Contact" On June 30, 1983 something unimaginable happened to Debra that turned her whole world upside down. An NHI contact event was witnessed by 3 other individuals and resulted in trace physical evidence that remained visible for several years. It left her injured, terrified and barely able to function. It also opened a floodgate of previously tucked away memories of contact throughout her entire life. After reading a book called Missing Time and reaching out to the author, Budd Hopkins, with his help she began to not just recover, but flourish. In her talk she recounts her transformative journey from fear to enlightenment. Debra's extraordinary experiences with Non-Human Intelligence defied conventional understanding and once defined by terror and confusion became the catalyst for profound personal growth.

Debra Jordan Kauble is the central figure ("Kathie Davis") in Budd Hopkins book Intruders: The Incredible Visitations at Copley Woods. She has written two books on her experiences. Abducted: The Story of the Intruders Continues and Extraordinary Contact: Life Beyond Intruders.

### Tim Murithi

"Pan-African Ufology: Africa's Experience with Non-Human Intelligence" Tim Murithi analyses the historical context of Pan-African Ufology, and engages with conceptual issues, including the extra-terrestrial, inter-time, and other dimensional nature of the UAP/UFO phenomenon. Dr. Murithi will discuss the phenomenon in Africa examined through an engagement with historical experiences of the Egyptians, the Dogon Peoples, of Mali, and their Sirius B Connection. He further assesses the legacy of practitioners including the work of Cynthia Hind on establishing UFO Afrinews, in 1988; the experiences of the Ariel School children in Zimbabwe. Dr. Murithi will conclude by exploring future directions for Pan-African and Global Ufology, including the need to focus on analysis; awareness-raising; re-setting human mindset; as well as identifying the strategies and modalities for engaging our Interstellar Visitors.

Professor Tim Murithi is the South African Representative to the International Coalition for Extraterrestrial Research (ICER), and a member of the Advisory Board of the Society for UAP Studies, with three decades of academic research experience in the field of conflict resolution and peacebuilding in Africa. He is currently a Professor of African Studies with the University of Cape Town, University of the Free State, and Stellenbosch University, in South Africa, author and editor of 13 academic books on peace and transitional justice in Africa. Following the release of the UAP videos in 2017, has authored a number of newspaper articles in South Africa on the UFO/UAP phenomenon, including in the Mail and Guardian, Daily Maverick, The Observer Anomalist and has been interviewed by the South African Broadcasting Corporation (SABC) TV, and talk radio CapeTalk 567 and 702 Talk Radio, and appeared on a number of podcasts including Whitley Strieber's Dreamland and Strange and Mysterious World podcast.

### Andrea Oddo

"Helping Experiencers through Ontological Shock with Community, Peer Support, and Compassionate Care" (With Charles Holt) This talk explores the development of peer-based digital communities to support experiencers and individuals undergoing ontological shock. In the absence of accessible, stigma-free clinical care and amid divergent interpretations within experiencer communities, significant gaps in support persist. Drawing on the John Mack Institute's experience, we propose a "Middle Path" approach that does not impose a singular worldview but instead fosters open, relational meaning-making. By integrating digital platforms, peer networks, and partnerships with clinicians and researchers, this model offers layered support for processing trauma, reframing fear, and navigating profound disruptions to one's sense of reality, identity, and world.

Andrea Oddo is an IT professional with 30 years of experience and a longtime advocate for experiencers. She moderates the largest UFO/UAP and Experiencer related social media communities and founded The Anomalous Coalition, a collaborative network of major ufology communities that serves to advance education, dialogue, and advocacy.

### Matthew Roberts

"The Phenomenon as a Driver of Evolutionary Psychological Development" When the phenomenon first became a part of my life I honestly had no idea what was going on, but in retrospect the patterns became very clear. Often when we think of human evolution we think of physical changes but give little attention to the mental changes that occur. We experience infancy, childhood, adolescence, and adulthood as a psychological evolution throughout our lives. At each of these phases we experience an expanded awareness coupled with extreme shifts in perception. We achieve these milestones through integrating experience and psychological modeling. The phenomenon serves this function by inducing experience and psychologically modeling the phase of development beyond adulthood.

Matthew Roberts is a former Naval cryptologist and intelligence analyst at the Office of Naval Intelligence, experiencer of the Phenomenon and author of Initiated: UAP, Dreams, Depression, Delusions, Shadow People, Psychosis, Sleep Paralysis, and Pandemics. He was present onboard the USS Theodore Roosevelt during the collection of the Gimbal and Go Fast UAP footage, and began to have follow-on experiences with the Phenomenon in the aftermath.
