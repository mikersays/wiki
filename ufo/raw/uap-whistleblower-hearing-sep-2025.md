# Military Whistleblowers Share New Evidence of Alleged UAP at Transparency Hearing
**Source:** https://defensescoop.com/2025/09/09/military-whistleblowers-share-new-evidence-alleged-uap-ufo-hearing/
**Author:** Brandi Vincent
**Date:** September 9, 2025

## Overview

On September 9, 2025, the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee's Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets held its first UAP-focused hearing. The hearing, titled "Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection," featured testimony from current and former U.S. military officials who shared firsthand accounts of encounters with unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP). The hearing was chaired by Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-FL), who emphasized the need for "stronger protections for everyone as a whole" and argued that UAP whistleblowers require "different" safeguards from standard whistleblower frameworks due to the sensitive classified nature of the programs involved.

## Key Witnesses and Their Testimony

### Jeffrey Nuccetelli (Air Force Veteran, 16 Years of Service)

Nuccetelli testified about multiple incidents that occurred between 2003 and 2005 near Vandenberg Air Force Base in California. He described encounters with massive objects he characterized as "like flying buildings." According to his testimony, these objects pulsed with light and moved in irregular patterns that did not correspond to any known aircraft or drone behavior. Nuccetelli stated that multiple personnel witnessed and documented these incidents but that they received "no guidance down on how to handle these events." His account underscores a recurring theme in UAP testimony: that military personnel who encounter anomalous phenomena lack clear reporting chains or protocols, leading to inconsistent documentation and institutional silence.

### Dylan Borland (Air Force Veteran)

Borland testified publicly for the first time about a 2012 UAP incident at Langley Air Force Base in Virginia. While specific details of the encounter were not fully elaborated in open session, Borland's testimony focused heavily on the personal and professional consequences he faced after reporting the incident. He alleged sustained retaliation, stating: "my professional career was deliberately obstructed." He described workplace harassment and concerted efforts to undermine his classified work eligibility, effectively punishing him for coming forward. His testimony served as a case study for why whistleblower protections specific to UAP reporting are urgently needed.

### Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins (U.S. Navy, 23 Years of Service)

Wiggins made history as the first active-duty Navy official to testify publicly about a UAP incident before Congress. He reported a 2023 incident aboard the USS Jackson in which "a self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped [object] emerged from the ocean." According to Wiggins, the object linked up with three similar objects before all four disappeared with "near-instantaneous acceleration." He stated that the observations were "not consistent with conventional aircraft or drones." The objects were detected on multiple sensor systems including radar, and no sonic boom was recorded despite the extreme speed of their departure. Wiggins's testimony is significant both for its recency (2023) and for the fact that it came from an active-duty service member willing to testify on the record.

## Video Evidence Presented

During the hearing, Rep. Eric Burlison (R-MO) unveiled previously unseen video footage showing a U.S. military MQ-9 Reaper drone firing a Hellfire missile at a high-speed orb off the coast of Yemen in October 2024. Burlison noted that the object appeared to be struck by the missile but was not destroyed: "It kept going, and it looked like the debris was taken with it." This footage represents one of the most significant pieces of visual evidence presented in a congressional hearing context, as it shows direct U.S. military engagement with an unidentified object and the apparent failure of a kinetic weapon to neutralize it.

## Whistleblower Protection Legislation

A major legislative goal highlighted during the hearing was the proposed UAP Whistleblower Protection Act (H.R. 5060), introduced by Reps. Tim Burchett and Anna Paulina Luna. The bill would codify distinct protections for individuals who disclose information about government-funded UAP research programs and materials to Congress. Joe Spielberger of the Project on Government Oversight testified that whistleblower protections are essential because "misdeeds that they expose can often have a direct impact on public safety, civil liberties and the security of our country."

## Institutional Context

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), established in 2022 under the Department of Defense, achieved full operational capacity in 2024 and manages dozens of open investigations. However, government officials have maintained secrecy regarding specific projects, caseloads, and findings, contributing to frustration among both lawmakers and the public. The hearing reflected bipartisan congressional frustration with the pace and depth of UAP disclosure from executive branch agencies.
