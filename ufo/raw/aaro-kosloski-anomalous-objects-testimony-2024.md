# Pentagon's UAP Office Director Confirms "Very Anomalous Objects" Under Investigation

**Author:** Micah Hanks
**Date:** November 19, 2024
**Publication:** The Debrief
**URL:** https://thedebrief.org/we-do-have-some-very-anomalous-objects-new-director-of-pentagons-uap-investigations-tells-lawmakers/

## Key Testimony

Dr. Jon Kosloski, the newly appointed Director of the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), testified before the Senate Armed Services Subcommittee that the U.S. is investigating phenomena he described as "some very anomalous objects." He emphasized that his office does not dismiss all reports as conventional explanations.

## Resolved Cases

Kosloski presented several cases his office has explained:

- **Puerto Rico Object (2013):** A video from a Customs aircraft assessed as "likely a pair of balloons or sky lanterns" floating at seven knots.
- **"Go Fast" Navy Video (2017):** Kosloski attributed the apparent high speed to parallax effect, stating "the object is not actually close to the water, but is rather closer to 13,000 feet."
- **Mount Etna Drone Footage (2018):** Through 3D modeling, the office determined the object was 170 meters away from the volcano's plume, not passing through it.

## Unresolved Cases

Kosloski detailed several anomalous incidents still under investigation:

- A law enforcement officer witnessed an orange orb hovering above ground with a "blacker than black" object nearby that ascended "10 to 100 times faster than any drone" without sound.
- A stationary cylinder comparable to a commercial airplane hovered over a southwestern facility before disappearing inexplicably.
- A small object captured on film passed between two aircraft at high speed.

## Approach Differences

Unlike his predecessor Sean Kirkpatrick, Kosloski emphasized outreach efforts. He stated the office has been "reaching out to the broader community, encouraging folks who had talked to AARO personnel in the past, and maybe felt uncomfortable to come back to us." This suggests an effort to rebuild trust with potential witnesses.

## Future Initiatives

Kosloski announced plans to launch a public reporting mechanism in 2025, allowing civilians to submit UAP sighting reports with attachments through the AARO website. He also highlighted the need for persistent monitoring at high-security facilities and requested continued congressional support.
