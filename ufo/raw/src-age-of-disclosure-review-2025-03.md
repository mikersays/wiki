# 'The Age of Disclosure' Review: A Documentary Claims to Offer Proof that Alien Spaceships Are Visiting Us. But Does It Really?

"The Age of Disclosure," which premiered at SXSW, is a documentary that purports to offer incontrovertible evidence that spaceships from other worlds are visiting us. The film features 34 senior members of the U.S. government, military, and intelligence communities, all claiming to have "direct knowledge" of UAPs (Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena).

The evidence presented consists primarily of blurry black-and-white U.S. government video footage showing tiny objects zipping over water surfaces. This is the same aerial phenomena footage witnessed by Navy pilots that was declassified in 2021. The film's testimonials describe otherworldly spacecraft capabilities, including speeds far beyond current human technology and the ability to stop instantly mid-flight.

Witnesses include prominent figures such as Senator Marco Rubio, former Director of National Intelligence Gen. Jim Clapper, South Dakota Senator Mike Rounds, and former Department of Defense officials Chris Mellon and Jay Stratton. They discuss alleged extraterrestrial crash landings and the U.S. government's strategy to reverse-engineer captured alien technology before enemies like China or Russia obtain it.

However, the reviewer expresses significant skepticism about the documentary's claims. Despite acknowledging the initial appeal of believing in alien visitation, the critic points out a fundamental logical flaw: in an age where cameras are ubiquitous—on phones, surveillance systems, and military compounds—there is no clear photographic evidence of actual alien spacecraft. The blurry government footage remains inconclusive.

The film frames these sightings through bureaucratic and scientific language, using terms like "UAPs" rather than the outdated "UFOs." It presents the testimonies in a somber, official tone rather than with wonder, emphasizing national security implications.

The reviewer notes historical patterns in how each era responds to extraterrestrial "evidence" tailored to its time. The 1938 "War of the Worlds" broadcast caused mass panic, the 1980s saw alien abduction narratives proliferate, and the 1990s brought conspiracy-focused entertainment like "The X-Files."
