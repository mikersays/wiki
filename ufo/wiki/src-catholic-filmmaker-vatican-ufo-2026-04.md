## Summary

OSV News (Catholic News Service) reports on Catholic filmmaker [[sam-sorich]]'s in-progress documentary responding to a claim aired in [[the-age-of-disclosure]] that the [[vatican]] holds evidence of [[non-human-intelligence]]. The allegation, originating in [[david-grusch]]'s 2023 congressional testimony, holds that [[pope-pius-xii]] learned of a 1933 UFO crash near Milan and later backchanneled information to the U.S. government. Sorich's team filmed at the [[bunker-soratte]] (a fascist-era complex with documented Vatican ties) in San Oreste, Italy, where the alleged craft's technology was reportedly studied. The [[vatican-observatory]] declined interviews; former director Br. [[guy-consolmagno]] said the observatory's standing policy is to avoid the UFO topic to avoid association with charlatans — a notable institutional artifact of [[uap-stigma]]. The piece also clarifies that an Italian-press "Holy See opens to UFOs" headline traced to a parish "Cultural Wednesdays" series at St. Anne's, not an official Holy See engagement. The film, expected late 2026, exemplifies [[theological-uap-framing]] as a Catholic response to mainstreaming UAP disclosure.

## Key Claims

- [[the-age-of-disclosure]] features a former CIA official alleging the [[vatican]] holds evidence of non-human intelligence (not angels).
- Per [[david-grusch]]'s 2023 testimony, [[pope-pius-xii]] allegedly knew of a 1933 UFO crash near Milan and backchanneled information to the U.S. government, which took possession after WWII.
- [[sam-sorich]]'s production company [[glass-darkly-films]] is producing a Catholic-perspective UFO documentary for late 2026 release.
- Filmmakers shot at the [[bunker-soratte]] in San Oreste, Italy; museum founder/mayor Gregory Paolucci confirmed a "real" Vatican-bunker connection but cannot link it to the alleged UFO.
- Paolucci received a WWII-era **Swiss Guard jacket** from a member of Italian intelligence services when he established the museum.
- The [[vatican-observatory]] declined all interviews; former director [[guy-consolmagno]] said the observatory deliberately avoids the UFO topic in media due to [[uap-stigma]].
- Pontifical-university professors told Sorich UFOs are "not serious" — comparable to "asking a zoologist about unicorns and dragons."
- Italian-press headlines about a "Holy See opens to UFOs" trace to a parish "Cultural Wednesdays" series at the Church of St. Anne organized by Pier Vincenzo Giudici (former deputy director of Vatican Radio) — not an official Vatican initiative.
- Podcaster-researcher [[jesse-michels]] appears on camera at the bunker.
- Investor [[brantly-millegan]] (ChurchPOP founder) frames the project as the Magisterium engaging contemporary culture.
- Former military chaplain [[matthew-gray]] says highly credentialed military contacts told him the U.S. government has recovered crashed UFOs.
