## Summary

[[DefenseScoop]] reports that Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]] told the House Defense Appropriations subcommittee on April 16 2026 that the Army is leaning on the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]] — the "Amazon-like" counter-UAS catalog stood up earlier in 2026 — to bolster security at upcoming stateside high-profile events including the **FIFA World Cup 2026**, **America 250** celebrations, and the **2028 Los Angeles Summer Olympics**. [[jiatf-401]] disclosed that **$13 million** of C-UAS tech (low-collateral effectors, sensors, radars, EW platforms) has moved through the marketplace since launch, with purchases going to the southern-border task force, CENTCOM (overseeing [[operation-epic-fury]] in Iran), and "individual services with homeland defense missions." Driscoll said state, local, and federal law enforcement can buy from the marketplace and that [[joint-base-myer-henderson-hall]] has hosted 350 local police departments to sync them into JIATF-401 efforts. The piece pairs the procurement story with [[tom-karako]]'s "Super Bowl bubbles all year round" framing and Driscoll's disclosure of 13,000 [[merops-interceptor|Merops]] interceptors bought at ~$15k/unit to counter [[shahed-drone|Shaheds]]. Positions the marketplace as the stateside-events complement to the overseas-ally angle in [[src-jiatf-401-marketplace-romania-2026-04]].

## Key Claims

- JIATF-401 has sold ~$13M of C-UAS tech via the marketplace since launch; buyers include the southern-border task force, CENTCOM, and homeland-defense services (JIATF-401 press release).
- State, local, and federal law enforcement can purchase from the marketplace; some purchases already made (Driscoll).
- [[joint-base-myer-henderson-hall]] has hosted 350 state/local police departments to "sync them in" to JIATF-401's C-UAS efforts (Driscoll).
- JIATF-401 has committed **$100M** for FIFA World Cup security focused on mobile C-UAS across 11 cities in 9 states (DefenseScoop).
- The marketplace catalog contains ~12 C-UAS systems and >1,600 items including repair parts (JIATF-401).
- Marketplace users "agree to use the same sight picture" for cross-jurisdiction drone monitoring (Driscoll); DefenseScoop notes it's unclear how this is technically implemented.
- America 250 (summer 2026) and the 2028 LA Summer Olympics are flagged as elevated stateside drone-threat events (DefenseScoop / Karako).
- [[operation-spider-web]] (Ukrainian truck-launched drone attack on Russian strategic aircraft) is cited as the doctrinal wake-up call for stateside fixed-target protection.
- Army bought 13,000 [[merops-interceptor|Merops interceptors]] at ~$15k/unit in the opening days of the Iran war; cost-curve advantage vs Shahed (~$45k+/unit) (Driscoll).
- Karako: "Super Bowl-style bubbles" of ubiquitous sensing + mobile air defense are now required year-round across many sites.

## Notable Quotes

> "State and local and federal law enforcement officers across the country can purchase from this site. We've already had purchases." — Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]]

> "It used to be that we just did the Super Bowl once a year with that kind of cap. But we need lots of Super Bowl-style bubbles over lots of things all year round." — [[tom-karako]], CSIS
