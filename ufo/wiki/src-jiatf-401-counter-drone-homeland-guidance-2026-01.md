## Summary

Primary-source [[department-of-war]] press release announcing updated guidance for [[counter-uas|counter-unmanned aerial systems]] operations in the homeland, signed by [[pete-hegseth|Secretary of War]] on December 8, 2025. The guidance, framed and released by [[jiatf-401]], streamlines and consolidates existing policies under the authority of [[10-usc-130i|10 U.S. Code § 130i]] and directs [[installation-commanders]] to issue installation-specific operating procedures within 60 days. Brig. Gen. [[matt-ross]] is the lone named official quoted, framing drones as "a defining threat for our time." The release introduces three institutional supports — [[joint-c-suas-university]] (JCU) training, transparent knowledge sharing, and a marketplace of validated materiel (precursor framing for the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]]).

## Key Claims

- Updated C-UAS guidance was signed by the Secretary of War on December 8, 2025 (DoW).
- Authority for detecting and mitigating UAS at military installations flows from 10 U.S. Code § 130i (DoW).
- Installation commanders must issue installation-specific operating procedures within 60 days (DoW directive).
- JIATF-401 provides JCU training, knowledge sharing, and a "marketplace of tested and validated materiel capabilities" (DoW).
- Proliferation of inexpensive, capable, weaponizable UAS by "peer competitors and non-state actors" presents a "direct and growing threat" to installations, personnel, and missions at home and abroad (DoW).
- Commanders are directed to assess vulnerabilities, conduct training drills, and develop a defensive posture (DoW).

## Notable Quotes

> "Drones are a defining threat for our time. Technology is evolving fast and our policies and c-UAS strategy here at home must adapt to meet this reality." — Brig. Gen. [[matt-ross]], Director of JIATF-401

> "Countering drones does not start and stop at the fence line. With this new guidance installation commanders are empowered to address threats as they develop." — Brig. Gen. Matt Ross

> "Hardware alone is not sufficient. We need to train service members and work in concert with interagency partners." — Brig. Gen. Matt Ross
