## Summary

Twelve years after [[malaysia-airlines-flight-mh370]] vanished on March 8, 2014 with 239 people aboard, a renewed deep-sea search by [[ocean-infinity]] in the southern Indian Ocean has failed to locate the wreckage. The [[air-accident-investigation-bureau-malaysia]] reported that two search phases (March 2025 and Dec 2025–Jan 2026) covered roughly 7,571 km² of a 15,000 km² target area under a "no-find, no-fee" contract worth $70 million. [[voice370]], representing victims' families, urged Malaysia to extend the contract and consider engaging other deep-sea exploration companies. Ocean Infinity's vessel has been redeployed for other work and is unlikely to return before winter weather closes the search window.

## Key Claims

- [[ocean-infinity]] surveyed ~7,571 km² of seabed across 28 days in two phases without finding confirmed wreckage (Air Accident Investigation Bureau statement)
- The search was conducted under a "no-find, no-fee" contract; Ocean Infinity would receive $70 million only if wreckage is found (Malaysian government terms)
- Ocean Infinity's vessel has been redeployed and is unlikely to return soon due to approaching winter and deteriorating sea conditions ([[voice370]])
- [[voice370]] urged Malaysia to grant contract extensions and open "no-find, no-fee" opportunities to other deep-sea companies
- A prior 2018 private search by [[ocean-infinity]] also found nothing
