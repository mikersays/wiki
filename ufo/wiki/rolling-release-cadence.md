**Rolling-release cadence** describes the [[department-of-war]]'s announced "tranches every few weeks" disclosure pattern for [[pursue]]. Confirmed first by anonymous DoW officials in the days after Release 01 ([[src-defensescoop-pursue-not-disclosure-2026-05]]) and operationalized 14 days later when **Release 02 dropped on May 22, 2026** ([[src-pentagon-second-batch-ufo-release-2026-05]]).

DoW frames the cadence as a workflow constraint: "tens of millions of records, some existing only on paper" must be reviewed across "dozens of federal agencies." The pattern lets DoW publish unresolved cases without committing to definitive assessments — preserving the government's "non-determination" posture while continuing a steady drip.

## Release history

- **Release 01** — May 8, 2026 ([[src-pursue-portal-launch-2026-05]]).
- **Release 02** — May 22, 2026 (Sandia 1948–1950, Sary Shagan 1973, helicopter glowing-orbs Form 302, Lake Huron 2023 shootdown footage, Apollo 12 medical-debrief audio, 2022 submarine-vicinity transmedium spheres). Multi-sourced: [[src-pentagon-second-batch-ufo-release-2026-05]] (Washington Times) + [[src-newsweek-pursue-release-02-2026-05]] (Newsweek).
- **Release 03+** — Anticipated cadence: every ~2 weeks; up to 46 Luna-demanded videos previewed by [[micah-hanks]] ([[src-debrief-pursue-next-batch-preview-2026-05]]); per [[avi-loeb]] (May 11, [[src-loeb-two-ufo-interpretations-2026-05]]) "hopefully will be released in the next batch... within the next month."
