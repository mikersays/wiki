**WASP-94Ab** is a [[hot-jupiter]] gas giant located ~690 light-years from Earth in the constellation Microscopium. It became the **first exoplanet on which a repeating daily weather cycle was confirmed**, reported by [[sagnick-mukherjee]] and [[david-sing]] in *Science* (May 21, 2026) using [[jwst]]'s NIRISS instrument and limb-resolved [[transmission-spectroscopy]] ([[src-jwst-wasp-94ab-weather-cycle-2026-05]]).

## Properties

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Distance | ~690 light-years |
| Radius | 1.72 R_Jup |
| Mass | ~0.45 M_Jup |
| Orbital period | 3.95 days |
| Orbital distance | ~5.1 million miles (closer than Mercury to the Sun) |
| Surface temperature | >2,200 °F (1,200 °C) |
| Rotation | Tidally locked (permanent day/night sides) |

## Daily weather cycle

- The permanent **morning (leading) limb** is blanketed in thick **magnesium-silicate clouds** (vaporized rock), detected at 9σ.
- The **evening (trailing) limb** is clear, showing water-vapor absorption at 10σ.
- The evening limb runs ~450 K hotter than the morning limb.
- Proposed mechanism: clouds condense on the cool nightside, ride equatorial super-rotation winds to the morning terminator, then sink or evaporate as they cross into the dayside.

## Why it matters

Separating the two limbs corrected a decade of cloud-contaminated, full-disc data. WASP-94Ab's inferred oxygen and carbon abundance dropped from "hundreds of times" Jupiter's to only ~5× Jupiter's — a value consistent with standard gas-giant formation. The blended-limb vs limb-resolved estimates differ at >4σ. The same cloud-cycle pattern was later found on **WASP-39b** and **WASP-17b**, suggesting pre-JWST hot-Jupiter composition estimates may be systematically biased. See [[exoplanet-atmosphere-models]] and the [[biosignatures]] caveat on limb-asymmetry averaging bias.
