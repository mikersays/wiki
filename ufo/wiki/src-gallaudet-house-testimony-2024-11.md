## Summary

Written testimony from retired Rear Admiral [[tim-gallaudet]] to the [[house-oversight-committee|House Oversight Committee's]] joint hearing "Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena: Exposing the Truth" on November 13, 2024. Gallaudet recounts a January 2015 incident as Commander of [[naval-meteorology-and-oceanography-command]] in which the operations officer of [[us-fleet-forces-command|U.S. Fleet Forces Command]] sent an "URGENT SAFETY OF FLIGHT ISSUE" email — attaching what is now known as the [[go-fast-video|"Go Fast" FLIR video]] — about repeated near-midair collisions during a [[uss-theodore-roosevelt|USS Theodore Roosevelt]] pre-deployment exercise; the email vanished from his and other recipients' accounts the next day and was never raised again. Gallaudet attributes the silence to a compartmented [[crash-retrieval-programs|special access program]] excluding senior officials and frames the episode as evidence of [[uap-stigma|over-classification]] endangering aviators. He calls for congressional oversight of [[aaro]] (which he accuses of running an "hours-long influence operation" on him), passage of the [[uap-disclosure-act|UAP Disclosure Act]] [[uap-records-review-board|UAP Records Review Board]] provisions stripped from the FY2024 NDAA, and a future whole-of-government UAP coordinating cabinet department. He also flags an unreported 2017 satellite-imagery UAP case raised to him by [[chris-mellon]] and emphasizes [[unidentified-submerged-objects]] as a parallel concern.

## Key Claims

- **January 2015 "URGENT SAFETY OF FLIGHT" email:** Sent on Navy secure network by the U.S. Fleet Forces Command operations officer to subordinate commanders; warned of "multiple near-midair collisions" during the Roosevelt-era pre-deployment exercise off the U.S. East Coast and threatened to shut the exercise down. Attached the now-declassified "Go Fast" FLIR video.
- **Email disappearance:** The email vanished from Gallaudet's and other recipients' accounts the following day; the Fleet Forces commander and ops officer never discussed the incident again, even in weekly safety meetings.
- **Inferred SAP:** Gallaudet concluded UAP information was held inside an intelligence-agency-managed compartmented program from which even flag officers were excluded.
- **AARO disinformation allegation:** During a 2024 meeting with the then-acting AARO director (per Gallaudet's later 2026 clarification, [[tim-phillips]]) and senior staff, Gallaudet says he was the target of an "hours-long influence operation" defending AARO's Historical Records Report Vol. 1, attacking the [[uss-nimitz-uap-incident|USS Nimitz "tic tac"]] case, and disparaging UAP whistleblowers.
- **No NDA:** Gallaudet states he never signed a UAP-related non-disclosure agreement.
- **Constitutional argument:** Failure to brief the Gang of Eight or the Senate Intelligence Committee on UAP SAPs is "not only illegal" but creates a separation-of-powers crisis.
- **2017 satellite case:** Chris Mellon (personal communication, Nov 11, 2024) flagged a 2017 satellite-imagery UAP case never shared with Congress; Gallaudet offered to provide dates in closed session.
- **Recommendations:** (1) AARO/ODNI/NSA briefings and oversight; pursue UAP at IC, NASA, DOE, DHS, NSF; leverage UAP to fix [[drone-incursions]] vulnerabilities. (2) Pass the UAP Disclosure Act UAP Records Review Board provisions stripped from the FY2024 NDAA conference. (3) After enactment, designate a cabinet department lead for whole-of-government UAP coordination — endorsed by the [[sol-foundation]].
- **USOs and water space:** UAP exhibit transmedium travel through the air-sea interface; the Pentagon's >$900B FY2025 defense budget inadequately addresses [[unidentified-submerged-objects]].
- **Affiliations:** [[galileo-project]] research affiliate; [[uap-disclosure-fund]] supporter; Sol Foundation senior strategic advisor; supports [[ryan-graves|Americans for Safe Aerospace]].
