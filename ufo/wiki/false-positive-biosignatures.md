**False-positive biosignatures** are abiotic pathways that generate atmospheric gases normally associated with life, producing signals that can be mistaken for [[biosignatures]]. Characterizing these pathways is essential for interpreting any [[jwst]] or future-telescope atmospheric detection.

## Key abiotic O₂/O₃ pathways

- **CO₂ photolysis** on rocky M-dwarf planets generates detectable abiotic O₂ and O₃ — particularly problematic because M-dwarfs are preferred targets for transmission spectroscopy
- **Hydrogen escape** leaves behind oxygen in some young atmospheres

## Refined 2026 modeling

A photochemical study in a Mars-like atmosphere found that **water-vapor-driven HOx chemistry** recycles CO and O back to CO₂, suppressing O₂/O₃ buildup. The revised maximum abiotic O₂ is ~2.7% at H = 0.0065 ppm — about **10× lower** than earlier estimates ([[src-oxygen-false-positive-biosignatures-2026-03]]).

## Implication

Detection of O₂ on an M-dwarf rocky planet cannot be interpreted as life without first ruling out CO₂-photolysis + HOx chemistry as the source. This is directly relevant to any future JWST or HWO biosignature claim, including the ongoing debate over [[src-jwst-k2-18b-biosignature-2025|K2-18b's DMS detection]]. [[sara-seager]] et al. (2025) treat false positives as a core reason single-gas detections on their own cannot confirm life — "parallel interpretations" will require next-gen facilities to resolve ([[src-jwst-biosignature-prospects-2025]]).

## Surface / mineral false positives

Per [[src-mineral-false-positives-biosignatures-2026-05|Parkinson, Kaltenegger et al. (2026)]], several **abiotic minerals** reproduce the canonical Earth surface biopigment biosignature — the [[photosynthesis-red-edge]] (PRE, ~700 nm reflectance step):

- **Sulfide minerals** (multiple species)
- **Tectosilicate minerals** (multiple species)
- **Potassium ferrocyanide** — prebiotically relevant cyanide salt

The authors argue that PRE biogenicity on an exoplanet must be evaluated in **atmospheric context** plus an explicit **Bayesian prior** comparing biological vs non-biological surface hypotheses. This expands the false-positive taxonomy from atmospheric gas-phase pathways (O₂/O₃) to **surface mineralogy**, directly affecting [[direct-imaging]] retrievals planned for the [[habitable-worlds-observatory]].

## Ocean-world organic-carbon background

On distant [[ocean-worlds]] (Europa, Enceladus), non-biological organics may dominate biological signal — the inverse of Earth's high-consumption regime. Targeted explicitly by NASA's 2026 [[invow|Investigating Ocean Worlds]] project, which integrates organic-carbon modification across rocky subseafloor / ocean / cryosphere domains ahead of [[europa-clipper]]'s 2030 arrival ([[src-whoi-invow-ocean-worlds-2026-05]]).

## Agnostic-detection countermeasures

- **[[assembly-theory|Assembly theory]]** — molecular construction complexity as a substrate-agnostic indicator. Featured AbSciCon26 session theme ([[src-abscicon26-media-tips-1-2026-05]]).
- **Statistical exoplanet-population diversity** — Yoffe & Klenner *Nature Astronomy* method ([[src-alien-biology-detection-method-2026-05]]).
- **Panspermia + environment-modification correlation** — Smith & Sinapayen ([[src-panspermia-agnostic-biosignature-2026-04]]).

## Solar-system extension: panspermia contamination

Per [[src-venus-panspermia-earth-origin-2026-04]], a [[jhuapl]]/[[sandia-national-laboratories]] team modeled Earth-to-Venus [[panspermia]] cell-transfer rates and found that ~100 viable Earth-derived cells per year may reach [[venus]]'s clouds (~20 billion over 1 Gyr). This adds a **panspermia-contamination failure mode** to the false-positive taxonomy: any positive Venus-cloud life detection would carry a non-trivial Earth-origin prior rather than implying independent abiogenesis. The same logic extends to other tightly-coupled planetary systems such as [[trappist-1]].
