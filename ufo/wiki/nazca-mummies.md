# Nazca "Non-Human" Mummies

The **Nazca mummies** are a set of small humanoid specimens recovered from the Nazca region of Peru and publicly presented by [[jaime-maussan]] as "non-human bodies." Maussan's most prominent presentation was to the **Mexican Chamber of Deputies in September 2023**, an event that drew global attention but was rejected by mainstream forensic specialists.

## Forensic assessment

Multiple independent forensic analyses (referenced in [[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]] and elsewhere) concluded that the specimens are **composites of human and animal bones, glue, synthetic rubber, and plant fibers** rather than non-human remains.

## Relevance to current UAP discourse

The Nazca mummies are the most-cited reference case for [[jaime-maussan]]'s artifact-presentation pattern, used by Colombian astronomers and international skeptics as a precedent when evaluating the [[buga-sphere]]:

- No documented [[chain-of-custody]] for the original Peruvian recovery.
- Spectacular public reveal that outran any peer-reviewed analysis.
- Named "experts" without institutional affiliation.
- Eventual mainstream-scientific rejection.
