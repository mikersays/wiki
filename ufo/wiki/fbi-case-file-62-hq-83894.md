# FBI Case File 62-HQ-83894

**62-HQ-83894** is a Federal Bureau of Investigation case file covering the years **1947–1968**, concerning unidentified objects and so-called "flying discs."

The file is the centerpiece of [[pursue]] **Release 01** (May 8, 2026). The PURSUE manifest CSV reveals **all 56 FBI records in Release 01 belong to this case** — 48 PDFs (sections, serials, and supplementary documents) plus 8 photographic-evidence images. Per the official description, this is the **complete case file** with newly declassified pages and only minor redactions, distinct from the partially-public FBI Vault version which has more redactions and missing pages.

## Notable Contents

- **High-profile incident accounts**, eyewitness testimonies, and public reports concerning UFOs and "flying discs" between June 1947 and July 1968.
- **Photographic evidence from sites like Oak Ridge, Tennessee** — a recurring DOE-site thread within the file.
- **Technical proposals regarding potential propulsion systems**.
- **Convention programs, researcher accounts, and extensive media coverage** from the period.
- **An FBI photo composite paired with the eyewitness sketch** of an "apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object," 130–195 feet in length, that "materialized out of a bright light in the sky… and disappeared instantaneously" — corroborating eyewitness reports from a **September 2023** incident (Sketch dated 2024-04-30). [[mick-west]]'s May 2026 Metabunk thread treats this overlay as a graphic reconstruction — *not* raw evidence — flagging that lab-rendered illustrations should be read as visualizations of witness narrative rather than primary sensor output ([[src-metabunk-pursue-analysis-2026-05]]).
- **1967 Chesapeake "transparent craft" report** — per Sunday Guardian's file-content inventory ([[src-sunday-guardian-162-files-2026-05]]), an FBI memo within 62-HQ-83894 documents a man's encounter with a "transparent craft" occupied by "**undersized creatures**" wearing "regular trouser pants and T-Shirts." The witness "spoke coherently" but appeared to be under "emotional strain."
- The file is contemporary with the [[fbi]]'s broader Cold War-era UFO interest and overlaps the lifespans of Project Sign, Project Grudge, and Project Blue Book.

## Internal-report count vs. digital-record count

Sunday Guardian characterizes the file as containing **18 separate reports** (eyewitness testimony, technical analysis, photographs, period media coverage). The [[pursue-release-01-catalog]] enumerates **56 digital records** under this case (48 PDFs + 8 images). Both can be true: "18 reports" is the file's internal narrative count; "56 records" is the discrete-PDF/image count after digitization. Flag if future ingest contradicts.

- Provides a discrete, citable artifact at the heart of the May 8, 2026 disclosure rollout — useful for tracking what fraction of subsequent releases is genuinely new vs. recycled FOIA holdings.
