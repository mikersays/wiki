**Galactic Center** — central region of the Milky Way, hosting the supermassive black hole Sgr A* and an extreme density of stars.

## Relevance to UAP / SETI

The Galactic Center is a strategic SETI target because:

1. **Stellar density** maximizes the line-of-sight count of potentially habitable systems within a single telescope beam.
2. It would be a natural **beacon site** for a galaxy-spanning civilization seeking to transmit to the widest stellar population.

## Vault use

- [[blipss|BLIPSS]] (Cornell/SETI Institute/Breakthrough Listen) targets the Galactic Center at 4–8 GHz with the [[green-bank-telescope|Green Bank Telescope]] for [[periodic-spectral-signal|periodic spectral signals]] ([[src-seti-galactic-center-search-2025-07]]).
- The [[sardinia-radio-telescope|Sardinia Radio Telescope]] high-frequency survey (Manunza et al. 2025) observed the Galactic Center at 6 GHz and 18 GHz — the first SETI search at 18 GHz — returning a null result ([[src-sardinia-technosignature-search-2026-05]]).
