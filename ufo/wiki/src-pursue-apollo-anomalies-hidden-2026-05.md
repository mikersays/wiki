## Summary

IBTimes UK frames the [[pursue]] Release 01 (May 7-8, 2026) not around what was disclosed but around what is allegedly being **withheld** — characterizing the [[donald-trump]] administration's [[department-of-war]]-led declassification as a politically convenient "shiny object" that conceals deeper Apollo-era anomalies. The piece highlights an [[apollo-12]] 1969 frame of a glowing horizon object labeled "unidentified phenomena," scattered bright spots in other Apollo 12 imagery, and the [[apollo-17]] photograph of three triangular dots with no consensus interpretation. It echoes CBS reporting that **cover-up claims are growing** around the released files, citing Iraq 2022 (vanishing objects) and Syria 2024 (erratic / deformed white-and-orange lights) videos as further unresolved cases. [[pete-hegseth]] and [[tulsi-gabbard]] are quoted endorsing the release; [[nasa]] has not formally commented. The article's distinctive angle versus Fox/CBS/AP coverage is its sustained framing of PURSUE as an act of **redaction and concealment** rather than transparency.

## Key Claims

- Multiple outlets argue [[pursue]] "does not go deep enough" and characterize Release 01 as a politically convenient "shiny object" (attributed to unnamed media consensus by Morilla).
- CBS News has reported that **cover-up claims are growing** around the declassified files (attributed to CBS via IBTimes).
- The [[apollo-12]] 1969 image of a glowing horizon object is labeled "unidentified phenomena" with scant accompanying notes (attributed to PURSUE file labels).
- [[apollo-17]] photo of three triangular dots remains without consensus interpretation (attributed to PURSUE files).
- Iraq 2022 footage shows objects vanishing; Syria 2024 footage shows "deformed white light with orange glowing elements that appear and disappear within seconds" (attributed to released DoD files).
- "No security clearance is required" to view PURSUE materials and "many materials remain unanalysed for specific anomalies" (attributed to DoD).
- [[nasa]] has not issued a formal comment on the declassified Apollo photos; agency spokespeople "previously attributed similar anomalies to known lunar conditions" (attributed to NASA via Morilla).
- IBTimes dates Trump's Truth Social directive to **9 February** — note: the wiki's other PURSUE coverage dates the directive to **19 February 2026**, so IBTimes's date is likely a reporting error.
