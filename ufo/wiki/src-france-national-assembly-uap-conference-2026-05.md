## Summary

France's [[french-national-assembly|National Assembly]] will host its first-ever parliamentary symposium on unidentified aerospace phenomena on **June 29, 2026**, organized by MP [[arnaud-saint-martin]] (La France Insoumise) with co-organizer MP [[pierre-henriet]] (Horizons), vice president of the [[opecst|Parliamentary Office for the Evaluation of Scientific and Technological Choices]]. The event will be held in the largest hall of the Assembly (250-seat capacity) with backing from [[cnes]] and [[geipan]]. The symposium frames UAP "research beyond fantasy" and is positioned as a landmark institutional milestone for European UAP science alongside the [[european-uap-day]] track. The report restates GEIPAN's official scope (collection, analysis, investigation, publication, archiving of UAP reports), pushing back against ET / paranormal-research framing.

## Key Claims

- France's National Assembly will host a UAP conference on June 29, 2026 in its largest hall (250 attendees). (Sentinel News / Saint-Martin)
- MP Arnaud Saint-Martin (LFI) is the lead organizer; MP Pierre Henriet (HOR), OPECST vice president, is co-organizer. (Sentinel News)
- [[cnes]] and [[geipan]] are supporting the event. (Sentinel News)
- [[opecst]] is a joint National Assembly + Senate advisory body of 36 members tasked with informing Parliament on scientific and technological choices. (Sentinel News)
- [[geipan]], established 1977, operates under tri-ministerial oversight: Economy/Finance, Higher Education/Research, and Armed Forces. (Sentinel News)
- GEIPAN classifies sightings by abnormality and reliability levels after investigation. (Sentinel News)
- Saint-Martin describes the symposium as "the first symposium of its kind in the history of Parliament." (Saint-Martin)
- Registration will open soon, capped at 250 seats. (Sentinel News)
