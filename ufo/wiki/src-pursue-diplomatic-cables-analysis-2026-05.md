## Summary

Blog-format overview of [[pursue|PURSUE]] Release 01 (May 8, 2026) published nine days after launch by Basement of the Bizarre (David Albaugh). The piece recaps the 162-file tranche across four categories — NASA archival mission data ([[apollo-11]]/[[apollo-12|12]]/[[apollo-17|17]]), [[fbi-case-file-62-hq-83894|FBI "flying disc" files]] (1947-1968), modern military encounters (2020-2026), and [[department-of-state|State Department]] diplomatic cables (1985-2025). It notes the portal logged 340 million hits in 12 hours. The article reproduces [[pete-hegseth]]'s "unresolved cases" framing and the Pentagon's statement that no redactions were applied to UAP-encounter content itself. A second tranche is projected for June 2026.

## Key Claims

- The PURSUE portal logged **340 million hits within its first 12 hours** (Pentagon officials)
- Release 01 comprises **162 files** (120 PDFs, 28 videos, 14 images) from DoD, FBI, NASA, and State Department; 108 files contain partial redactions solely protecting identities, facility locations, or sensitive military sites
- [[buzz-aldrin]] reported a "sizeable" object alongside a "bright light source" near the Moon during [[apollo-11]] in 1969 (declassified debriefing transcript)
- [[apollo-12]] and [[apollo-17]] materials include December 1972 photographs of three "dots" in a triangular formation with investigative "areas of interest" overlays
- An [[fbi]] dossier of 18 documents spans 1947-1968, includes Oak Ridge, Tennessee photographs and propulsion-system technical proposals
- The "Aegean Sea" Incident (2023): an object executing **multiple 90-degree turns at ~80 mph** just above the ocean surface
- Dozens of recent encounter reports track white, fast-moving, or "bouncy ball" objects over Iraq, Syria, the Persian Gulf, the Strait of Hormuz, the Gulf of Aden, and Greece
- [[department-of-state]] contributed diplomatic cables from **1985 through late 2025** covering UAP incidents in Papua New Guinea, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Georgia, and Mexico
- A second tranche is projected for **June 2026** on a rolling basis

## Notable Quotes

> "No redactions were made to information detailing the actual nature or existence of the UAP encounters." — Pentagon officials
