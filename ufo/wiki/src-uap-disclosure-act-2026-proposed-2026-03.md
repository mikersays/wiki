## Summary

The [[new-paradigm-institute]] publishes the full proposed legislative text of the **[[uap-disclosure-act]]** (UAPDA), the most comprehensive UAP transparency bill introduced in Congress. Championed by [[chuck-schumer]] and [[mike-rounds]], the bill would establish a UAP Records Collection at the [[national-archives]], create a 9-member independent [[uap-records-review-board]] with subpoena power, impose a presumption of immediate disclosure on all federal UAP records, authorize the federal government to exercise **eminent domain** over recovered [[technologies-of-unknown-origin]] and biological evidence of [[non-human-intelligence]] held by private entities, and define key terms including "legacy program," "close observer," and "controlling authority." The bill explicitly cites inadequacy of FOIA and overbroad [[atomic-energy-act]] exemptions as barriers to disclosure, and authorizes $20M for implementation.

## Key Claims

- All federal UAP records carry a **presumption of immediate disclosure**; must be publicly accessible within 30 days of receipt by the Archivist, digitized within 180 days (Sec. L104)
- Disclosure may be postponed only for grave national-security, source-protection, privacy, or confidentiality reasons — and all records must be disclosed within **25 years** unless the President personally certifies continued postponement (Sec. L105–L106)
- The **[[uap-records-review-board]]** comprises 9 impartial citizens (including a national-security official, scientist, economist, historian, and sociologist), appointed by the President with Senate approval; empowered to subpoena, hold hearings, and compel agency transmission (Sec. L107)
- The federal government shall exercise **eminent domain** over all recovered UAP technologies and biological evidence of [[non-human-intelligence]], including materials held by private contractors (Sec. L110)
- **"Legacy program"** is defined as any effort to collect or reverse-engineer UAP technologies predating the Act — the first statutory acknowledgment of such programs (Sec. L103)
- **"[[technologies-of-unknown-origin]]"** covers any materials, vehicles, or equipment lacking known human manufacture (Sec. L103)
- UAP definition includes six observables: instantaneous acceleration, hypersonic velocity without thermal signature, transmedium travel, non-aerodynamic lift, multispectral signature control, and biological/physical effects on observers (Sec. L103)
- FOIA is declared inadequate and Atomic Energy Act exemptions are cited as a barrier to UAP record disclosure (Sec. L102)
- $20,000,000 authorized for FY2025 implementation (Sec. L114)
- Review Board sunsets September 30, 2030 unless extended (Sec. L107)

## Notable Quotes

> "Credible evidence shows UAP records exist that have not been declassified due to Atomic Energy Act exemptions and overbroad interpretations of 'transclassified foreign nuclear information.'" — Sec. L102(a)(4)
