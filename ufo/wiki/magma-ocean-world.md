A **magma ocean world** is a small-planet class proposed by [[harrison-nicholls]], [[raymond-pierrehumbert]] et al. ([[university-of-oxford]]) in 2026 ([[src-l-98-59-d-sulphur-magma-world-2026-05]]). Unlike early Earth — which underwent a transient magma-ocean phase before its crust cooled — magma ocean worlds maintain a **persistent, global silicate magma ocean over billions of years**, sustained by a thick heavy-molecule atmosphere driving a runaway [[greenhouse-effect|greenhouse effect]] that prevents crust formation.

## Prototype

[[l-98-59-d]] — 1.6 R⊕, lower density than expected for terrestrial composition; orbits the [[m-dwarf-stars|M dwarf]] [[l-98-59]] ~35 ly away in Volans. Atmosphere dominated by heavy sulphur-bearing molecules ([[sulphur-atmosphere]]). Likely formed via atmospheric loss from a [[mini-neptune|sub-Neptune]] precursor.

## Significance

- Adds a fourth category to the rocky / gaseous / water-rich planetary taxonomy.
- Surface is uninhabitable, but the class widens the planetary diversity catalogue used to interpret [[biosignatures]] and [[false-positive-biosignatures]].
- Demonstrates that coupled atmosphere–interior modelling can reconstruct deep planetary history from mass, radius, and atmospheric composition alone.
- Sulphur-magma worlds may be a broad population once more next-generation telescope characterization is available ([[jwst]] and successors).
