# Pentagon publishes first batch of declassified UFO files under new program

## Summary

[[stars-and-stripes]] (Phillip Walter Wellman) covers the May 8, 2026 [[pursue]] launch for a US military readership. The piece centers on the **combatant-command provenance** of the released video material — submissions from [[us-indo-pacific-command]], [[centcom]], and [[us-european-command]] are explicitly named as "unresolved" — and on [[apollo-12]] photographs in which "unidentified phenomena are visible." It reproduces [[pete-hegseth]]'s "justified speculation" line, attributes the rolling-release cadence and interagency framing to the [[department-of-war]], and lists [[aaro]], the White House, [[odni]] ([[tulsi-gabbard]]), [[department-of-energy]], [[nasa]] ([[jared-isaacman]]), and [[fbi]] as participants. Tone is neutral and procedural; no new files or technical detail beyond the launch wire. Distinct vs. other PURSUE wires: this is the only one to lead with **Apollo 12** rather than [[apollo-17]] for the lunar imagery, and the only one to **name all three combatant commands** explicitly.

## Key Claims

- PURSUE Release 01 = **162 files** published at `www.war.gov/ufo`; more on a rolling basis (DoW statement).
- Released combatant-command videos are explicitly **[[us-indo-pacific-command]], [[centcom]], and [[us-european-command]]** submissions described as "unresolved" — first launch-wire to enumerate all three commands by name.
- Several **[[apollo-12]]** moon-landing photographs (1969) are included with "unidentified phenomena … visible" — Stripes leads with Apollo 12; other launch-day wires lead with [[apollo-17]].
- Interagency participants: White House, [[odni]], [[department-of-energy]], [[nasa]], [[fbi]], plus [[aaro]] inside [[department-of-war]].
- "Unresolved" defined per the portal as cases where the government "is unable to make a definitive determination" — can occur for lack of sufficient data.
- [[tulsi-gabbard]] frames the release as the **start** of a continuing review.
- [[jared-isaacman]] commits NASA scientific expertise as more files are examined.
