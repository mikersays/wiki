## Summary

[[rafael-luque]] et al. publish in *Astronomy & Astrophysics* the first joint analysis of the full panchromatic (0.6–12 µm) [[jwst]] spectrum of [[k2-18b]], combining NIRISS, NIRSpec, and MIRI observations using three independent data-reduction pipelines and two retrieval codes. The study finds **insufficient statistical evidence** for dimethyl sulfide (DMS) or dimethyl disulfide (DMDS) in K2-18b's atmosphere. **This directly contradicts [[src-jwst-k2-18b-biosignature-2025]]**, in which [[nikku-madhusudhan]]'s team reported a three-sigma DMS/DMDS detection using MIRI alone — the strongest potential [[biosignatures|biosignature]] claim on any exoplanet. The non-biological molecule ethane (C₂H₆) produces statistically indistinguishable fits, introducing a [[false-positive-biosignatures|false-positive]] pathway.

## Key Claims

- **No statistical evidence for DMS/DMDS**: Bayesian model comparison (δ ln Z < 1) shows no preference for DMS/DMDS models when using the JExoRES data reduction
- **Contradicts Madhusudhan et al.**: The three-sigma DMS/DMDS claim does not survive the first panchromatic joint analysis; the detection was an artifact of limited molecular model sets and single-pipeline sensitivity
- **Ethane as alternative**: Non-biological ethane (C₂H₆) fits the data equally well, introducing a false-positive pathway specific to DMS/DMDS
- **Pipeline dependence**: Results from JExoRES, exoTEDRF, SPARTA, and Eureka! pipelines diverge, demonstrating the DMS/DMDS preference lacks robustness across independent reductions
- **MIRI features implausibly large**: Mid-infrared spectral features attributed to DMS/DMDS appear implausibly large relative to shorter-wavelength observations, suggesting noise contamination
- **~25 more MIRI transits needed**: Even under the most favorable DMS/DMDS model, approximately 25–26 additional MIRI transits would be required for a 3σ detection

## Notable Quotes

> "The first joint analysis of the panchromatic (0.6-12 µm) spectrum of K2-18 b finds insufficient evidence for the presence of DMS and/or DMDS in the atmosphere of the planet." — Luque et al.

> "We find that any marginal preferences are the result of limiting the number of molecules considered in the model and oversensitivity to small changes between data reductions." — Luque et al.

## Contradiction Note

This paper is in direct tension with [[src-jwst-k2-18b-biosignature-2025]], which reported DMS and DMDS detection at three-sigma significance. The Luque et al. reanalysis — using broader wavelength coverage, more pipelines, and more complete molecular models — concludes that the earlier claims were premature and not statistically robust. The [[k2-18b]] DMS/DMDS biosignature, previously described as "heavily disputed" ([[src-k2-18b-technosignature-null-2026]]), is now formally rebutted by a peer-reviewed panchromatic analysis. This also weakens the [[hycean-worlds]] biosignature narrative, though the hycean atmospheric structure hypothesis itself is not directly tested.
