# David Vélez ("El Potro")

**David Vélez**, known as **"El Potro,"** is a Cali, Colombia–based UAP enthusiast and shareholder in [[germany-company|Germany Company]], a Cali firm that retails metal detectors marketed for "hidden treasure" (gold, silver, jewelry, coins, *guacas*). He operates the Facebook page **"Cazadores de Ovnis Latinoamérica"** (Latin America UFO Hunters) and is the cousin of [[jose-arias-restrepo|José Arias Restrepo]], the named finder of the [[buga-sphere|Buga Sphere]].

## Role in the Buga Sphere narrative

Per [[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]:

- Received the sphere from Arias and kept it on a display stand at Germany Company in Cali for ~2 months, reportedly handling it in an electronics-grade lead radiation suit.
- Claims he "measured electromagnetic fields" and "detected certain signals" from the object.
- Source of the unattributed **carbon-14 result of 12,560 years** ([[src-buga-sphere-uga-carbon-date-claim-2025-10]], [[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]]).
- Coined the term **"drovni"** (drone-UFO) to describe the sphere.
- Claims ~170 videos of similar sphere sightings across Colombia (Medellín, Tuluá, Yumbo) and ties them to airport closures in Denmark (Sept 2025) and Manchester (Nov 2024).
- Says an **unnamed Bogotá laboratory** contacted him claiming the sphere was part of an experiment they had been running since 2011 and asked for its return; he refused, citing no documentation.
- Handed the sphere to [[jaime-maussan]]'s team; Maussan exported it from Colombia to Mexico without restriction.

He later appears in the [[valdias-sphere]] arc as a co-participant ([[src-buga-sphere-maussan-tv-valdias-report-2026-05]]).

## Caveats

All claims attributed to Vélez are **unverified, single-source**. The 12,560-year date he cites has not been published by any named laboratory; the [[university-of-georgia]] has not confirmed the result; and the Bogotá-2011-experiment claim is described without documentation.

## Naming note

Earlier wiki context and some search results conflated Vélez with "José Luis Velázquez." El País (Cali) ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]) is the primary investigative source and consistently uses **David Vélez "El Potro"**; that is the canonical name on this wiki.
