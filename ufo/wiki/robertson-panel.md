The **Robertson Panel** was a 1953 CIA-convened scientific advisory panel that concluded UFO reports were driven by misidentification of mundane aerial objects but could be exploited for psychological warfare.

## Recommendations

The panel recommended debunking UFO sightings to prevent public hysteria and to ensure that classified aircraft — such as the Lockheed U-2 high-altitude reconnaissance plane — would not attract attention (Durant 1953). According to [[src-uap-reddit-disclosure-conspiracy-2026-05]], this policy "laid the groundwork for decades of government-sponsored disinformation campaigns."

## Distinction from Later Operations

While the Robertson Panel's goal was to **deter public discussion** of UFOs, the 1980s AFOSI [[majestic-12]] disinformation campaign (run in part by [[richard-doty]]) was aimed at **deterring specific researchers** considered threats to national security (Pilkington 2010). Both are cited by Bastos & Duarte (2026) as examples of [[confirmed-conspiracy-theory|confirmed conspiracy theories]].

## Legacy

The panel's legacy extends through [[project-blue-book]] (1952–1969), Project Sign (1947–1949), and Project Grudge (1949–1952). [[jacques-vallee]]'s three-category cover-up framework directly addresses the institutional attitudes that the Robertson Panel institutionalized. The panel's influence on [[uap-stigma]] persists in academic and military settings, as documented by the Yingling et al. faculty surveys cited in [[src-academic-uap-stigma-2026-04]].
