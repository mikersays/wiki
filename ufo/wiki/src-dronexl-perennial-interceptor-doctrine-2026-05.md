## Summary

DroneXL's Haye Kesteloo reads the **$500M three-year IDIQ** that [[jiatf-401]] awarded [[perennial-autonomy]] on May 18 2026 (covered as the award in [[src-perennial-autonomy-jiatf-401-500m-2026-05]]) as the doctrinal consolidation of "Ukraine's cheap-interceptor math" into standing U.S. procurement. The throughline is the **interceptor cost curve**: U.S. forces opened the Iran war ([[operation-epic-fury]]) firing million-dollar missiles at ~$30K [[shahed-drone|Shaheds]], then switched to the ~$15K [[merops-interceptor|Merops]]. The piece traces Eric Schmidt's startup from **White Stork (2023) → Project Eagle (Feb 2024) → Perennial Autonomy (2026)**, reports **4,000+ Merops intercepts** in Ukraine since mid-2024, and situates the IDIQ as the consolidation step after the DOGE drone-buying takeover (Oct 2025), the [[drone-dominance|Drone Dominance]] program (Dec 2025), and the Army's emergency 13,000-unit Merops buy. It flags that even at $15K, Merops runs 6–7× the price of a ~$2,000–2,500 Ukrainian Wild Hornets Sting — the gap being its AI/autonomy layer.

## Key Claims

- The contract "locks into standing American procurement" a doctrine shift from million-dollar missiles to ~$15K interceptors against ~$30K–50K Shaheds (DroneXL / Kesteloo).
- Schmidt's venture chronology: **White Stork** (2023, after 2022 meetings with Ukrainian officials) → **Project Eagle** (Feb 2024) → **Perennial Autonomy** (2026) (DroneXL).
- Perennial recruited former Pentagon innovation chief [[will-roper|Will Roper]] plus Apple and SpaceX engineers (DroneXL).
- Merops (also called the **Surveyor**) is a fixed-wing interceptor flying >280 km/h (175 mph), fast enough to catch jet-powered Shahed variants; pilot-cued then autonomous via onboard computer vision + RF detection with jam-resistant links (DroneXL, citing Defense Express).
- Perennial reports **4,000+ Russian drones intercepted** in Ukraine since mid-2024 (Perennial, per DroneXL).
- Army Sec. [[dan-driscoll|Dan Driscoll]] (April testimony): 13,000 Merops bought at ~$15K each in the war's first days; a Shahed costs $30K–50K (DroneXL citing Driscoll).
- Ukrainian **Wild Hornets' Sting** interceptors cost ~$2,000–2,500 — roughly 6–7× cheaper than Merops; the premium reflects Merops's AI/autonomy layer (DroneXL).
- European co-production: Perennial partners with Munich-based [[twentyfour-industries|Twentyfour Industries]] to build Merops in Germany; **Lithuania bought 48 Merops in April**; Poland and Romania have trained/deployed on NATO's eastern flank (DroneXL).
- The procurement pattern — DOGE seizing drone buying (Oct 2025), [[drone-dominance|Drone Dominance]] (~$1B, Dec 2025), the emergency 13,000-unit buy — culminates in this IDIQ as a "standing, enterprise-wide line of credit" (DroneXL's Take).
