# Jaime Maussan

**Jaime Maussan** is a Mexican journalist and long-time UAP advocate. He is best known internationally for presenting purported "non-human bodies" — the [[nazca-mummies|Nazca mummies]] — to the Mexican Chamber of Deputies in September 2023; the bodies were forensically assessed as composites of human/animal bones, glue, synthetic rubber, and plant fibers.

## 2025–2026 Buga Sphere arc

- **March 2025 — recovery in Colombia.** A metallic sphere is allegedly found near [[buga|Buga, Valle del Cauca]] by [[jose-arias-restrepo|José Arias Restrepo]] and passed to his cousin [[david-velez-el-potro|David Vélez "El Potro"]], who keeps it ~2 months at his Cali firm [[germany-company|Germany Company]] before transferring it to Maussan's team. Maussan exports it to Mexico without restriction — no [[chain-of-custody]] documentation ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]).
- **June 2025 — U.S. press conference** with [[steven-greer]] and Rep. [[eric-burlison]] in the U.S., reviving viral attention ([[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]]).
- **Oct 2025 — Colombian-press skeptical roundup.** El País (Cali) and Infobae publish detailed accounts in which four named astronomers ([[santiago-vargas]], [[marino-guarin]], [[oscar-amaya-montoya]], [[lauren-flor-torres]]) uniformly reject the ET framing and identify optical-fiber components and absence of atmospheric-entry crust.
- **Oct 2025 — 12,560-year UGA claim.** PRISM MarketView promotes a claim that an Accelerator Mass Spectrometry result from the [[university-of-georgia]] dated internal resin to ~12,560 years ([[src-buga-sphere-uga-carbon-date-claim-2025-10]]). No UGA report, personnel, or methodology has been published.
- **Mar 15 2026 — Mexico City National Auditorium reveal**, broadcast to ~8,000 attendees.
- **May 8 2026 — NPC co-presentation** with Greer at the 25th-anniversary [[the-disclosure-project]] event ([[src-greer-disclosure-event-npc-2026-05]]).
- **May 10 2026 — Valdías Sphere addition.** Maussan's *No Humano* TV segment introduces the [[valdias-sphere|Valdías Mountain Range Sphere]], a second metallic sphere from the Antioquia / Medellín region recovered by [[william-zuniga|William Zúñiga]] ([[src-buga-sphere-maussan-tv-valdias-report-2026-05]]). Maussan announces a withhold-until-final-report policy for Valdías, contrasting Buga's incremental claims.

The wiki treats all Buga-Sphere and Valdías-Sphere claims as **unverified, single-source** pending independent laboratory documentation.

## Pattern

- Maussan's artifact presentations consistently follow the same arc: dramatic public unveiling, no documented [[chain-of-custody]], "experts" without institutional affiliation, no peer-reviewed corroboration, eventual mainstream rejection ([[nazca-mummies]], Atacama humanoid, Buga Sphere, Valdías Sphere).
- Co-appears with [[steven-greer]] / [[the-disclosure-project]] as part of an international UAP-advocacy circuit distinct from U.S. government disclosure tracks ([[uap-disclosure]], [[pursue]]).
- Critics — including U.S.-based skeptic [[julia-mossbridge]] and Colombian astrophysicists [[santiago-vargas]] / [[lauren-flor-torres]] — invoke the Nazca-mummies precedent when evaluating new Maussan artifacts.
