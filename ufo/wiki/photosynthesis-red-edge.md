The **photosynthesis red edge** (PRE; also called the **vegetation red edge**, VRE) is a sharp step-like increase in spectral reflectance near ~700 nm produced by chlorophyll-bearing land vegetation on Earth. It is detectable in disk-integrated reflectance spectra of Earth and is the canonical **surface biopigment biosignature** target for future visible-to-NIR [[direct-imaging]] missions, including the [[habitable-worlds-observatory|Habitable Worlds Observatory]].

## Detection history

- Galileo spacecraft flyby of Earth (1990) detected the PRE in disk-integrated Earth spectrum — landmark calibration observation.
- Earthshine spectroscopy has reproduced PRE detection from Earth-as-exoplanet observations.

## Known mineral false positives (2026)

According to [[src-mineral-false-positives-biosignatures-2026-05|Parkinson, Kaltenegger et al. (2026)]], the following abiotic surface materials produce PRE-like reflectance steps in existing mineral spectral databases:

- **Sulfide minerals** (multiple species)
- **Tectosilicate minerals** (multiple species)
- **Potassium ferrocyanide** — a prebiotically relevant cyanide salt

This expands the [[false-positive-biosignatures]] taxonomy from gas-phase abiotic O₂ ([[src-oxygen-false-positive-biosignatures-2026-03]]) to surface mineralogy. The authors argue PRE biogenicity must be evaluated in atmospheric context plus an explicit Bayesian prior comparing biological vs non-biological surface hypotheses.
