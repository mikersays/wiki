# Buga Sphere

The **Buga Sphere** is a basketball-sized metallic object that, per the recoverers' account, fell to the ground in the **Alto Bonito sector of [[buga|Buga]], Valle del Cauca, Colombia in March 2025**. It was promoted publicly through 2025–2026 by Mexican ufologist [[jaime-maussan]] and co-presented with [[steven-greer]] at the May 8 2026 [[the-disclosure-project]] National Press Club event ([[src-greer-disclosure-event-npc-2026-05]]) as a possible non-human artifact. **All physical, biological, and dating claims about the sphere are unverified, single-source, and disputed by named Colombian and international scientists.**

## Provenance (per recoverers)

- **Finder:** [[jose-arias-restrepo|José Arias Restrepo]], a peanut vendor and metal-detector hobbyist from Buga. While metal-detecting for old coins in March 2025, he saw the object flying near him "like a soccer ball with a motor." A companion filmed the encounter on an iPhone 11 for 8 minutes.
- **Intermediary:** Arias's cousin [[david-velez-el-potro|David Vélez "El Potro"]], shareholder in the Cali metal-detector retailer [[germany-company|Germany Company]] and operator of the "Cazadores de Ovnis Latinoamérica" Facebook page. Vélez handled the sphere in a lead suit and kept it on a display stand at his company for ~2 months.
- **Transfer:** [[jaime-maussan]]'s team contacted Vélez and brought the sphere from Colombia to Mexico. El País Cali ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]) notes the export occurred without restriction; no [[chain-of-custody]] documentation has been published.
- **First major public reveal:** March 15 2026 at Mexico City's National Auditorium (per [[src-buga-sphere-maussan-tv-valdias-report-2026-05]]).
- **June 2025 press conference** with [[steven-greer]] and Rep. [[eric-burlison]] in the U.S. (per [[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]]).
- **May 8 2026 NPC reveal** with Greer and Maussan at the 25th-anniversary [[the-disclosure-project]] event.

## Claims attributed to the sphere (all unverified)

- **Mass:** initially estimated 2 kg, allegedly grew to ~10 kg over days.
- **Physical effects on handlers:** vomiting, dizziness, metallic taste; lymphocyte DNA damage in handlers (cited in [[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]]).
- **Surface:** seamless metallic appearance, "hard aluminum" construction, no atmospheric-entry pitting; 31 equatorial black dots; engravings described in pro-Maussan press as "proto-Sanskrit" or Paleo-Hebrew-like.
- **Internal structure:** concentric layers; 31 microscopic resin-anchored channels likened to fiber-optic assemblies; 18 symmetric microspheres allegedly containing ~12,000-year-half-life radioactive isotopes; "self-healing micropores."
- **Other:** alleged gravity-offsetting EM field; 0.1% daily mass gain — all unsubstantiated.
- **Carbon-14 result of ~12,560 years** for an internal resin sample, attributed to the [[university-of-georgia|University of Georgia]] but with no published lab report, named researchers, calibration data, or peer review (see [[src-buga-sphere-uga-carbon-date-claim-2025-10]] for the promotional articulation; [[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]] and [[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]] for the methodological rebuttals).

## Scientific assessment

**Colombian astronomers** ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]], [[src-buga-sphere-infobae-true-origin-2025-10]]) uniformly assess the object as a contemporary terrestrial artifact:

- [[santiago-vargas]] (Universidad Nacional, Observatorio Astronómico): compounds are "very terrestrial"; identifies **optical fibers** in the structure; likens to the Arizona desert monolith publicity stunt.
- [[marino-guarin]] (Escuela de Astronomía de Cali): "charlatanism"; "if it had substance, it would circulate in scientific journals, not on TikTok"; no UGA or Mexican-university report exists.
- [[oscar-amaya-montoya]] (Fundación Casa de la Ciencia de Buga): "probable that this is a recent technological artifact manufactured on Earth"; AI-era video fabrication is plausible.
- [[lauren-flor-torres]] (Universidad de Antioquia astrophysicist): the sphere's pristine surface is inconsistent with atmospheric entry; real meteorites carry heat-fractured crusts.

**International skeptics:**

- [[julia-mossbridge]] (University of San Diego, UAP researcher): the object "seems kind of like an art project... only humans can do"; urges submission to [[galileo-project]]-grade analysis.
- **Astrophyzix** ([[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]]): radiocarbon ages organic samples only, not inorganic containers; conflating resin age with object age is "a fundamental methodological error"; no UGA documentation exists.

**Competing terrestrial provenance:** an unnamed Bogotá laboratory reportedly told Vélez the sphere was part of an experiment they had been running since 2011 and asked for its return; Vélez refused.

## Pattern

The Buga Sphere fits the [[jaime-maussan]] artifact arc — viral reveal, no documented chain of custody, "experts" without institutional affiliation, no peer review. The Colombian press ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]) explicitly compares it to Maussan's previously debunked [[nazca-mummies|Nazca "non-human" mummies]] (assessed in 2023 as composites of human/animal bone, glue, synthetic rubber, and plant fibers). See [[steven-greer]] for the parallel [[the-disclosure-project]] history with the Atacama humanoid.

A **second Colombian sphere** — the [[valdias-sphere|Valdías Mountain Range Sphere]] — was added to the Maussan narrative in May 2026 ([[src-buga-sphere-maussan-tv-valdias-report-2026-05]]).
