## Summary

A Newsweek follow-up (April 16, 2026) to the missed April 14 deadline for 45+ UAP videos requested by Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]] from [[department-of-war]] Secretary [[pete-hegseth]]. Speaking on former Rep. Matt Gaetz's OAN show, Luna described how her staff had to initiate contact with the Pentagon after receiving no response and how she subsequently escalated to the White House. She expressed confidence in Hegseth's support for [[donald-trump]]'s declassification directive and warned unnamed Pentagon resisters they are "about to get rolled." [[aaro]] issued a statement confirming it is "working in close coordination with the White House and across federal agencies" to consolidate UAP records and expedite release — consistent with [[src-war-department-uap-release-2026-04]]. The article adds Hegseth's on-record "in full compliance with that executive order" quote from February 2026.

## Key Claims

- Pentagon missed Luna's April 14 deadline for 45+ UAP videos depicting spherical, cigar-shaped, and Tic Tac-like objects over war zones, oceans, and restricted airspace (Luna)
- Luna's March letter cited incidents near Iran, Syria, U.S. bases/airports, and a 2023 Lake Huron event (Luna)
- No Pentagon response until Luna's staff reached out; she then escalated to the White House after the Pentagon "refused to respond" (Luna)
- Pentagon eventually confirmed receipt and offered a future briefing of the House Oversight Committee — an offer Luna rejected as not the Pentagon's call to make (Luna)
- AARO is "working in close coordination with the White House and across federal agencies" to consolidate UAP records and expedite release (DoW/AARO statement to Newsweek)
- Hegseth told reporters in February he had no specific timeline but would be "in full compliance with that executive order" (Hegseth, on record)
- Trump's February 2026 directive to release UAP/alien files has not been upheld as of April 16 (Luna)
