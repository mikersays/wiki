**Anduril Industries** is a U.S. defense technology company focused on autonomous systems, counter-UAS, and AI-driven sensing. The NORTHCOM [[flyaway-kit|Flyaway Kit]] deployed in the opening hours of [[operation-epic-fury]] is Anduril-built, combining:

- **Wisp** — AI-infrared, 360-degree motion sensor
- **Heimdal** — mobile thermal optics and radar trailer
- **Pulsar** — AI-enhanced electromagnetic warfare platform; RF detection and jamming
- **Anvil** — self-guided drone-on-drone interceptor (physically rams targets)
- **[[lattice-c2|Lattice]]** — AI-enabled, open-architecture command-and-control software fusing sensors and effectors

See [[src-northcom-drone-incursion-2026-03]], [[src-drone-incursions-epic-fury-2026-03]], and [[src-epic-fury-drone-defeat-2026-03]].

## March 2026 — $20B Army enterprise IDIQ

Per [[src-anduril-army-20b-counter-drone-idiq-2026-03|Breaking Defense]]: the Army awarded Anduril a new firm-fixed-price enterprise IDIQ contract vehicle with a **$20 billion ceiling over 10 years**, consolidating 120 prior Army-Anduril contracts. The first task order — **$87M** for [[lattice-c2|Lattice]] C2 software supporting [[jiatf-401]]'s C-UAS mission — makes Lattice the JIATF-401 C2 backbone. Pentagon-wide buyers can place orders against the vehicle. President and CBO [[matthew-steckman]] characterized the figure as an ordering ceiling, not obligated funding. The award is part of a broader Army [[army-enterprise-contracts|enterprise-contract consolidation]] under [[brent-ingraham]] (118 → 14 contracts in 8 months). Closest precedent: [[palantir]]'s August 2025 $10B enterprise IDIQ.
