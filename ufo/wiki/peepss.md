**PEEPSS — Photonic-Enabled ExoPlanet Spectroscopic Sensor** — proposed integrated instrument concept for NASA's [[habitable-worlds-observatory|Habitable Worlds Observatory]] (HWO). Lead author [[genevieve-markees]]; arXiv:2604.23126; SPIE proceedings DOI 10.1117/12.3064610.

## Design

- Uses [[photonic-lantern|photonic lanterns]] to couple coronagraph **dark-hole** light into single-mode fibers feeding the main spectrograph.
- Simultaneously uses **rejected host-star light from inside the dark hole** for **focal-plane wavefront sensing**.
- Combining science (spectroscopy) and wavefront-sensing channels in one photonic system eliminates **non-common-path errors** between sensing and science arms.

## Why it matters

- HWO must achieve **~10⁻¹⁰** star/planet contrast — requires outstanding wavefront measurement and control.
- Particularly advantageous in the **near-infrared (NIR)** bandpass.
- Coronagraph inner working angle scales as λ/D, so NIR imaging normally suffers larger IWAs than visible — PEEPSS reduces the achievable NIR IWA, expanding accessible exoplanet parameter space for habitable-zone imaging and [[biosignatures|biosignature]] spectroscopy.
