# Explosive New Whistleblower Confirms "Immaculate Constellation" UAP Program

## Summary

In April 2025, former Pentagon intelligence analyst [[matthew-brown]] became the first government insider to publicly confirm the existence of [[immaculate-constellation]], a classified UAP surveillance program. Brown appeared on-camera in an interview series by investigative journalists Jeremy Corbell and George Knapp. His credentials -- including roles as policy advisor for the Office of the Secretary of Defense and technical advisor for OUSD(I&S), with TS/SCI clearance -- lend significant weight to his claims.

## Key Claims

- **[[immaculate-constellation]] is an unacknowledged Special Access Program (USAP)** created after [[lue-elizondo]] exposed [[aatip]] through the 2017 New York Times article. It was designed to continue UAP data collection under tighter secrecy.
- The program served as a **central parent USAP** that consolidated UAP data from across military and intelligence collection platforms, both "tasked" and "untasked."
- Brown discovered the program **accidentally** while managing files on a Pentagon server, encountering a classified 2018 Schriever War Game document containing program details and UAP imagery.
- The discovered document referenced **Russian naval awareness of UAP** near the Kamchatka Peninsula, with Russian vessels appearing to anticipate the arrival of a "large black triangle" craft.
- The program's database includes UAP imagery captured via infrared sensors, FLIR systems, full-motion video, and still photography.
- Brown was measured in his claims: "Nothing in those videos proves extraterrestrial or non-human... certainly anomalous, exotic, and unexplainable."

## Official Response

The **Department of Defense** denied the program's existence. Spokesperson Sue Gough stated: "The Department of Defense has no record, present or historical, of any type of SAP called 'IMMACULATE CONSTELLATION.'"

## Congressional Involvement

- A report on [[immaculate-constellation]] was allegedly submitted to Congress and referenced in documents for a November 2024 House hearing.
- Journalist Michael Shellenberger presented information about the program at congressional proceedings.
- The ODNI released a FOIA-related document (DF-2025-00021) pertaining to the program.

## Connection to Broader Disclosure

Brown's testimony reinforces [[david-grusch]]'s claims about [[crash-retrieval-programs]] and [[uap-reverse-engineering]], while focusing specifically on the surveillance and data-collection apparatus. Together they suggest a larger ecosystem of classified UAP-related activities.
