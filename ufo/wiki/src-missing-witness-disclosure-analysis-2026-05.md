## Summary

Fliegerfaust analysis weaving together [[anna-paulina-luna]]'s April 29, 2026 "interdimensional beings" SCIF claim on Pod Force One, [[donald-trump]]'s renewed "near future" UAP file-release promise, the Pentagon's missed April 14 deadline for Luna's 46 demanded UAP videos, and the House Oversight investigation led by [[james-comer]] and [[eric-burlison]] into 13 missing or dead Americans tied to nuclear, rocket, and aerospace work. Centers [[sullivan-whistleblower|Matthew James Sullivan]]'s May 2024 death before scheduled congressional testimony as the strongest individual case, while auditing weaker names on the list (Loureiro, Grillmair, Los Alamos non-scientists) against prosaic explanations. Argues the disclosure pipeline exists in statute (NARA Record Group 615) but has not yet received Luna's 46 videos, Sullivan's testimony material, or Luna's SCIF evidence, leaving rhetoric ahead of artifacts.

## Key Claims

- [[sullivan-whistleblower|Sullivan]] died May 12, 2024 of a ruled accidental overdose (alcohol, alprazolam, cyclobenzaprine, imipramine) weeks before scheduled Congressional UAP testimony; his eulogist was retired Maj. Gen. David Abba, formerly director of the DoD Special Access Program Central Office (attributed to Northern District OCME ruling, obituary)
- [[eric-burlison]] publicly called Sullivan's death a "suspicious suicide" despite the medical examiner's accidental ruling, but has not placed supporting evidence on the committee record (attributed to Burlison via Fox News and April 16 letter to Patel)
- [[james-comer]] and Burlison sent letters on April 20, 2026 to [[kash-patel]] (FBI), [[pete-hegseth]] (DoW), Chris Wright (DOE), and [[jared-isaacman]] (NASA) requesting briefings on 13 missing/dead Americans by April 27; only the Pentagon responded substantively (attributed to Comer/Burlison letters)
- David Wilcock died by suicide on April 20, 2026 in Boulder County; Luna posted about his "tragic passing" the next day (attributed to Boulder County Sheriff, Luna social media)
- [[aaro]] has not published its 2025 annual report; Congress no longer treats AARO as a neutral arbiter (attributed to Fliegerfaust analysis)
- NARA Record Group 615 (44 U.S.C. 2107 note, per NDAA 2024 sections 1841–1843) has received initial agency transfers but does not yet contain the 46 Luna videos or Sullivan's testimony material (attributed to statutory record)
- [[william-neil-mccasland]]'s wife Susan Wilkerson publicly stated he retained only common clearances since retiring 13 years earlier and had only "a brief association with the UFO community" (attributed to Wilkerson public statement)
