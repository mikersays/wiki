The **Merops** is a low-cost (~$15,000/unit) U.S. Army counter-drone **interceptor** built by [[perennial-autonomy]] and deployed against [[shahed-drone|Iranian Shahed-style loitering munitions]] during [[operation-epic-fury]]. It is among the systems credited with shifting the cost curve in favor of the defender: Shaheds are estimated at ~$45k+/unit, so Merops trades favorably even at multi-shot engagement rates.

## Key facts

- Per Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]] (April 16 2026 House Defense Appropriations testimony), the Army purchased **13,000 Merops units** in the **opening days of the Iran war** ([[operation-epic-fury]]) — see [[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04]].
- CSIS analyst [[tom-karako]] has previously credited Merops and similar low-cost interceptors with doing the "lion's share of the drone killing" in CENTCOM (see [[src-counter-drone-middle-east-jiatf-2026-04]]).

## Doctrinal context

The Merops buy is part of the broader U.S. response to [[operation-spider-web]] and the [[shahed-drone]] proliferation problem — a deliberate move down the cost curve so the defender does not run out of $1M-per-shot interceptors first.

## Procurement

- **May 19 2026** — included under the **$500M three-year IDIQ** awarded to [[perennial-autonomy]] by [[jiatf-401]] for enterprise C-UAS support; bundled with [[bumblebee-counter-uas]] V1/V2 and [[hornet-strike-drone]] ([[src-perennial-autonomy-jiatf-401-500m-2026-05]]).

## Open questions

- Platform details (range, payload, control mode) remain underspecified in public sources.
