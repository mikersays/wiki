The **Merops** (also called the **Surveyor**) is a low-cost (~$15,000/unit) U.S. Army counter-drone **interceptor** built by [[perennial-autonomy]] and deployed against [[shahed-drone|Iranian Shahed-style loitering munitions]] during [[operation-epic-fury]]. It is among the systems credited with shifting the cost curve in favor of the defender: Shaheds are estimated at ~$45k+/unit, so Merops trades favorably even at multi-shot engagement rates.

## Platform

Per [[src-dronexl-perennial-interceptor-doctrine-2026-05]] (citing Defense Express): a fixed-wing interceptor flying **>280 km/h (175 mph)** — fast enough to catch jet-powered Shahed variants. It is **pilot-cued at launch** then autonomous in terminal engagement via onboard **computer vision + RF detection** with jam-resistant control links. Originally built by [[perennial-autonomy]] (ex–White Stork / Project Eagle) for Ukrainian crews, where Perennial reports **4,000+ intercepts of Russian drones since mid-2024**.

## Key facts

- Per Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]] (April 16 2026 House Defense Appropriations testimony), the Army purchased **13,000 Merops units** in the **opening days of the Iran war** ([[operation-epic-fury]]) — see [[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04]].
- CSIS analyst [[tom-karako]] has previously credited Merops and similar low-cost interceptors with doing the "lion's share of the drone killing" in CENTCOM (see [[src-counter-drone-middle-east-jiatf-2026-04]]).

## Doctrinal context

The Merops buy is part of the broader U.S. response to [[operation-spider-web]] and the [[shahed-drone]] proliferation problem — a deliberate move down the cost curve so the defender does not run out of $1M-per-shot interceptors first.

## Procurement

- **May 19 2026** — included under the **$500M three-year IDIQ** awarded to [[perennial-autonomy]] by [[jiatf-401]] for enterprise C-UAS support; bundled with [[bumblebee-counter-uas]] V1/V2 and [[hornet-strike-drone]] ([[src-perennial-autonomy-jiatf-401-500m-2026-05]]).

## Cost curve

Even at ~$15K, Merops runs **6–7× the price of a ~$2,000–2,500 Ukrainian Wild Hornets Sting** interceptor; per [[src-dronexl-perennial-interceptor-doctrine-2026-05]] the premium reflects Merops's AI/autonomy layer. Open question: whether $15K is a price floor or entry-tier pricing.

## European production

Co-produced in Germany with Munich-based [[twentyfour-industries]] for European/NATO buyers; **Lithuania bought 48 units in April 2026**, with Poland and Romania training/deploying on NATO's eastern flank ([[src-dronexl-perennial-interceptor-doctrine-2026-05]]).
