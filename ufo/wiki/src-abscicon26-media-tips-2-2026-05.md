## Summary

Second media tipsheet for [[abscicon26|AbSciCon26]] — the biennial Astrobiology Science Conference hosted by the [[american-geophysical-union]] in Madison WI (May 17–22 2026, ~900 contributions). Tipsheet 2 surfaces sessions across five umbrellas: **robot explorers** for Mars and icy worlds ([[icefin]], [[mars-caves]] heli-drone, 15 m drill), **planetary protection** (including a new [[cospar]] icy-worlds policy update — see [[icy-worlds-protection-policy]]), [[technosignatures]] (spider pulsars, megamirrors, exo-Earth detectability), **the astronaut life** ([[synthetic-biology-spaceflight|synthetic biology, plasma-jet decontamination, bioplastic Mars habitats]]), and **"what is life?"** humanities/philosophy/safety sessions including a dedicated session on the threats astrobiologists face from public backlash ([[astrobiology-safety]]). Companion to [[src-abscicon26-media-tips-1-2026-05]].

## Key Claims

- **AbSciCon26** convenes ~900 posters/talks/town halls/plenaries in Madison, WI (May 17–22 2026), hosted by [[american-geophysical-union]].
- [[roger-wiens]] (Purdue) presents a proposed heli-drone concept to explore Mars lava-tube caves.
- [[henry-wolf]] presents an underwater charging station extending dive time for the [[icefin]] sub-ice robot, an Europa-precursor platform.
- [[samuel-howell]] presents a Juneau Icefield expedition that sampled a 300-meter-deep subglacial reservoir as a Europa drilling test run.
- [[joey-palmowski]] (Purdue) presents a drill payload enabling Mars landers to sample 15 m below the surface.
- [[jennifer-blank]] presents specialized robots for spelunking Mars caves for biosignatures.
- [[cospar]] released an update to its **Planetary Protection Policy** (descended from the 1967 [[outer-space-treaty|UN Outer Space Treaty]]) defining icy-world categories, the driest/coldest conditions Earth life can survive, and a **1,000-year biological-exploration period** during which contamination controls apply ([[icy-worlds-protection-policy]]).
- Technosignature sessions include: stellar-plasma distortion of alien radio signals (extends [[src-plasma-technosignature-scattering-2026]]); "spider pulsars as escape ships" speculation; software to detect megamirrors around exoplanets; and which exoplanets could observe Earth via transit photometry.
- Astronaut-life research includes freeze-dried protein-builder pharmacy ("just add water" drugs viable ~3 years), plasma-jet cleaning of fabrics, bioplastic Mars habitats ([[robin-wordsworth]]), and a blind-astronaut simulated mission.
- A dedicated online session, **"Safety for Scientists: Astrobiology, a Global Issue, and a Call to Action,"** addresses harassment of life-search researchers and proposes international safety recommendations — extending the academic-stigma thread from [[src-academic-uap-stigma-2026-04]].
- Humanities sessions include "Why haven't we met any aliens? They may have… lost their reason to live" and "How the military-industrial complex shapes astronomy."

## Notable Quotes

> "Searching for life beyond earth can become an incendiary activity: it often generates significant public attention and emotion around a topic that is inherently controversial and uncertain. Misunderstandings about the science involved sometimes end in public backlash that threatens scientists' safety." — AGU advisory, Safety for Scientists session

> "The new guidelines define icy worlds, establish the driest and coldest conditions at which Earth life can survive and assign a 1,000-year period of biological exploration for all icy worlds during which contamination controls should be honored to preserve them for science." — AGU advisory, on the updated COSPAR Planetary Protection Policy
