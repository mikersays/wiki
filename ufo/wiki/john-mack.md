# John E. Mack

Harvard Medical School psychiatrist (1929–2004), Pulitzer Prize winner (for a biography of T. E. Lawrence), and the most academically credentialed figure to take the **alien-abduction / experiencer phenomenon** seriously as a clinical subject. Mack approached experiencers not as deluded but as people reporting genuine, transformative anomalous experiences, drawing both prominence and institutional controversy at Harvard in the 1990s.

After his death in 2004, his research participant and personal assistant [[karin-austin|Karin Austin]] helped preserve his archives; in **2022 those archives were transferred to Harvard University and Rice University's [[center-for-the-impossible|Center For The Impossible]]**, where they anchor an ongoing multidisciplinary metadata analysis of experiencer materials. His name and legacy are carried by the [[john-mack-institute|John Mack Institute]].
