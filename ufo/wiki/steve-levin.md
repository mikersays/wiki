**Steve Levin** is the [[juno]] project scientist and MWR co-investigator at [[nasa-jpl]]. He provided the key caveat on the Europa ice-shell measurement: the ~29 km estimate refers to the cold, rigid, conductive outer layer of pure water ice; total shell thickness could be greater if a warmer convective layer exists beneath, or ~5 km less if the ice contains dissolved salt.
