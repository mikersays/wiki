# Disclosure Foundation

Nonprofit advocating UAP transparency through legal action, policy advocacy, and whistleblower support.

## Leadership

- **Executive Director:** [[jordan-flowers]]
- **Board chair:** [[chris-mellon]] (per [[src-defensescoop-pursue-not-disclosure-2026-05]], May 14 2026)

## Profile

- Recurring on-the-record critic of [[aaro]]'s reporting cadence and classification record.
- Cited as a counterweight to skeptics in PURSUE-era coverage (see [[src-sciam-pursue-skeptics-2026-05]]).
- Provides interpretive framing for journalists covering [[uap-disclosure]] policy.
- Takes [[pursue]] Release 01 "at face value as a genuine transparency effort — while remaining clear-eyed that genuine efforts can still fall short" (Mellon, May 2026).

## Disclosure Forum (June 2026)

Per [[src-defensescoop-pursue-not-disclosure-2026-05]], the foundation will convene a **Disclosure Forum in June 2026** with legislators, national-security officials, scientists, legal experts, and journalists. Agenda includes [[psyops-disinformation|1950s-era provenance laundering]] as a top-tier PURSUE-era public-accountability matter raised by [[jordan-flowers]].

## NSA FOIA Release (May 2026)

In May 2026, the foundation released more than **300 pages** of [[nsa]] intelligence reports on UFO sightings obtained through a FOIA request and subsequent appeal. Many records were formerly classified [[top-secret-umbra]]. The documents are linked to a 1980 FOIA lawsuit and the **Yeates Memo** (declassified 2009), whose supporting materials had been withheld until this appeal succeeded. Chief legal officer [[hunt-willis]] leads the ongoing legal effort against remaining NSA redactions on UAP material dating to the 1960s ([[src-disclosure-foundation-nsa-documents-2026-05]], [[src-newsweek-pursue-13-jets-star-object-2026-05]]).

This represents a **non-governmental, legal-process channel** for UAP document release, distinct from the executive-branch [[pursue]] initiative.
