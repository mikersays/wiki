## Summary

A comprehensive 118-page review paper (arXiv:2605.21093) cataloging the full diversity of proposed [[technosignatures]] in the scientific literature. The work originated as a collective workshop at the **Penn State SETI Symposium (PSETI 2023)** and is led by [[clement-vidal]] with 20 co-authors. The review is organized by spatial scale — starting from Earth's surface and expanding outward through Earth orbit, the [[solar-gravitational-lens]] region, the [[oort-cloud]], exoplanetary systems, stellar engineering, compact objects, interstellar communication and travel, and galactic/extragalactic/universal scales. It introduces both the [[kardashev-scale]] and the [[barrow-scale]] (inward-facing miniaturization of technology). The paper concludes with a synthesis of [[biosignatures]]–technosignature synergies, [[anomaly-detection]] strategies, multimodal search approaches, instrumentation, search prioritization, and epistemological challenges.

## Key Claims

- Technosignatures span all spatial scales from Earth's surface ([[terrestrial-technosignature|terrestrial leakage]], [[nighttime-light-emissions]]) to universal signatures; this is the most comprehensive single catalog of proposed types to date (Vidal et al.)
- Industrial atmospheric pollutants such as SF6 and NF3 at ~1 ppm concentrations could be detectable in the atmospheres of "service worlds" orbiting [[m-dwarf-stars]], extending the terrestrial-technosignature concept to exoplanetary atmospheres (figure from Seager et al. 2023)
- The [[barrow-scale]] (inward miniaturization) complements the outward-facing [[kardashev-scale]] and both should inform [[seti]] search strategies (Vidal et al.)
- Synergies between [[biosignatures]] and technosignatures searches are underexploited; the paper advocates multimodal and [[anomaly-detection]] strategies rather than single-channel approaches (Vidal et al.)
- The search space for interstellar communication has many under-explored dimensions beyond radio and optical, including stellar modification, stellar pollution, and compact-object engineering (Vidal et al.)
