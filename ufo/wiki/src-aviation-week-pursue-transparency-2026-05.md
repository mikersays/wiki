## Summary

Aviation Week's [[pursue|PURSUE]] Release 01 coverage adopts an aerospace-industry trade frame: it foregrounds the **platform and sensor provenance** of the unresolved video trove rather than the political-disclosure framing dominant in mainstream wires. Everstine and Norris emphasize that the bulk of the recent military reports involve **General Atomics ASI [[reaper-uas|MQ-9 Reapers]]** flown by USAF (including special-operations sorties out of the UAE) using full-motion video and other sensors. They flag that one diamond-shaped Greece UAP was **detectable only via short-wave infrared (SWIR)** — a sensor-modality observation distinct from the standard MTS-B IR critique. The piece also surfaces operational-context details that other outlets glossed: **X-band radar jamming from the Turkish border** during a February 2023 F-15E Syria report, and a 2022 [[reaper-uas|MQ-9]] over western Syria approached by a Russian Su-27/Su-35. Reception-wise the article is neutral-to-skeptical: it questions whether future tranches will include collection methodology, classification rationale, or full-resolution sensor data.

## Key Claims

- PURSUE Release 01 contains **162 total records**; ~82 events tied to U.S. military sources, 12 from [[nasa]] (Gemini/Apollo/Skylab), 28 video clips, ~93 linked to [[aaro]] submissions, ~50% mission reports.
- The bulk of recent-operations reports involve **General Atomics ASI MQ-9 Reapers** ([[reaper-uas]]) operated by the USAF; encounter geography spans Mediterranean, Middle East, Africa, Asia, North America.
- A [[centcom|CENTCOM]]-submitted [[reaper-uas|MQ-9]] clip over Greece (1m05s) shows a "diamond-shaped" object moving at 434 kt (500 mph), only detectable via **short-wave infrared (SWIR)** sensor.
- A June 2024 special-operations [[reaper-uas|MQ-9]] flying from the **UAE** reported a "hot, spherical object" with a "vertical unwavering probe."
- March 2023 USAF **F-16s in Iraq** reported "several bright objects maneuvering quickly" through targeting pods.
- August 2020 **U.S. Navy MH-60** over the Persian Gulf reported three small contacts "appearing with a wing and airframe structure."
- November 2016 **U.S. Navy P-8** observed a sea-skimming-missile-like UAP at ~500 kt above the ocean.
- February 2023 **F-15Es** over Syria near al-Shaddadi reported a balloon-like UAP and experienced **X-band radar jamming from the Turkish border**.
- May 2022 [[reaper-uas|MQ-9]] in western Syria was approached by a Russian Su-27 or Su-35 that flew close beneath the drone.
- [[fbi|FBI]] accounts for **56 of 162** records, including the 1964 "Socorro saucer" New Mexico State Police case and 30+ late-2025 photo records.
- Heavily redacted 2025 FBI-to-AARO report describes a "super-hot" orb hovering over an unidentified U.S. military facility, traveling 20 mi at a speed too fast for a pursuing helicopter, with an additional "swarm" of lights.
- Open question: whether future PURSUE tranches will disclose collection methodology, classification, analytic conclusions, or **full-resolution sensor data**.
