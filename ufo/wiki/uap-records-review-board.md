# UAP Records Review Board (UAP RRB)

Independent expert body proposed by the [[uap-disclosure-act]]: composed of subject-matter experts, empowered to examine all governmental UAP records regardless of classification, with authority to compel disclosure and report to the President.

Modeled explicitly on the **JFK Records Review Board** established by the 1992 JFK Assassination Records Collection Act. Senate-passed in 2023 as part of the [[uap-disclosure-act]]; the RRB-empowering provisions were deleted in the FY2024 NDAA conference and remain a top advocacy ask of [[tim-gallaudet]], [[chris-mellon]], the [[uap-disclosure-fund]], and the [[sol-foundation]] ([[src-gallaudet-house-testimony-2024-11]]).

What *did* survive the conference was the National-Archives-only mandate — the [[uap-records-collection|UAP Records Collection]] at [[nara]] (Record Group 615). In the absence of an empowered RRB, enforcement of that mandate has fallen to congressional oversight: Rep. [[eric-burlison]]'s May 2026 records interrogatories to the [[mitre|MITRE]] [[ffrdc]] (and his earlier [[mit-lincoln-laboratory]] letter) press private contractors to identify and transfer responsive UAP records ([[src-burlison-mitre-press-release-2026-05]]).
