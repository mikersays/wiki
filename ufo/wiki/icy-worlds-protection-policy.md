The **Icy Worlds Planetary Protection Policy** is a 2026 [[cospar]] update to the international planetary-protection framework descended from Article IX of the 1967 [[outer-space-treaty|UN Outer Space Treaty]]. Surfaced at [[abscicon26]] ([[src-abscicon26-media-tips-2-2026-05]]), the update introduces:

- A formal **icy-world** classification.
- Empirically anchored bounds on the **driest and coldest conditions under which Earth life can survive** — defining the contamination-risk envelope.
- A **1,000-year biological-exploration period** during which contamination controls apply to icy-world targets, "to preserve them for science."

The policy directly affects mission design for [[europa-clipper]], JUICE, and any future Enceladus/Europa drill or lander mission. Sister concept to the broader [[planetary-protection]] page.
