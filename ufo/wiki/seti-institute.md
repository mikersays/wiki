The **SETI Institute** is a non-profit astrobiology and [[seti|Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence]] research organization based in Mountain View, California. It is institutionally distinct from the broader [[seti]] concept but runs or partners on many of the major technosignature programs, including [[breakthrough-listen]].

The Institute publishes a news channel that covers its scientists' work and related exoplanet/astrobiology results — source of [[src-alpha-centauri-a-exoplanet-2026]] (Franck Marchis interviewing STScI's Julien Girard on the JWST Alpha Centauri A candidate) and the BLIPSS announcement.

SETI Institute also conceived and advocates for [[project-blue]], a dedicated telescope to directly image planets around [[alpha-centauri]] A and B.

## Programs and partnerships

- **[[blipss|BLIPSS]]** — Cornell + SETI Institute + Breakthrough Listen 4–8 GHz [[green-bank-telescope|GBT]] [[galactic-center|Galactic Center]] search for [[periodic-spectral-signal|periodic spectral signals]] ([[src-seti-galactic-center-search-2025-07]]). Lead [[akshay-suresh]]; advisor [[vishal-gajjar]]; GBT Project Scientist [[steve-croft]] (SETI Institute Adjunct Senior Astronomer).

- **Orpheus** — Mars VTOL hopper mission proposal by [[connor-bunn]] and [[pascal-lee]] (57th [[lunar-and-planetary-science-conference]]); targets extant-life [[biosignatures]] in Cerberus Fossae volcanic vents ([[src-orpheus-mars-volcanic-hopper-2026-04]])
