# Unmanned Aircraft Flight Restriction (UAFR)

A new airspace designation proposed by the [[faa]] in a May 6, 2026 notice of proposed rulemaking (docket FAA-2026-4558), "Restrict the Operation of Unmanned Aircraft in Close Proximity to a Fixed Site Facility" ([[src-faa-drone-rule-critical-infrastructure-2026-05]]). A UAFR creates a legal "virtual no-trespassing sign" over a specific fixed-site facility — horizontal boundary at the property line, vertical ceiling generally 400 ft AGL, 5-year renewable term.

## Variants

- **Standard UAFR** — for operators of the 16 critical-infrastructure sectors defined by [[national-security-memorandum-22|NSM-22]]. Transit permitted for FAA Parts 91 / 107 / 108 / 135 / 137 operators carrying [[remote-id]].
- **Special UAFR** — for federally sponsored sensitive sites under DHS, DoD, DoE, DoJ, or FAA sponsorship. Far stricter access controls.

## Eligibility examples (Standard)

- Power plants ≥ 500 MW
- Oil refineries ≥ 100,000 bpd
- Prisons ≥ 500 inmates (state prisons newly eligible under the FAA Reauthorization Act of 2024)
- Commercial venues ≥ 2.5M annual attendance
- Data centers ≥ 1 Tbps
- Level I trauma centers with helipads

Food/agriculture and water/wastewater sectors lack draft eligibility criteria; FAA is soliciting comment.

## Prerequisites (§74.56)

Four protective security measures, all required before a UAFR is approved:

1. Restricted access
2. Designated security personnel
3. Security monitoring
4. **[[remote-id|Remote ID]] receiving capability** (14 CFR part 89-compatible; in-house or contracted)

## Important limitations

- A UAFR is a **legal designation only**. It does NOT grant detection or mitigation authority — those still flow from 6 U.S.C. 124n, 10 U.S.C. 130i, and other federal C-UAS statutes.
- Application has four stages (eligibility, need, externalities, NEPA review); FAA targets 90-day final-determination after submission.
- FAA estimates $5,000-$10,000 application cost; example Remote ID receiver: ~$2,800 one-time + ~$1,000/year.

## Significance

Framed by [[airsight]] as "the clearest signal yet" that critical-infrastructure airspace protection is shifting from discretionary security investment to a **regulated capability**. Civilian/critical-infrastructure analogue to the military-base authority expansion in the [[counter-act|COUNTER Act]] (2026 NDAA).

Comment period closes **July 6, 2026**.
