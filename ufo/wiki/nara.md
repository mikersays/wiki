The **National Archives and Records Administration (NARA)** is the U.S. agency responsible for preserving and providing access to federal government records.

## Role in UAP disclosure

Under the FY2024 NDAA, NARA is the designated home of the national [[uap-records-collection|UAP Records Collection]] (**Record Group 615**). Federal agencies — and, per Rep. [[eric-burlison]], contractors and [[ffrdc|FFRDCs]] such as [[mitre|MITRE]] and [[mit-lincoln-laboratory|MIT Lincoln Laboratory]] — are directed to identify, review, digitize, and transfer appropriate UAP records to NARA for public release ([[src-burlison-mitre-press-release-2026-05]]).

This NARA-housed collection is the surviving "National-Archives-only" outcome of the [[uap-disclosure-act]] after the proposed presidentially-appointed [[uap-records-review-board|UAP Records Review Board]] and the recovered-material / eminent-domain provisions were stripped in the FY2024 NDAA conference ([[src-schumer-rounds-uap-colloquy-2023]]).
