**Richard Russell** — U.S. Senator (D-GA); chair of the Senate Armed Services Committee in 1955.

## UAP relevance

In **October 1955**, while traveling on a train in the former Soviet Union, Russell — accompanied by [[e-u-hathaway|Lt. Col. E. U. Hathaway]] (U.S. Army) and others — reported observing two "flying disc aircraft" through a train window. The U.S. Air Attaché called them "excellent sources." The cable also contained Soviet jet-bomber descriptions and notes on a Russian/Czech railroad gauge-change switching system.

The 1955 Russell sighting was previously released by CIA in partially-redacted form. The [[pursue|PURSUE Release 01]] version (surfaced by [[npr|NPR]] May 8 2026 — see [[src-npr-pursue-release-2026-05]]) is the canonical text of the cable.
