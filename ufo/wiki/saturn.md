# Saturn

Sixth planet from the Sun; second-largest gas giant; host of the most extensively studied planetary system in astrobiology after Jupiter, primarily due to the 2004-2017 [[cassini-huygens|Cassini-Huygens]] mission.

## Key Cassini-derived facts

- **Rotation rate ~10.7 hours** — pinned by the 22 proximal orbits of the [[grand-finale]]; Voyager could not resolve this. ([[src-cassini-huygens-ocean-worlds-2026-04]])
- **[[saturn-magnetosphere|Magnetosphere]]** — rotation-dominated and mass-loaded by [[enceladus]]-supplied plasma, with its magnetic cusp in the afternoon sector rather than at noon (Xu et al. 2026). Aligns Saturn with Jupiter as a rotation-dominated regime; suggests Earth's solar-wind-dominated regime may be the special case.
- Ring system precisely characterized in mass and composition.
- Multiple moons of astrobiological interest: [[enceladus]] (subsurface ocean, plumes) and [[titan]] (methane/ethane hydrology, subsurface water ocean).
