# Kardashev Scale

Classification of hypothetical civilizations by their energy use, proposed by Soviet astronomer Nikolai Kardashev in 1964:

- **Type I:** harnesses the total energy reaching its home planet (~10¹⁶ W for Earth).
- **Type II:** harnesses the total energy of its host star (~10²⁶ W; e.g., a Dyson sphere).
- **Type III:** harnesses the total energy of its host galaxy (~10³⁶ W).

Used as a yardstick for both speculative engineering scales and [[seti|SETI]] / [[technosignatures]] detection volumes.

## Relevance to UAP / astrobiology vault

- The May 2026 **BRaTs** framework ([[src-brats-radio-technosignatures-2026-05]], [[michael-a-garrett]]) argues that broadband radio leakage from a **Kardashev Type I** civilization should be detectable out to **~100 pc** using next-gen wide-field surveys ([[ska]]) with [[vlbi]] follow-up.
- Existing narrowband SETI volumes for Type I leakage have been much smaller; the broadband-Doppler-insensitive deep-field strategy extends reach by enabling long integrations on the same patch of sky.
