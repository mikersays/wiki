# Kimberly S. Engels

Associate Professor of Philosophy at **Molloy University** and Research Director of the [[john-mack-institute|John Mack Institute]]. Her work applies existentialism, ethics, and phenomenology to the **lived conscious experience of people reporting UAP/NHI contact**, and she is the author of *Contact and Multidimensional Subjectivity: Towards a Phenomenology of Disclosure* (College Publications, 2026).

At the July 2026 [[src-breaking-the-taboo-uap-contact-symposium-2026-05|"Breaking the Taboo" symposium]] (co-organizer), Engels argues that **anthropocentric ethical frameworks are insufficient** to evaluate moral responsibility in encounters where one party (the UAP intelligence) may be "situated differently in time" and hold knowledge unavailable to humans — a complement to [[michael-bohlander]]'s use-of-force argument. She also supplies the phenomenological framework guiding [[karin-austin]]'s experiencer-archive analysis.
