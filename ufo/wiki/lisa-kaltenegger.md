**Lisa Kaltenegger** is the founding director of the [[carl-sagan-institute|Carl Sagan Institute]] at Cornell University and a pioneer of exoplanet [[biosignatures|biosignature]] spectral modeling. Her work spans habitable-zone characterization, terrestrial-atmosphere analogues for exoplanet retrieval, and the design of [[habitable-worlds-observatory|HWO]]-class direct-imaging targets.

## Selected work covered in this wiki

- 2026 mineral-false-positives study with Mia Belle Parkinson, [[university-of-edinburgh|Edinburgh's]] Beth Biller, Grant Lach, and Sean McMahon — finds that sulfide minerals, tectosilicate minerals, and potassium ferrocyanide reproduce the ~700 nm [[photosynthesis-red-edge|photosynthesis red edge]], the canonical surface biopigment biosignature ([[src-mineral-false-positives-biosignatures-2026-05]]).
