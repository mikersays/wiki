## Summary

[[newsnation]] report on [[pursue]] Release 02 (May 22, 2026), the Pentagon's second batch of declassified UAP files released at war.gov/UFO. The drop includes over 40 videos requested by lawmakers, plus documents, images, and NASA mission audio. Key items include a 116-page [[armed-forces-special-weapons-project]] file documenting 1948–1950 UAP sightings at [[sandia-new-mexico]], a 1973 [[cia]] report on a Soviet sighting at [[sary-shagan]], [[apollo-12]] medical-debrief audio describing eyes-closed light flashes, [[department-of-energy]] incident reports, and the [[lake-huron-shootdown-2023]] footage. Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]] confirms over 40 lawmaker-requested videos are included, specifically the [[eglin-afb-diamond-formation-2023]] clip she and [[matt-gaetz]] personally requested in 2023.

## Key Claims

- PURSUE Release 02 includes over 40 videos requested by lawmakers, along with files and NASA mission audio (Pentagon)
- A 116-page AFSWP file documents UAP sightings and investigations at Sandia, NM from 1948 to 1950 — the AFSWP being the direct successor to the Manhattan Project (DoW)
- A CIA information report describes a 1973 UAP sighting in the Soviet Union (DoW)
- DOE submitted an unidentified-object incident report with a firsthand U.S. intelligence-official witness account (DoW)
- The Eglin AFB video that Luna and Gaetz visited the base to request in person in 2023 is included; [[aaro]] previously assessed it as a commercial lighting balloon (Luna / AARO)
- Lake Huron shootdown footage released — the UAP was shot down after the Chinese spy-balloon episode (DoW)
- One video shows the U.S. tracking a foreign submarine as a UAP becomes visible in the frame — consistent with [[transmedium-uap]] reporting (DoW)
- Two videos show a possible USO (three objects in water, fourth enters/exits frame) and a fast-mover making sudden directional changes (DoW)
- [[apollo-12]] audio captures Conrad and two pilots describing light flashes observed while trying to sleep, recorded Dec 31, 1969 (NASA/DoW)
- Mercury-Atlas mission audio describes astronauts seeing unidentified "light shavings" (NASA/DoW)
