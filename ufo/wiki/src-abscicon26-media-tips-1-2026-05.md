## Summary

[[astrobiology-com|Astrobiology.com]] (Keith Cowing, May 18 2026) reproduces [[american-geophysical-union|AGU]]'s first media tipsheet for [[abscicon26|AbSciCon26]] — the biennial **Astrobiology Science Conference** (Monona Terrace, Madison WI, May 17–22 2026). Across ~900 posters, talks, town halls, and plenaries, AGU highlights ~20 sessions under four umbrellas: [[biosignatures]], Earth extremes, evolution of life near and far, and exoplanets / habitable zones. Tipsheet 1 surfaces research threads already tracked in this vault — [[assembly-theory]] and surface-mineral [[false-positive-biosignatures]], technosignature searches including a humpback-whale "missed signals" non-human-intelligence analog, [[magnetotactic-bacteria]] biosignatures for energy-poor exoplanets, deep-seafloor and hydrogen-using extremophiles, [[infrared-photosynthesis]] pushing the [[photosynthesis-red-edge]], and a [[hycean-worlds]] early-Earth hydrogen-atmosphere paper led by Paul Rimmer. The release is a forward-look advisory; no new data products are published yet.

## Key Claims

- **AbSciCon26** is hosted by AGU in Madison, WI (May 17–22 2026), with ~900 scientific contributions.
- Sessions group under four umbrellas: biosignatures, Earth extremes, evolution of life near and far, and exoplanets / habitable zones.
- [[estelle-janin|Estelle Janin]] (Monday "Assembly Theory Across Scales") presents "signs of exoplanet life in atmospheric complexity" — assembly-theory framing of biosignature detection.
- Fred Sharpe presents humpback whale "night thrums" as an Earth analog for "missed signals from non-human intelligences" under SETI/[[technosignatures]] sessions.
- [[felisa-wolfe-simon|Felisa Wolfe-Simon]] presents magnetotactic-bacteria magnets as candidate life signs on energy-poor exoplanets.
- [[laura-barge|Laura Barge]] (Tue oral): "Biosignatures of Earth's earliest life and how to spot them."
- [[william-brazelton|William Brazelton]] (Tue oral): drilling expedition finds living microbes 1 km beneath the seafloor.
- Angelique Ray (Wed): mountain-summit soil microbes use hydrogen gas for energy.
- Kayla Sorensen (Wed): microbes may subsist on oxygen made by rocks — "aerobic aliens" session.
- Jani Radebaugh (Wed, [[titan]] session "Dragonfly and Beyond"): Alaskan-dunes mission searching for clues to life in alien sands.
- Rohan Shiradhonkar (Mon): infrared-light photosynthesis pushes the photosynthesis red edge outward, expanding habitability.
- [[paul-rimmer|Paul Rimmer]] (Tue): a hycean early Earth may have had a short-lived hydrogen atmosphere setting conditions for life — direct link to [[hycean-worlds]] / [[k2-18b]] discussion.
- Ana Lobo (Mon): boom-bust seasons may make biosignature detection harder; Jonathan Jernigan: extreme seasons may boost marine but limit land life.
- Chase Cooper (Mon): lunar orbiter glint of Earth's oceans, tests method for finding exo-oceans.
- Companion meetings flagged: JpGU-AGU Joint Meeting (May 24–29, Chiba) and AGU26 Annual Meeting (Dec 7–11, San Francisco).

## Notable Quotes
