# Mick West

**Mick West** is an independent UAP-skeptic researcher, debunker, and founder of the **Metabunk** discussion forum (metabunk.org). Author of *Escaping the Rabbit Hole* (2018). His analytic frame emphasizes prosaic explanations rooted in sensor physics — [[parallax]], [[lens-flare-explanations|lens flare]], thermal blooming, and similar [[infrared-imagery|IR-imagery]] artifacts — and he is a recurring critical voice on viral UAP videos.

## Role in the May 2026 PURSUE rollout

On the day of [[pursue]] Release 01 (May 8, 2026), West opened a Metabunk thread coordinating community frame-by-frame analysis of the 162-file tranche ([[src-metabunk-pursue-analysis-2026-05]]). Same-day findings credited to West and contributors:

- Release dominated by [[infrared-imagery|thermal IR]] videos from [[reaper-uas|Reaper-type UAS]] platforms with strong [[parallax]] and limited context.
- **PR-19 = Baghdad Phantom**, previously leaked unredacted by [[jeremy-corbell]] in 2023 — PURSUE adds nothing new for that case.
- **PR-48** likely shows offshore wind turbines with sensor floaters/[[lens-flare-explanations|lens artifacts]].
- FBI lab-rendered "bronze metallic object" overlay (in [[fbi-case-file-62-hq-83894]]) is a graphic reconstruction, not raw evidence.

In the same news cycle, [[src-sciam-pursue-skeptics-2026-05]] quoted West summarily characterizing the videos as "**more dots, more parallax**."

## Methodology

- Frame-by-frame public analysis on Metabunk, often crowdsourced.
- Comparison of UAP imagery against publicly available satellite imagery, AIS data, and known optical artifacts.
- Distinction between "object" (3D morphology presupposed) and "area of contrast" (the sensor-faithful descriptor for IR features).

## Critique of AARO's lighting-balloon hypothesis (May 2026)

In comments to [[the-debrief|The Debrief]] previewing [[pursue]] Release 02 ([[src-debrief-pursue-next-batch-preview-2026-05]]), West directly criticized [[aaro]]'s preferred prosaic explanation for the [[eglin-afb-diamond-formation-2023|January 26, 2023 Eglin AFB diamond-formation]] incident:

> "The lighting balloon hypothesis always felt like something someone at AARO liked, but wasn't really supported by much evidence."

This pushes back against the [[lighting-balloon-hypothesis]] pattern in AARO case resolutions — a notable instance of a leading skeptic critiquing an AARO explanation as under-supported (rather than the more common reverse).
