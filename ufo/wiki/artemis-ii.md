**Artemis II** is NASA's first crewed Artemis-program mission — a lunar-flyby flight that includes imaging of the dark side of the Moon as part of its mission profile.

Per [[avi-loeb]]'s May 11, 2026 *The Hill's Rising* interview ([[src-loeb-two-ufo-interpretations-2026-05]]), the **thousands of dark-side-of-the-Moon images** captured by Artemis II in the weeks before the interview (April–May 2026) showed **no anomalous orbital lights**. Loeb uses this null result to argue that Apollo-era lunar-light claims ([[lunar-uap-historical-claims]]) are most plausibly **asteroid impacts** or **optical artifacts**, not anomalous craft.
