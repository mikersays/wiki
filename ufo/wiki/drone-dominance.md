# Drone Dominance

**"Drone dominance"** is a named investment priority in the FY27 [[department-of-war]] budget request, alongside space superiority and increased magazine depth. Per [[pete-hegseth|Secretary of War Hegseth]]'s April 29, 2026 posture testimony to the [[house-armed-services-committee]] ([[src-hegseth-fy27-posture-testimony-2026-04]]), the FY27 $1.5T topline is positioned as a generational reindustrialization that "shifts the entire military acquisition paradigm" toward areas including drones and the next-generation F-47 fighter.

## FY27 line items

Per DefenseScoop ([[src-shield-ai-lucas-drone-swarm-2026-05]]):

- **$53.6B** for autonomy, drones, and contested logistics.
- **$21B** for munitions, counter-drone systems, and collaborative combat aircraft (CCA).

## Operational realization — LUCAS + Hivemind

The May 2026 selection of [[shield-ai]]'s [[hivemind]] swarm-autonomy software as the AI pilot for the [[lucas-drone|LUCAS]] one-way attack drone — first combat-used by [[centcom]] during [[operation-epic-fury]] (Iranian-clone OWA UAS) — is the headline FY27 offensive-capability program executing the drone-dominance doctrine. The Iranian [[shahed-drone|Shahed]]-driven cost-imbalance framing motivates the offensive shift.

## Relation to existing C-UAS / drone-incursion mission

Hegseth's "drone dominance" framing is offensive (U.S. capability development) but pairs with the defensive posture documented in the [[drone-incursions]] and [[counter-uas]] threads — including the [[jiatf-401]] >$600M C-UAS commitment, the [[flyaway-kit]] homeland mission, and [[operation-epic-fury]] CENTCOM C-UAS spending. It also overlaps with [[autonomous-warfare]] as a competitor-investment area.
