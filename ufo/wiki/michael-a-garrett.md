# Michael A. Garrett

Radio astronomer and [[seti]] researcher; lead author of the May 2026 arXiv preprint (2605.10212) introducing the **BRaTs** (Broadband Radio Technosignatures) framework ([[src-brats-radio-technosignatures-2026-05]]). Garrett argues that modern terrestrial RF infrastructure is becoming wideband and distributed, so narrowband-only SETI surveys risk discarding the actual leakage of advanced civilizations as unstructured noise. Proposes a tiered observational pipeline of wide-field [[ska|SKA]] surveys with [[vlbi]] follow-up.

## Key contributions

- BRaTs framework (2026): hierarchical broadband leakage search using SKA + VLBI.
- Multi-parameter candidate discriminator: high brightness temperature, negligible circular polarization, spectral non-uniformity, interstellar scintillation, sub-milliarcsecond astrometric co-motion with nearby stars/exoplanets.
- Argument that broadband continuum signals are Doppler-drift insensitive, enabling long-integration "SETI Deep Fields" extending [[kardashev-scale|Kardashev Type I]] detection volume to ~100 pc.
