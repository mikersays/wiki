## Summary

The [[washington-times]] reports on the second tranche of [[pursue|PURSUE]] UAP records released by the [[department-of-war]] on Friday May 22, 2026 — the first rolling-release follow-on to Release 01 ([[src-pursue-portal-launch-2026-05]]). The lead anecdote is a Form 302-style account of a senior U.S. intelligence officer pursuing "glowing orbs" near a sensitive U.S. military facility, with the object rising from the ground, approaching within 10 feet of the helicopter under night-vision, splitting in two, and outrunning the chase. New historical material includes 200+ [[sandia-new-mexico|Sandia, New Mexico]] sightings (1948–1950) by the U.S. Air Force and the [[armed-forces-special-weapons-project]], and a 1973 [[cia]] document on a Soviet source's observation at the [[sary-shagan]] weapons-testing range in Kazakhstan. The Pentagon emphasizes "unresolved cases," chain-of-custody caveats, and invites private-sector analysis. [[pete-hegseth]] is quoted; war.gov/ufo has received more than 1 billion hits since the May 8 launch.

## Key Claims

- Pentagon released a **second batch** of formerly classified UAP records on Friday May 22, 2026 (attributed to Pentagon officials).
- A senior U.S. intelligence officer reported pursuing "glowing orbs" investigating "loud thuds" at an undisclosed sensitive U.S. military test range "last year"; the object split in two and outpaced the helicopter (Form 302-style report quoted; relates to the late-2025 western U.S. orb-pursuit imagery already cataloged via [[src-fox-helicopter-near-miss-orbs-2026-05]]).
- Newly released earliest files include **200+ sightings of "green orbs," "discs," and "fireballs" reported in Sandia, New Mexico from 1948 to 1950** by the U.S. Air Force and the [[armed-forces-special-weapons-project]], post-WWII successor to the Manhattan Project.
- **war.gov/ufo has received more than 1 billion hits** since launch on May 8 at President Trump's direction (Pentagon officials).
- Materials come from "several government agencies, including the military, the State Department and NASA" — same interagency footprint as Release 01.
- Pentagon: "Many of these materials lack a substantiated chain-of-custody."
- **1973 [[cia]] document**: a Soviet intelligence source at the [[sary-shagan]] weapons-testing range in Kazakhstan reported a "luminous, bright-green unidentified object" forming "concentric circles" over several minutes before dissipating, with no accompanying sound.
- DoW will continue releasing tranches "every few weeks" ([[rolling-release-cadence]]), coordinating "dozens of federal agencies" and reviewing "tens of millions of records, some existing only on paper."
- DoD "welcome[s] the application of private sector analysis, information, and expertise."
