**David Sing** is the Bloomberg Distinguished Professor of Earth and Planetary Sciences at Johns Hopkins University and a leading researcher in exoplanet-atmosphere [[transmission-spectroscopy]]. He was the **principal investigator and senior author** on the *Science* (May 21, 2026) study that resolved the first confirmed daily weather cycle on an exoplanet, the [[hot-jupiter]] [[wasp-94ab]], with lead author [[sagnick-mukherjee]] ([[src-jwst-wasp-94ab-weather-cycle-2026-05]]).

He has long described clouds as a central obstacle to exoplanet atmospheric characterization — "a thorn in our side" — and the limb-resolved [[jwst]] technique demonstrated on WASP-94Ab offered a way around the problem by isolating the planet's clear evening limb.

## Notable Quote

> Clouds are "a thorn in our side ... like trying to look at the planet through a foggy window."
