**Charles "Pete" Conrad Jr.** was the commander of [[apollo-12]] (Nov 1969), the second crewed lunar landing. Per [[newsweek|Newsweek]] coverage of [[pursue]] Release 02 ([[src-newsweek-pursue-release-02-2026-05]]), Conrad — along with [[richard-gordon]] and [[alan-bean]] — appears in a post-mission medical-debrief audio recording describing "streaks of lights" visible with eyes closed during sleep periods. NASA later attributed the phenomenon to internal visual effects (cosmic-ray phosphenes) rather than external light sources.
