**Vishal Gajjar** is an astronomer at the [[seti-institute]] and lead author of the 2026 Astrophysical Journal paper on [[stellar-plasma-scattering]] of radio [[technosignatures]] (see [[src-plasma-technosignature-scattering-2026]]). His work demonstrates that stellar wind plasma turbulence and coronal mass ejections near a transmitting planet can broaden narrowband radio signals before they exit the home system, causing them to evade standard [[seti]] detection pipelines. He argues this effect may partly explain the radio silence seen in [[technosignatures]] surveys.

## BLIPSS (2025)

Gajjar is also the advisor on [[blipss|BLIPSS]] — the Cornell/SETI Institute/Breakthrough Listen 4–8 GHz Galactic Center search for [[periodic-spectral-signal|periodic spectral signals]] led by his graduate student [[akshay-suresh]] ([[src-seti-galactic-center-search-2025-07]]). Gajjar argues that pulse trains are more energy-efficient for interstellar communication than continuous emission, motivating the new signal class.

## Sardinia high-frequency survey (2025)

Gajjar is a co-author on the [[sardinia-radio-telescope|Sardinia Radio Telescope]] high-frequency (6/18 GHz) technosignature survey of the [[galactic-center|Galactic Center]] and 72 [[tess|TESS]] targets — the first SETI search at 18 GHz, returning a null result ([[src-sardinia-technosignature-search-2026-05]]).
