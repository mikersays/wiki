# 3I/ATLAS

Third confirmed [[interstellar-objects|interstellar object]] (after 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov), discovered by the ATLAS survey. Per [[src-loeb-panspermia-impact-survival-2026-03]], [[spherex]] detected organic molecules on 3I/ATLAS, and [[avi-loeb]] proposes using such interstellar objects as carriers for a [[directed-panspermia]] capsule of microbes, nutrients, and power — an act Loeb calls more consequential than Voyager's Golden Record.

Loeb also frames some interstellar objects as possible "Trojan Horses" — outwardly natural comets carrying technological payloads.

## Composition (Nature Astronomy, May 2026)

A University of Michigan–led study measured an anomalously high **[[deuterium|deuterium-to-hydrogen (D/H)]]** ratio in 3I/ATLAS's water — about **30× higher than solar-system comets** and **~40× higher than Earth's oceans**, the highest of any known planetary system or comet. The result implies formation in a much colder, lower-radiation region than the solar nebula and was the first deuterated-water analysis of an interstellar object, made via MDM Observatory gas detections plus [[alma]] submillimeter spectroscopy ([[src-3i-atlas-heavy-water-2026-05]]). This is a mainstream-astronomy composition data point alongside Loeb's speculative framing above.

## Dual-spacecraft UV observation (November 2025)

SwRI-led Ultraviolet Spectrograph (UVS) instruments aboard ESA's [[juice|JUICE]] and NASA's [[europa-clipper|Europa Clipper]] (PI [[kurt-retherford]]) imaged both hemispheres of 3I/ATLAS simultaneously as it passed between the two spacecraft — the first simultaneous two-direction views of a comet's coma. They detected hydrogen, oxygen, and carbon emissions extending ~5 million km from the nucleus, with elevated carbon vs. typical solar-system comets cited as corroborating the comet's interstellar origin ([[src-3i-atlas-dual-spacecraft-imaging-2026-05]]).
