# Trump and Pentagon Promise 'Never-Before-Seen' UAP File Release

## Summary

At a May 3, 2026 White House event for NASA astronauts, [[donald-trump]] told reporters the [[department-of-war]] is preparing to release "very interesting" UFO files uncovered by his administration, sustaining the buildup that began with his February 2026 directive on [[uap-disclosure]]. A Pentagon statement confirmed [[aaro]] is now coordinating with the White House on a "never-before-seen UAP information" rollout, with a second AARO public report covering recent sightings expected soon. Former AARO director [[sean-kirkpatrick]] dismissed the promises as "bluster" and a "shiny object" tied to the Iran war, saying he has reviewed the records and there are no bombshell revelations. The piece also revisits [[anna-paulina-luna]]'s unfulfilled March 2026 video demand to [[pete-hegseth]], VP [[jd-vance]]'s self-described "obsession" with UFO files and stated plan to investigate [[area-51]], and historical UFO interest from prior presidents (Clinton, Carter, Reagan).

## Key Claims

- Trump said at a May 3 White House astronaut event that the administration will release "a lot of things that we haven't" and "some of it's going to be very interesting" (Trump).
- The Pentagon says [[aaro]] is working with the White House to release "never-before-seen UAP information" (Pentagon statement).
- AARO's 2024 debut report revealed hundreds of new UAP incidents but found no confirmed alien-technology sightings; a second report covering more recent sightings is expected soon (Fortune/AP).
- Kirkpatrick: Trump's promises are "bluster" and a "shiny object" to distract from the Iran war; no document with photos or alien interviews exists (Kirkpatrick).
- Kirkpatrick: viral pill-shaped UAP videos are typically jet engines blooming on infrared cameras (Kirkpatrick).
- Luna's March letter to Hegseth demanded dozens of UAP videos labeled with names like "Spherical UAP in clouds"; the deadline passed without videos being produced (Fortune/AP).
- Trump told a [[turning-point-usa]] Phoenix audience in April that "the first releases will begin very, very soon" (Trump).
- VP JD Vance described himself as "obsessed" with UFO files and told podcaster [[benny-johnson]] he intends to "get to the bottom of the UFO files"; invoking Christian faith, Vance said sightings reported as aliens may be "spiritual demons" (Vance).
- Trump signaled personal skepticism in Phoenix: "I figured this was a good crowd because I know you people, you're really into that. I don't know if I am" (Trump).
- The [[task-force-declassification-federal-secrets]] heard fall 2025 testimony from current and former service members; a senior Navy officer described a 2023 California-coast Tic Tac sighting in which a glowing object emerged from the ocean and joined three similar objects before they sped away (Fortune/AP; speaker not named).
- Luna told [[joe-rogan]] she has seen evidence of "interdimensional beings" (Fortune/AP, citing 2025 Rogan podcast).
- [[steven-spielberg]] has an upcoming film titled "Disclosure Day" (Fortune/AP).
- [[greg-eghigian]] (Penn State historian of UFOs): "disappointment can almost be guaranteed" for die-hard UFO followers regardless of disclosure outcome.
- Bill Clinton ordered a [[roswell-incident]] review around its 50th anniversary in 1997; Carter and Reagan claimed personal pre-presidential UFO sightings (Fortune/AP).
