The **Parliamentary Group for the Study of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena** is a non-partisan body in the Japanese Diet focused on UAP policy, with **roughly 80 lawmakers** as members per [[src-japan-pentagon-uap-trove-reaction-2026-05]] (formed 2024). On **March 24, 2026** the group announced it would formally propose creating a specialized government body to oversee UAP intelligence, with consideration scheduled for a Tokyo meeting on March 30, 2026 ([[src-japan-uap-strategy-proposal-2026-03]]).

## Proposal summary

- **Host body** — Deputy Chief Cabinet Secretary for Crisis Management, Cabinet Secretariat
- **Framing** — whole-of-government (not purely defense)
- **Working hypothesis** — human adversary technologies, not non-human intelligence
- **Rationale cited** — U.S. declassification under [[donald-trump]]; ongoing [[genkai-nuclear-uap-incident]]

The group's work builds on former MP [[yoshiharu-asakawa]]'s rejected May 2025 proposal and on [[minoru-kihara]]'s 2024 Defense Ministry posture shift.
