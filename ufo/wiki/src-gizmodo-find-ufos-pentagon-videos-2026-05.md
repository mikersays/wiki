## Summary

[[gizmodo|Gizmodo]]'s [[matt-novak|Matt Novak]] (May 8, 2026) treats the [[src-pursue-portal-launch-2026-05|PURSUE Release 01]] at war.gov/UFO as a "Where's Waldo"–style exercise — most clips are just small dots — but singles out [[dow-uap-pr38]] (2013) as genuinely intriguing for its eight-pointed-star geometry. Frames the rollout as political theater by [[donald-trump]]: a "first tranche" promoted with "maximum transparency" rhetoric but personally treated by Trump as a "Have Fun and Enjoy!" curiosity, which Novak reads as evidence that the administration itself does not believe the material is significant. Embeds a media-criticism thread aligning the release with the [[disclosure-as-distraction]] critique: pre-release "Earth-shattering / religion-questioning" hype as "transparent" cover for the Iran war, record-low consumer sentiment, and high gas prices. Adds an underappreciated methodological caveat: many DoD-listed "date taken" fields are January 1, which Novak suspects is a filler convention for year-only metadata — a useful caution for anyone catalog-mining the release.

## Key Claims

- Pentagon released videos, images, and PDFs at the war.gov/UFO domain on May 8, 2026; Novak flags the domain styling as "intentionally trying to appear mysterious."
- **Most videos are visually thin** — a small dot quickly floating by — but **DOW-UAP-PR38** (2013) shows a shape "resembling an eight-pointed star with arms of alternating length."
- Trump described the drop on Truth Social as the "first tranche" of "maximum transparency," but his casual "Have Fun and Enjoy!" framing signals the admin itself does not believe the contents are significant.
- Pre-release hype claiming the videos would be "Earth-shattering" and would lead people to question religious faith is dismissed as "ridiculous propaganda" and a distraction from administration political pressures.
- Many of the DoD-listed "date taken" fields are January 1 of various years, which Novak argues is likely filler indicating only year-level precision — a metadata caveat for catalog use.
- 27+ video files were released with technical specifications and timestamps; Novak walks through the full set.
