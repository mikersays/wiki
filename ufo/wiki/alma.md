# Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA)

**ALMA** is a submillimeter/millimeter radio interferometer of 66 antennas on the Chajnantor plateau in Chile's Atacama Desert, operated as an international partnership (ESO, the U.S. NSF/NRAO, and Japan's NINS, in cooperation with Chile). Its high spectral sensitivity can distinguish deuterated water (HDO) from ordinary water (H₂O), making it a key instrument for measuring isotopic ratios in comets and other icy bodies.

## Vault relevance

ALMA provided the submillimeter observations used to measure the [[deuterium|deuterium-to-hydrogen]] ratio of the interstellar comet [[3i-atlas]], complementing early gas-emission detections from the MDM Observatory — the first deuterated-water analysis of an [[interstellar-objects|interstellar object]] ([[src-3i-atlas-heavy-water-2026-05]]).
