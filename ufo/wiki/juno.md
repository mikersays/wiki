**Juno** is a [[nasa]] Jupiter orbiter launched in 2011 as part of the New Frontiers Program, managed by [[nasa-jpl]]. Its principal investigator is [[scott-bolton]] of the [[southwest-research-institute]].

## Europa Science

Juno's **Microwave Radiometer (MWR)**, originally designed for Jupiter atmospheric study, was repurposed for icy-moon science. During a September 29, 2022 flyby within ~360 km of [[europa]], the MWR produced the first measurement discriminating between thin- and thick-shell models of Europa's ice crust, estimating the conductive ice shell at ~29 km thick. This result was published in *Nature Astronomy* (Dec 17, 2025).

## Relevance

The Europa ice-shell measurement provides critical pre-arrival context for [[europa-clipper]] (arriving 2030) and [[juice]] (arriving 2031), informing habitability assessments for [[ocean-worlds]] astrobiology.
