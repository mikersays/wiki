# Dragonfly Mission

[[nasa]] rotorcraft mission to Saturn's moon [[titan]]. Selected as the fourth New Frontiers mission. Will carry a TMAH-style wet-chemistry organic-molecule analysis package inheriting heritage from [[curiosity-rover]]'s [[sample-analysis-at-mars|SAM]] instrument ([[src-curiosity-mars-life-molecules-2026-04]]).

Titan's surface has rivers and lakes of liquid methane and ethane and a thick organic-haze atmosphere — a prime target for prebiotic chemistry and astrobiology research. The mission's existence as a funded program (rather than a concept paper) is a direct product of the [[huygens-probe]]'s 2005 surface landing, which converted Titan from theory into geology ([[src-cassini-huygens-ocean-worlds-2026-04]]).
