**BLIPSS — Breakthrough Listen Investigation for Periodic Spectral Signals** — Cornell University + [[seti-institute|SETI Institute]] + [[breakthrough-listen]] collaboration. Lead [[akshay-suresh]] (Cornell graduate student), advisor [[vishal-gajjar]].

## Search design

- **Telescope:** [[green-bank-telescope|Green Bank Telescope (GBT)]].
- **Band:** 4–8 GHz.
- **Target:** [[galactic-center|Galactic Center]] — dense stellar population maximizes line-of-sight count of potentially habitable systems and is a strategic beacon site for a galaxy-spanning civilization.
- **Signal class:** [[periodic-spectral-signal|periodic spectral signals]] — trains of narrowband pulses (sub-FM-channel bandwidth, periodic temporal structure). Distinct from pulsars (broadband) and from classical continuous-wave (CW) narrowband [[technosignatures|SETI]].
- **Validation:** algorithm recovers known pulsars with expected periodicities before being applied to production GBT scans.

## Output

- Paper: Suresh et al., "A 4–8 GHz Galactic Center Search for Periodic Technosignatures," accepted *Astronomical Journal*.
