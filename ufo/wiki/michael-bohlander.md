# Michael Bohlander

Holder of the **Chair in Global Law and SETI Policy at Durham University** (UK) and a career international criminal-law jurist. Bohlander has served as International Co-Investigating Judge in the Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia since 2015 and was an international judge of the Kosovo Specialist Chambers (2017–2022); he was a life-tenured member of the German judiciary from 1991 before joining Durham in 2004. Since 2020 his primary research focus has been **the consequences of contact with extraterrestrial intelligence for human law and society**.

## Contact ethics / international criminal law thesis

At the July 2026 [[src-breaking-the-taboo-uap-contact-symposium-2026-05|"Breaking the Taboo" symposium]] — which he co-organized — Bohlander argues that the use of force commonly described in abduction/contact narratives, by overriding human autonomy, constitutes **violence by the alien intelligence that "can rise in severity to the level of … core crimes under international criminal law."** This applies a jurist's legal-standard framework to experiencer testimony and complements Karin Austin's argument for case-by-case witness-credibility assessment.
