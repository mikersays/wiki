**GEIPAN** (Groupe d'études et d'informations sur les phénomènes aérospatiaux non identifiés) is France's UAP investigation unit, operating since 1977 within the French space agency [[cnes|CNES]]. As of 2026 it has archived approximately **5,300 cases**, of which 2-3% remain unexplained (see [[src-academic-uap-stigma-2026-04]]). GEIPAN is the longest continuously running government UAP program in the world and is cited frequently as a model for institutional UAP investigation in international comparisons.

GEIPAN operates under tri-ministerial oversight: **Economy/Finance**, **Higher Education/Research**, and **Armed Forces**. The office classifies investigated sightings along two axes — abnormality and reliability — and explicitly frames its scope as "not a research group focusing on UFOs, extraterrestrial life or other paranormal phenomena" ([[src-france-national-assembly-uap-conference-2026-05]]).

Per [[src-canada-sky-canada-project-2025-02]], MP [[larry-maguire]] names France and the United States as the two key international partners with which a future Canadian UAP lead would coordinate — placing GEIPAN alongside [[aaro]] as the institutional templates for a Canadian body.

## June 29, 2026 — National Assembly symposium

GEIPAN is a named institutional supporter of the June 29, 2026 [[french-national-assembly|French National Assembly]] symposium on UAP, organized by MP [[arnaud-saint-martin]] (LFI) with co-organizer MP [[pierre-henriet]] (HOR, OPECST vice president) — the first such event in French parliamentary history ([[src-france-national-assembly-uap-conference-2026-05]]).
