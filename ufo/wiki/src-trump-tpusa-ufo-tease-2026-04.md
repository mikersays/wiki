## Summary

Speaking at the [[turning-point-usa]] Action Conference in Phoenix on April 17, 2026, [[donald-trump]] told the crowd the U.S. government will "very, very soon" begin releasing previously secret files on UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena. The remarks reaffirm Trump's February 2026 directive (covered in [[src-uap-trump-disclosure-2026]]) tasking [[pete-hegseth]] and the [[department-of-war]] with identifying and releasing UAP records. White House deputy press secretary [[anna-kelly]] confirmed the tasking on the record. Newsweek frames the tease as occurring amid renewed congressional pressure ([[src-pentagon-uap-deadline-missed-2026-04]]) and after [[barack-obama]]'s February rapid-fire podcast remark that aliens are "real, but I haven't seen them." The piece pairs the political news with [[avi-loeb]]'s commentary that the most probative releases would be high-resolution satellite imagery and recovered physical materials testable for non-solar-system isotopic signatures (echoing [[src-loeb-wh-uap-videos-skeptic-2026-04]]).

## Key Claims

- Trump publicly committed at TPUSA Phoenix on April 17, 2026 to begin file releases "very, very soon." (Trump)
- Trump in February 2026 directed Hegseth and other agencies to start identifying and releasing UFO-related government files. (Newsweek / White House)
- White House deputy press secretary Anna Kelly confirmed Trump "directed the Department of War to begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena, and unidentified flying objects." (Anna Kelly)
- Hegseth told reporters in February he had no timeline but would be in "full compliance" with the executive order. (Hegseth)
- Obama's February 2026 podcast remark to Brian Tyler Cohen ("they're real, but I haven't seen them") helped catalyze public interest; he later clarified he saw no evidence of ET contact during his presidency. (Newsweek)
- Loeb argues the most consequential potential evidence would be high-resolution satellite imagery and recovered physical materials testable for non-solar-system isotopic signatures. (Loeb)
- Astronomers and former defense officials caution that genuine astrobiological interest centers on microbial life, not advanced alien technology, and that prior Pentagon reviews found no ET tech on Earth. (Newsweek synthesis)
