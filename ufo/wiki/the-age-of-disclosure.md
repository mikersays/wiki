# The Age of Disclosure

2025 documentary feature directed by Dan Farah; premiered at SXSW on March 9, 2025. Built around **34 senior U.S. government, military, and intelligence officials** who claim direct knowledge of UAPs and assert an active reverse-engineering arms race against China and Russia.

## Thesis

- An 80-year cover-up of [[non-human-intelligence|non-human intelligent life]] and recovered craft.
- A U.S. effort to [[uap-reverse-engineering|reverse-engineer]] alleged [[crash-retrieval-programs|crash-retrieved]] technology.
- Strategic urgency: lose the race against China/Russia and lose the future.

## Notable on-camera participants

- Sen. [[marco-rubio]] (R-FL)
- Former DNI [[jim-clapper]]
- Sen. [[mike-rounds]] (R-SD)
- [[chris-mellon]] — "This is the biggest discovery in human history."
- [[jay-stratton]], former director of the U.S. Government's UAP Task Force — "I have seen with my own eyes non-human craft and non-human beings."
- [[tim-gallaudet]] (retired RAdm)

## Visual evidence

The film leans on the **2021-declassified Navy footage** (the same blurry water-surface objects already in public circulation) plus on-camera testimony. Critics — notably [[uap-stigma|disclosure-skeptic]] reviewers — have flagged the absence of new clear photographic evidence as a logical problem in a camera-saturated era.

## Reception

- Variety's [Owen Gleiberman gave a skeptical review at SXSW](https://variety.com/2025/film/reviews/the-age-of-disclosure-review-sxsw-1236332637/) (March 9, 2025), framing each era's ET narrative as culturally tailored: 1938 War of the Worlds, 1980s abduction lore, 1990s X-Files, 2020s national-security disclosure ([[src-age-of-disclosure-review-2025-03]]).
- The film has become a touchstone for the disclosure-vs-skeptic divide, fueling the April 2026 [[sean-kirkpatrick]] / [[tim-gallaudet]] public clash ([[src-gallaudet-kirkpatrick-clash-2026-04]]).

## Catholic / theological response

Per [[src-catholic-filmmaker-vatican-ufo-2026-04]] (OSV News, late 2025): Catholic filmmaker [[sam-sorich]]'s production company [[glass-darkly-films]] is shooting a late-2026 documentary as a direct response to the film's claim — sourced to a former CIA official — that the [[vatican]] holds evidence of [[non-human-intelligence]]. The Sorich film features podcaster [[jesse-michels]] and former military chaplain [[matthew-gray]], and frames its task as the living Catholic Magisterium engaging contemporary culture. The [[vatican-observatory]] declined Sorich's interviews.
