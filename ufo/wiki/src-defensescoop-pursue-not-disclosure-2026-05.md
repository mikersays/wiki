# 'Data alone is not disclosure': UAP research community reacts to Trump's first PURSUE file drop

[[brandi-vincent]]'s DefenseScoop wrap-up of UAP-research-community reactions to the May 8, 2026 [[pursue]] Release 01 file drop, framed as a "historic — yet incomplete — step." Six on-record voices — [[chris-mellon]] ([[disclosure-foundation]] chair), [[tim-gallaudet]] ([[sol-foundation]] / [[disclosure-foundation]] / [[americans-for-safe-aerospace]] advisor), [[ryan-graves]] ([[americans-for-safe-aerospace]]), [[jordan-flowers]] ([[disclosure-foundation]]), [[enigma-labs]] consultant **Rojas**, and Australian researcher **Lavac** — broadly judge the ~160-file tranche (100+ redacted) as low-quality and largely previously disseminated, lacking the provenance and sensor context needed for independent analysis. A "War Department official" confirms that [[aaro]] coordinates declassification for posting at war.gov/ufo while continuing its statutory resolution mission on aaro.mil, and that PURSUE drops will continue on a rolling basis with no government interpretation. [[apollo-12]] and [[apollo-17]] imagery, [[us-indo-pacific-command|USINDOPACOM]] and Papua New Guinea reports, and concerns about 1950s [[psyops-disinformation]] artifacts being laundered into the release are flagged as items deserving closer attention.

## Key Claims

- PURSUE Release 01 contained "a little more than 160 files," with at least 100 including redactions.
- Per "War Department official": "AARO is responsible for coordinating the declassification of identified DOW files. AARO also prepares DOW files and files provided by other agencies for posting on war.gov/ufo. AARO will continue to conduct its statutory mission of detecting, identifying, analyzing, and resolving UAP. Any encounters described in these releases that are resolved in the future will be posted on aaro.mil."
- [[chris-mellon]]: the release "puts something important on the record: the government has been collecting data, receiving reports, and conducting analysis on UAP for decades, and in many cases withheld that material from public view. That is no longer deniable." Mellon chairs the [[disclosure-foundation]] board.
- [[enigma-labs]] consultant Rojas is cross-referencing the PURSUE cases against Enigma's >200,000 public sightings database for location/time clusters; PURSUE items being added to Enigma's free-app maps.
- [[tim-gallaudet]] advises [[sol-foundation]], [[disclosure-foundation]], and [[americans-for-safe-aerospace]]; future drops of the classified Pentagon/IC videos/sonar/radar/FLIR he is "aware of" will be the test of "true transparency."
- Australian researcher Lavac: "the story here isn't necessarily the content, but the process of official acknowledgment"; the U.S.–Australia "transparency gap" is "becoming untenable"; unresolved [[us-indo-pacific-command|USINDOPACOM]] and Papua New Guinea reports are standouts indicating UAP near Australian shores.
- [[jordan-flowers]]: 1950s U.S. government UAP disinformation campaigns (covering classified weapons programs) may have produced artifacts now being re-released as authentic UAP records — see [[psyops-disinformation]]; June 2026 [[disclosure-foundation|Disclosure Forum]] will dig into provenance and integrity with legislators, national-security officials, scientists, legal experts, and journalists.
- [[ryan-graves]]: useful releases need "clear provenance, sensor context, metadata, chain of custody, date and location, and enough operational context to allow serious independent analysis"; goal is "a durable, credible reporting and analysis process, not just periodic releases of low-context files"; [[americans-for-safe-aerospace]] is mining PURSUE for sensor data, pilot/aircrew reports, operational-hazard analyses, and stigma-reducing process changes.
- The IC has "almost certainly" already done sophisticated integrated analytical work on this data, "but none of that analytical product has been released" — anonymous former senior defense official.
- Several officials hinted that the majority of the files included media that has already been widely disseminated by NARA, DOD, NASA, or other federal agencies.
- [[apollo-12]] and [[apollo-17]] imagery in the release is flagged as placing UAP-adjacent observations "in a context of the highest-credibility witnesses operating the most sophisticated systems of their era."

## Notable Quotes

> "We view [this initial release] as a meaningful step towards transparency, not because it resolves the major questions, but because it puts something important on the record... That is no longer deniable." — [[chris-mellon]]

> "If any of those are released, we will know whether or not true transparency is the objective." — [[tim-gallaudet]]

> "The story here isn't necessarily the content, but the process of official acknowledgment." — Lavac

> "If some of what is now being released to the public as authentic government UAP records are in fact disinformation artifacts from that era, the public deserves to know." — [[jordan-flowers]]

> "The goal should be a durable, credible reporting and analysis process, not just periodic releases of low-context files." — [[ryan-graves]]

> "We are not providing any comment or assessment on the files overall or on any specific file, so that the American people can make up their own minds regarding the files." — anonymous "War Department official"
