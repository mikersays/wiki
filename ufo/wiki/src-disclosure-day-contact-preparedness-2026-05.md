## Summary

[[space-com]] article by [[leonard-david]] (April 4, 2026) polling six experts on public readiness for UFO/UAP disclosure and extraterrestrial contact. Timed to [[donald-trump]]'s February 2026 UAP file-release directive and [[steven-spielberg]]'s forthcoming [[disclosure-day-film]]. Experts split into two broad camps: those who believe disclosure would be revolutionary but manageable, and those who warn the public may not be ready for all possible truths.

## Key Claims

- [[greg-eghigian]] (Penn State): Every government disclosure effort produces believers and skeptics — "the appetite for disclosure is likely to never be satisfied" (Eghigian)
- Steven Dick (ex-NASA historian, [[galileo-project]] member): UAPs deserve serious research but are logically distinct from ET contact; prefers building new instruments over reviewing old imagery; notes "astrotheology" has emerged as a field (Dick)
- [[avi-loeb]] (Harvard, [[galileo-project]]): Proposes immediate declassification of incidents 50+ years old as a low-risk first step; bureaucratic incentives in intelligence agencies resist disclosure (Loeb)
- Carol Cleland (U. of Colorado Boulder, [[seti-institute]] affiliate): Does not expect disclosure; reactions would range from rejection to religious fervor to indifference (Cleland)
- [[george-knapp]]: Non-ET explanations could be more disturbing than the alien hypothesis — some possibilities "might be truly upsetting to people's religious beliefs" (Knapp)

## Notable Quotes

> "The existence of secrecy means even apparent openness can be suspected of disguising more secrecy. The appetite for disclosure is likely to never be satisfied." — Greg Eghigian

> "One step that can be taken immediately is to declassify events from 50 years ago. Technologies used half a century ago are no longer strategically sensitive." — Avi Loeb

> "I know that the public says we can handle the truth. I'm not entirely sure that's true, because we don't really know what the truth is." — George Knapp
