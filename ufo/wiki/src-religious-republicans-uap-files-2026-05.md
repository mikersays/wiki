## Summary

[[courthouse-news-service|Courthouse News Service]]'s May 15, 2026 reported piece (Benjamin S. Weiss) surveys how conservative Christian House Republicans are squaring their faith with [[pursue]] Release 01. Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]] (R-FL) posted a "biblically accurate" angel — interlocking rings studded with eyes and wings — shortly after the Pentagon dropped the PURSUE files; commenters linked the image to a 2013 Middle East "eight-pointed star" UAP IR clip (cf. [[dow-uap-pr38]]). Luna told Courthouse News in a Capitol-corridor interview that the post was "unrelated." The piece then catalogs explicit Christian-eschatological readings from Rep. [[lauren-boebert]] (Nephilim / "demonic"), a measured rebuttal from Rep. [[eric-burlison]] ("stick to what the Bible really says"), and an open posture from Rep. [[tim-burchett]] invoking Ezekiel's "wheel within a wheel" and Genesis 1:1. The article extends the [[theological-uap-framing]] strand into mainstream legal/political reporting and is the first vault source where Burlison engages the theological frame directly rather than purely as a legislator.

## Key Claims

- Luna posted a "biblically accurate angel" image shortly after the PURSUE files dropped; commenters on X linked it to the 2013 Middle East IR "eight-pointed star" UAP clip ([[dow-uap-pr38]]).
- Luna told Courthouse News outside the Capitol the post was "unrelated" to the [[department-of-war]] release and was merely "a modern-day interpretation" of angelic form.
- Rep. [[lauren-boebert]] (R-CO) said on a podcast that PURSUE documents prompted her to think of Old-Testament "fallen angels and [[nephilim|Nephilim]]" and that the phenomenon is "more spiritual, and, if you really want to go there, demonic."
- Rep. [[eric-burlison]] (R-MO) did not dismiss Boebert's framing but advised sticking to scripture; he speculated Old-Testament writers may have "seen things" much like modern UAP encounters and said briefings he has received "fit" the "Christian worldview." Maintained his own approach is "more secular" and truth-seeking.
- Rep. [[tim-burchett]] (R-TN) said "I don't have a problem with" colleagues' biblical interpretations and cited the Book of Ezekiel's "wheel within a wheel… like a gyroscope" and Genesis 1:1: "I think we try to confine God into some Americanized version and he's a lot bigger than that."
- PURSUE is a rolling release; the Pentagon pledged additional tranches and separate reporting on "resolved" cases per [[donald-trump]]'s directive.
