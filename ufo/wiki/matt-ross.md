**Matt Ross** is a U.S. Army Brigadier General serving as director of [[jiatf-401]] as of April 2026. He has framed the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]] as a tool for scaling the C-UAS industrial base "from the tactical edge to critical infrastructure protection in our homeland and for our allies and partners," in announcing [[romania]]'s entry to the marketplace (see [[src-jiatf-401-marketplace-romania-2026-04]]).

## January 2026 — earliest on-record JIATF-401 quotes

Ross's first set of public framing statements appear in the DoW January 26, 2026 press release on the SecWar-signed homeland C-UAS guidance ([[src-jiatf-401-counter-drone-homeland-guidance-2026-01]]):

> "Drones are a defining threat for our time. Technology is evolving fast and our policies and c-UAS strategy here at home must adapt to meet this reality."

> "Countering drones does not start and stop at the fence line. With this new guidance installation commanders are empowered to address threats as they develop."

> "Hardware alone is not sufficient. We need to train service members and work in concert with interagency partners."

## May 2026 directed-energy 5-base pilot

Per [[src-directed-energy-5-bases-pilot-2026-05]], Ross announced on May 7 2026 a Pentagon pilot to deploy [[directed-energy-weapons]] (high-energy lasers and high-powered microwaves) at five U.S. bases within 180 days. He framed the pilot as "no silver bullet" — DEW is one element of a layered defense, not a replacement for existing kinetic and RF C-UAS:

> "Countering unlawful and adversarial drone activity is a homeland defense imperative. There is no 'silver bullet' to address this challenge, and this pilot program integrates cutting-edge technology into the department's broader counterdrone toolkit."
