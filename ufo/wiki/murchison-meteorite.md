# Murchison meteorite

Carbonaceous chondrite that fell near Murchison, Victoria, Australia on 28 September 1969. One of the most-studied meteorites in astrobiology because of its rich and well-preserved prebiotic organic inventory, including amino acids, nucleobases, and [[hexamethylenetetramine]].

## Astrobiology relevance

Murchison is the canonical case for [[exogenous-organics|exogenous organic delivery]] to early Earth. Its analyses have repeatedly been used to anchor "in-situ vs. exogenous" debates over the origin of life. [[amy-williams]] cites Murchison alongside [[bennu-asteroid|Bennu]] in the TIME writeup ([[src-mars-organics-fresh-clues-2026-04]]) of the [[curiosity-rover|Curiosity]] [[sample-analysis-at-mars|SAM]]/TMAH organics result.
