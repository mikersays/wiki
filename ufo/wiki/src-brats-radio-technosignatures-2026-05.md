## Summary

Astrobiology.com (May 13 2026) summarizes a new arXiv preprint (2605.10212) by [[michael-a-garrett]] proposing **BRaTs** — Broadband Radio Technosignatures — as a complement to traditional narrowband [[seti]]. Garrett argues terrestrial technology is shifting toward distributed, low-power, wideband digital infrastructure, so narrowband-only surveys risk discarding the aggregate leakage of advanced civilizations as unstructured noise. A tiered framework uses wide-field next-gen surveys ([[ska]] and precursors) for initial detection and [[vlbi|Very Long Baseline Interferometry]] for high-resolution follow-up. Because broadband continuum emission is largely insensitive to Doppler drift, long "SETI Deep Fields" become viable, extending [[kardashev-scale|Kardashev Type I]] leakage detection volume to ~100 pc. Candidates are isolated via a multi-parameter diagnostic: high brightness temperature, negligible circular polarization, spectral non-uniformity, interstellar scintillation, and sub-milliarcsecond astrometric co-motion with nearby Galactic stars/exoplanets.

## Key Claims

- The modern human RF environment is moving from narrowband beacons toward distributed wideband digital leakage; SETI's narrowband bias may be systematically discarding the actual signature of comparable civilizations.
- A hierarchical observational strategy — wide-field arrays ([[ska]] and precursors) first, then [[vlbi]] follow-up — can isolate planetary-scale broadband leakage.
- Broadband continuum emission is largely **Doppler-drift insensitive**, enabling long-integration "SETI Deep Fields" not viable for narrowband searches.
- Detection volume for [[kardashev-scale|Kardashev Type I]] leakage extends to **~100 pc** under this framework.
- Multi-parameter discriminator vs astrophysical confounders: high **brightness temperature**, negligible **circular polarization**, **spectral non-uniformity**, **interstellar scintillation**, and **sub-milliarcsecond astrometric co-motion** with nearby stars/exoplanets.
- Complementary to — not replacing — narrowband SETI; addresses a different signal class (unintentional leakage vs intentional beacons).

## Notable Quotes

> "The strict adherence to narrowband filtering that characterises most SETI surveys, therefore, risks discarding the aggregate leakage signatures of advanced civilisations by systematically misclassifying them as unstructured noise." — Garrett et al.
