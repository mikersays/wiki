# PURSUE Portal Launch (war.gov/UFO)

## Summary

On May 8, 2026, the [[department-of-war]] launched the public-facing [[pursue]] portal at `war.gov/UFO`. The portal operationalizes [[donald-trump]]'s February 19, 2026 Truth Social directive ([[src-uap-trump-disclosure-2026]]) and is overseen by DoW with [[odni]] support. The portal is structured around rolling tranches of declassified, **unresolved** UAP cases, supplementing — not replacing — [[aaro]]'s statutory work on resolved cases. **Release 01**, "CLEARED FOR RELEASE — MAY 8, 2026," is introduced by a [[pete-hegseth]] statement and contains roughly 170 records (page 1 of 17, ~10 records per page). Page 1 is composed entirely of [[fbi]] PDFs from a single file series `65_HS1-834228961_62-HQ-83894` (sections 2–7, 9, 10; serials 130, 153) — see [[fbi-case-file-62-hq-83894]]. The page opens with a 17-image evidence carousel of infrared stills and recreations (2020–2026) including an [[apollo-17]] archival lunar image. The page was retrieved via Wayback Machine after war.gov returned HTTP 403.

## Key Claims

- PURSUE expands as **P**residential **U**nsealing and **R**eporting **S**ystem for UAP **E**ncounters; hosted at `war.gov/UFO`.
- DoW with [[odni]] support is conducting a government-wide effort spanning "tens of millions of records, many existing only on paper."
- New tranches will post "every few weeks" on a rolling basis.
- PURSUE handles **unresolved** cases; [[aaro]] retains statutory responsibility for resolved-case reporting.
- Carousel images include cases from western U.S. (Sept and Dec 2025), UAE, Greece, African airspace, a 2020 USAF southern-U.S. case, an INDOPACOM "football-shaped" object near Japan, a 2026 U.S. Army case, a 2023 southeastern-U.S. recreation, and an Apollo 17 archival image with three lights enlarged above the lunar terrain.
- "Learn More" links externally only to `aaro.mil`.
- The page does not include a public sighting-submission form, despite the "Reporting" element of the PURSUE acronym.
