## Summary

Astrobiology.com summary of a technology-development paper (arXiv:2604.23126) presenting simulations of **[[peepss|PEEPSS — Photonic-Enabled ExoPlanet Spectroscopic Sensor]]**, an integrated [[photonic-lantern]]-fed spectrograph concept for NASA's [[habitable-worlds-observatory|Habitable Worlds Observatory]] (HWO). HWO must achieve ~10⁻¹⁰ star/planet contrast, which demands extreme wavefront stability across the [[direct-imaging|coronagraph]] system. PEEPSS uses photonic lanterns to couple light from the coronagraph "dark hole" (where exoplanets sit) into single-mode fibers feeding the main spectrograph, while simultaneously using rejected host-star light from inside the dark hole for **focal-plane wavefront sensing**. Combining the science channel and wavefront sensor in one photonic system eliminates **non-common-path errors** between sensing and science arms. The concept is especially valuable in the **near-infrared (NIR)**, where the diffraction-limited inner working angle (IWA ∝ λ/D) otherwise grows beyond habitable-zone separations; PEEPSS pushes the achievable IWA inward, expanding HWO's accessible parameter space for habitable-zone exoplanet imaging and spectroscopic [[biosignatures|biosignature]] characterization.

## Key Claims

- HWO requires ~10⁻¹⁰ contrast — requires outstanding wavefront measurement and control through the telescope + coronagraph chain.
- PEEPSS uses a set of **photonic lanterns** to couple coronagraph dark-hole light efficiently into single-mode fibers and the main spectrograph.
- Rejected starlight interior to the dark hole feeds the **focal-plane wavefront sensor**, eliminating non-common-path errors between sensing and science channels.
- One photonic system combines the science (spectroscopy) and wavefront-sensing channels.
- Particularly advantageous in the **near-infrared** bandpass, "of particular interest for HWO."
- Coronagraph IWA scales as λ/D, so NIR imaging normally suffers larger IWAs than visible — PEEPSS reduces the achievable NIR IWA, increasing accessible exoplanet parameter space.
