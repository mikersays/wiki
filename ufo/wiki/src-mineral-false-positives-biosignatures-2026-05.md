## Summary

Parkinson, [[lisa-kaltenegger]], Biller, Lach, and McMahon (Cornell [[carl-sagan-institute]] / [[university-of-edinburgh]]) systematically search existing mineral reflectance databases for non-biological analogues of the **[[photosynthesis-red-edge|photosynthesis red edge]]** (PRE, also "vegetation red edge" / VRE) — the sharp ~700 nm reflectance step seen in Earth's disk-integrated spectrum that is the canonical surface-pigment [[biosignatures|biosignature]] target for the [[habitable-worlds-observatory]]. They find that several **sulfide minerals**, **tectosilicate minerals**, and the prebiotically important cyanide salt **potassium ferrocyanide** produce PRE-like reflectance steps, expanding the [[false-positive-biosignatures]] taxonomy from gas-phase abiotic O₂ ([[src-oxygen-false-positive-biosignatures-2026-03]]) to **surface mineralogy**. The authors characterize each false-positive feature and argue that distinguishing biopigments from mineral mimics will require **atmospheric context** plus an explicit **Bayesian prior** comparing biological vs non-biological surface hypotheses. The paper directly affects HWO and other visible-to-NIR [[direct-imaging]] missions.

## Key Claims

- The **photosynthesis / vegetation red edge** (PRE/VRE) is a sharp step-like reflectance increase near ~700 nm, detected in Earth's spectrum by the Galileo flyby and in Earthshine observations.
- PRE-like reflectance features have been a leading surface-biosignature target for future direct-imaging missions including the [[habitable-worlds-observatory]].
- Until now, no systematic survey existed of how often abiotic minerals mimic this feature.
- Sulfide minerals, tectosilicate minerals, and potassium ferrocyanide (a prebiotically relevant cyanide salt) all show PRE-like reflectance edges in existing mineral spectral databases.
- Biogenicity of any PRE-like feature on an exoplanet must be evaluated in **atmospheric context** rather than from the surface spectrum alone.
- Confirming a surface biopigment may ultimately require a **prior-probability assessment** (Bayesian framing) of biological vs non-biological surface materials.
- Implicates the visible-to-near-infrared spectral regime targeted by HWO-class disk-integrated reflectance spectroscopy.

## Notable Quotes

> "We find that several sulfide and tectosilicate minerals, as well as the prebiotically important cyanide salt, potassium ferrocyanide, have PRE-like features." — paper abstract

> "The future evaluation of the biogenicity of PRE-like features in exoplanet reflectance spectra can be informed by the atmospheric context, but may require an assessment of the prior probability of non-biological and biological hypotheses about the surface materials of exoplanets." — paper abstract
