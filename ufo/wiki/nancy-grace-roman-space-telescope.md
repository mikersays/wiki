The **Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope** is a NASA wide-field infrared observatory scheduled to launch in **2027**. Its coronagraph instrument is designed to pioneer space-based wavefront control for direct imaging of Jupiter-sized reflected-light exoplanets — a methodology category covered in [[direct-imaging]].

Roman is a key part of the follow-up path for the [[jwst]] exoplanet candidate around [[alpha-centauri-a]] ([[src-alpha-centauri-a-exoplanet-2026]]) and the cold gas giant [[epsilon-indi-ab]], whose [[water-ice-clouds]] inferred from JWST mid-IR retrievals ([[src-jwst-ice-clouds-exoplanet-2026-04]]) are directly observable in reflected light. Stepping stone toward the [[habitable-worlds-observatory]].
