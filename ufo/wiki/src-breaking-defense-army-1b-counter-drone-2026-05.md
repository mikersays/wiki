## Summary

[[breaking-defense|Breaking Defense]] ([[carley-welch]], May 15 2026) breaks down the Army's **FY27 budget request for small counter-UAS (sUAS)** procurement: **$994M discretionary** — nearly double the **$596M** enacted in FY26 ($336M discretionary + $260M reconciliation/mandatory). Sits under DoD's $1.5T FY27 topline. Budget justification books frame the spend as a **"systems of systems"** architecture spanning expeditionary/mobile platforms, sensors, effectors, EW, and an interoperable fire-control layer. Eight line items are itemized, with the largest being **operational sUAS** ($414M) and the most specific including the **[[next-generation-cuas-missile|Next Generation cUAS Missile (NGCM / Freedom Eagle-1)]]** by [[aerovironment]], **two [[enduring-high-energy-laser|Enduring High Energy Laser (E-HEL)]]** systems under a $66M directed-energy line, and **800 kinetic + 29 non-kinetic** effectors. Complements rather than overlaps the [[src-anduril-army-20b-counter-drone-idiq-2026-03|$20B Anduril Lattice IDIQ]] (a C2 contract ceiling) and the [[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04|JIATF-401 marketplace]] (procurement channel) by quantifying actual FY27 dollars at the small end (Group 1/2) of the threat spectrum. Also confirms continued procurement under the legacy [[family-of-counter-uas|Family of Counter Unmanned Aircraft Systems (FoCUS)]] program with $5M for ground readiness/logistics. Reiterates the [[merops-interceptor|cost-curve]] framing: [[shahed-drone|Shahed]] at ~$4k vs PAC-3 MSE at $3–4M.

## Key Claims

- **FY27 request: $994M** for small cUAS procurement; **FY26 enacted: $596M** ($336M discretionary + $260M reconciliation). Roughly doubles the discretionary line.
- Architecture is **"systems of systems"** — expeditionary/mobile platforms, sensors, effectors, EW components, interoperable fire-control.
- **Eight FY27 line items:**
  - **$414M — Operational sUAS:** c-UAS batteries, expeditionary mobile platforms and sensors.
  - **$165M — Fixed:** homeland/installation defense for joint force + Army; sUAS logistics; tied to ongoing DoD–[[faa]] friction over base-defense authorities.
  - **$132M — Effectors:** 800 kinetic, 29 non-kinetic, and **24 NGCM** units. NGCM = Next Generation cUAS Missile, aka **Freedom Eagle-1**, made by [[aerovironment]].
  - **$108M — Squad / individual soldier:** dismounted, handheld, wearable systems vs Group 1/2 UAS.
  - **$80M — Brigade-and-below:** Group 1/2 sUAS defense.
  - **$66M — Directed energy:** two [[enduring-high-energy-laser|E-HEL]] systems; rapid prototyping; interoperable with **Integrated Battle Command System-Maneuver (IBCS-M)**.
  - **$24M — Launchers:** 12 expeditionary + 10 mobile launchers for one c-UAS battery.
  - **~$5M — sUAS ground readiness:** logistics for the [[family-of-counter-uas|FoCUS]] program.
- Driver framing: cheap FPVs killing multi-million-dollar tanks in Ukraine; ~$4k Shahed vs $3–4M PAC-3 MSE in [[operation-epic-fury]].
- No direct quotations — all attribution is to Army FY27 budget justification books.
