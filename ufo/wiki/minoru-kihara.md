**Minoru Kihara** is a Japanese cabinet official. As **Defense Minister in 2024**, the Japanese Defense Ministry under his leadership shifted posture to treat **unidentified aerial objects as security concerns**, emphasizing the need to analyze and respond to incursions in Japanese airspace — a significant doctrinal change in [[japan]]'s UAP policy.

This shift preceded [[yoshiharu-asakawa]]'s May 2025 proposal and the [[japan-uap-parliamentary-group]]'s March 2026 proposal ([[src-japan-uap-strategy-proposal-2026-03]]).

## Chief Cabinet Secretary (2026)

By May 2026, Kihara was serving as **Chief Cabinet Secretary**. On **May 13, 2026** he confirmed at a Tokyo press conference that he had personally viewed the [[pursue|PURSUE Release 01]] materials released by the U.S. [[department-of-war]] on May 8, and that Japan was reviewing the 161 files with U.S. and partner governments ([[src-japan-pentagon-uap-trove-reaction-2026-05]]). He cited the 2023 [[us-indo-pacific-command|INDOPACOM]] [[dow-uap-pr47]] infrared clip and a 2024 "football-shaped" object near Japanese airspace, and said any release of Japanese-held UAP footage would be evaluated case-by-case to protect intelligence sources.
