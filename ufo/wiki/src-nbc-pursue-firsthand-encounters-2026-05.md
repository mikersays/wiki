## Summary

[[nasa]] / [[odni]]-sourced first-hand testimony anchors this NBC News feature (Laura Strickler, Colleen Long, Justin Goldman, Mosheh Gains; May 22 2026) on [[pursue|PURSUE]] **Release 02**, which foregrounds **human witness accounts** rather than the file-inventory or skeptic angles covered elsewhere ([[src-dow-pursue-release-02-2026-05]], [[src-newsweek-pursue-release-02-2026-05]], [[src-pentagon-second-batch-ufo-release-2026-05]]). It leads with a late-2025 [[odni|ODNI]] memo from a senior U.S. intelligence officer whose helicopter, investigating "thuds" near a mountain range, met an orange orb that split in two, rose from the ground to within ~10 ft, dropped below, and sped away — then "chased" called-in fighter jets, matching their speed and flight path ("We were virtually speechless"). It threads in a 1970 [[los-alamos|Los Alamos]] scientist's handwritten letter on green lights seen 1948–1951, a 1972 [[cia|CIA]] officer's concentric-green-circles sighting in the USSR, the 2023 [[lake-huron-shootdown-2023|Lake Huron]] shootdown, and NASA audio including [[scott-carpenter|Scott Carpenter]]'s 1962 [[project-mercury|Project Mercury]] "snowflakes" and the [[apollo-12|Apollo 12]] (1969) crew. NBC stresses the government again drew no conclusions and attached its standard non-determination disclaimer.

## Key Claims

- A senior U.S. intelligence officer's [[odni|ODNI]] memo (late 2025) describes a helicopter "thuds" investigation near a mountain range: an orange orb split in two, changed direction, rose from the ground to within ~10 ft of the helicopter, dropped below, and sped away (per the officer's memo).
- Fighter jets were called in; the orbs matched the jets' speed and flight path and appeared to "chase" them; the officer filed a debrief that night (officer's notes).
- The documents are a second batch of declassified UAP files released Friday by the Trump administration; mostly [[department-of-war|Defense Department]] material plus **seven NASA files and one CIA file**, spanning the 1940s through six months ago, redacted to protect witnesses/locations/sensitive info (NBC).
- 1970 handwritten letter: a [[los-alamos|Los Alamos]] scientist reports regularly seeing green lights 9–11 p.m. in the mountains during 1948–1951, once "moving in some kind of formation" (per the letter).
- 1972: a [[cia|CIA]] officer in the USSR, after watching a Canada–USSR sports competition, saw a green circle widen into concentric circles (per the file).
- Released videos show spherical, oblong and cigar-shaped objects over land, lake and sea — including a Nov 2020 circular object over the central U.S., objects over Kabul, Syria and Kazakhstan (a burst of oval white light near Karaganda International Airport), an object near a surfaced submarine, and a fast-moving object **downed over [[lake-huron-shootdown-2023|Lake Huron]] in 2023** (recovery status unclear from the footage).
- NASA audio: [[scott-carpenter|Scott Carpenter]], during three Earth revolutions in [[project-mercury|Project Mercury]] (1962), describes randomly moving particles "exactly like snowflakes"; the [[apollo-12|Apollo 12]] crew (1969) also describe odd things seen aboard.
- The government drew no conclusions on the UAPs' origins and attached a disclaimer that nothing should be read "as reflecting an analytical judgment, investigative conclusion, or factual determination" (NBC / disclaimer text).

## Notable Quotes

> "The ground team suddenly radioed that the object had risen from the ground, approached within ten feet of the helicopter, dropped below us and sped away." — senior U.S. intelligence officer, ODNI memo

> "We were virtually speechless after these observations." — senior U.S. intelligence officer

> "Their motion is random. They look exactly like snowflakes to me." — Scott Carpenter, Project Mercury (1962)
