# Chain of Custody

**Chain of custody** is the documented sequence of possession, transfer, and analysis of a physical sample from its discovery through every laboratory result. Without it, a claimed analytical result cannot be reliably tied to a specific object, leaving contamination, substitution, or post-discovery introduction of materials as unresolved confounds.

## Standard elements

- Discovery context (location, depth, stratigraphy, witnesses).
- A continuous custody log from recovery through every transfer and analysis.
- Sample-prep methodology, named handlers, and the lab(s) involved.
- Independent laboratory identification and audit trail.

## Relevance to UAP artifact claims

Most publicly promoted "recovered" UAP artifacts lack documented chain of custody. This is a recurring failure mode in the [[jaime-maussan]] / [[steven-greer]] / [[the-disclosure-project]] artifact arc:

- The [[buga-sphere]] was held privately for ~2 months at [[germany-company|Germany Company]] in Cali by [[david-velez-el-potro|David Vélez]] before transfer to Maussan; export to Mexico was unrestricted and undocumented ([[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]]).
- The [[valdias-sphere]] was carried out of the Colombian jungle in a backpack by [[william-zuniga|William Zúñiga]] and handed directly to Maussan on television ([[src-buga-sphere-maussan-tv-valdias-report-2026-05]]).
- The [[nazca-mummies]] had no documented recovery context prior to Maussan's 2023 Mexican Chamber of Deputies presentation.
- The Atacama "humanoid" (later identified as a human fetus with skeletal abnormalities) was likewise presented with no archaeological provenance — see [[steven-greer]].

Together with [[radiocarbon-dating]], chain-of-custody absence is one of the two principal evidentiary gaps cited by Colombian astronomers ([[santiago-vargas]], [[marino-guarin]], [[oscar-amaya-montoya]], [[lauren-flor-torres]]) and international skeptics ([[julia-mossbridge]], Astrophyzix) in their rejection of the [[buga-sphere]] case.
