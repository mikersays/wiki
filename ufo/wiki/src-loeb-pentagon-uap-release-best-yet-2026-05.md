## Summary

Medium essay by [[avi-loeb]] (May 10, 2026) analyzing the May 8, 2026 [[pursue|PURSUE Release 01]] of UAP files. Loeb enumerates the **161 records** released — 82 from the [[department-of-war]], 56 from the [[fbi]], 12 from [[nasa]], 8 from the [[department-of-state]], 28 videos, and 14 images — and frames the launch as primarily **psychological**: legitimizing UAP as mainstream scientific and security discourse. He stresses that **"the best is yet to come,"** because higher-quality data requires longer bureaucratic vetting, and notes that the 46 [[anna-paulina-luna]]-requested videos ([[src-luna-46-uap-videos-demand-2026-03]]) are still pending. After review by his [[galileo-project]] research team, Loeb concludes that **none of the released items requires an exotic origin** — images resolve as optical reflections or human-made objects, video details are redacted, and [[apollo-12|Apollo]] [[apollo-17|lunar lights]] are likely asteroid-impact flashes or optical artifacts ([[lunar-uap-historical-claims]]). He highlights a **1947 letter to the Air Force chief of staff** on flying discs as evidence that serious officials have documented unidentified objects "across agencies, across decades, across the world."

## Key Claims

- **PURSUE Release 01 totals: 161 records on May 8, 2026** — 82 [[department-of-war]], 56 [[fbi]], 12 [[nasa]], 8 [[department-of-state]], 28 video records, 14 image records. (Note: variance with the 162-file count reported elsewhere; see [[pursue-release-01-catalog]].)
- The **46 UAP videos demanded by [[anna-paulina-luna]]** are not in this tranche and "will likely be released in future batches."
- **Best is yet to come:** higher-quality data requires more vetting; subsequent installments follow in coming weeks.
- **Null on exotic origin:** the [[galileo-project]] research team found no released object "sufficiently extraordinary to require an exotic origin."
  - Images explainable as **camera-optics reflections or human-made objects**.
  - Video details "unfortunately redacted."
  - **Apollo lights above the lunar surface** plausibly **asteroid-impact flashes** (per Artemis II reports) or **optical artifacts** ([[lunar-uap-historical-claims]], [[apollo-12]], [[apollo-17]]).
- A **1947 letter to the Air Force chief of staff** discussing flying discs is among the released files.
- **Biggest impact is psychological** — legitimizing UAP as mainstream scientific/security discourse.
- [[galileo-project|Galileo Project]] relevance: civilian [[uap-triangulation]] + AI can independently resolve distance/velocity/acceleration; annual defense budget is ~10⁶× the Galileo Project budget.
- Invokes **Fermi's 1950 "Where is everybody?"** as a scientific (not philosophical) question; probes could plausibly already be in the cosmic backyard given star-formation timelines.
- [[donald-trump|President Trump]] said **"Have fun and enjoy!"** about the release; Loeb did exactly that across ~two dozen TV interviews, including Newsmax.
