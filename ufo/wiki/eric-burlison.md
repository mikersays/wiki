**Rep. Eric Burlison** (R-MO) is a leading UAP legislative figure in the 119th Congress, serving on the House Oversight Committee alongside [[anna-paulina-luna]] and [[tim-burchett]].

## Key Actions

- Introduced the **UAP Disclosure Act of 2025** as an NDAA amendment on August 29, 2025, seeking government record preservation, National Archives access, and an independent declassification board for UAP records.
- At the September 9, 2025 House hearing ([[src-uap-transparency-hearing-2026-04]]), publicly released for the first time footage of an MQ-9 firing a Hellfire missile at a high-speed orb off Yemen (October 2024). The object was struck but continued on course, apparently intact.
- Co-drafted UAP transparency legislation with Rep. Luna and Rep. Burchett.
- **January 2026 (Varginha 30th-anniversary press conference):** Attended [[james-fox]]'s UAP (UFO) Press Conference marking the 30th anniversary of the [[varginha-incident]]; publicly framed his legislative push as "marching orders" from witness testimony and called for passage of the [[uap-disclosure-act]] and either bundled or standalone [[uap-whistleblower-protections]]. Said he remains "skeptical until I get my hands on physical evidence" but is "going to take it seriously investigating myself" ([[src-burlison-marching-orders-2026-01]]).
- **June 2025:** participated in a U.S. press conference with [[steven-greer]] and [[jaime-maussan]] introducing the [[buga-sphere|Buga Sphere]] to U.S. audiences (per [[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]]). The event generated coverage but produced no verifiable evidence; the Buga claims have since been rebutted by [[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]] and rejected by named Colombian astronomers.
- **April 2026:** referred the May 2024 death of UAP whistleblower [[sullivan-whistleblower|Sullivan]] (Bronze Star intelligence officer, [[nasic]]/[[nsa]]) to the [[fbi]] as "sudden and suspicious," days before he was scheduled to testify on [[crash-retrieval-programs]] ([[src-sullivan-whistleblower-death-2026-04]]). Adds a whistleblower-safety advocacy dimension to his prior legislative profile.

## May 8, 2026 PURSUE Reaction

After the [[pursue]] launch, Burlison posted on X: "**This is a historic first step… Thank you for taking on the swamp to make this release happen. You are the president of the people!**" The Washington Examiner ([[src-war-department-uap-declassification-2026-05]]) framed Burlison and [[tim-burchett]] as the named congressional beneficiaries of the rollout, omitting [[anna-paulina-luna]].

His office issued a **formal House press release** the same day ([[src-burlison-commends-trump-uap-release-2026-05]]) titled "Burlison Commends President Trump's Historic Release of UAP Files," elevating the X-post messaging to official congressional-office language. Notable elements:

- Confirms the official PURSUE acronym expansion and lists interagency participants verbatim — White House, [[odni]], [[department-of-energy]], [[department-of-war]] (housing [[aaro]]), [[nasa]], [[fbi]], "additional components of U.S. intelligence agencies."
- Codifies the "disclosure president" frame in formal venue: "He is the only president willing to stand up to the deep state and push this information out to the American people. Trump has been the disclosure president!"
- Cites "years of classified briefings, whistleblower interviews, and oversight work" as personal background.
- Newsweek's May 8 coverage ([[src-newsweek-luna-teases-disclosure-2026-05]]) attributes a parallel "historic first step in what I trust will be just one of many" line to Burlison.
