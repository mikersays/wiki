## Summary

Connecticut state Rep. [[joe-hoxha]] (R-Bristol) introduced **H.B. 5422**, a bill directing the University of Connecticut to partner with state agencies to study UAP and explore establishing a dedicated state research center. The Appropriations Committee heard testimony on March 12, 2026. The bill has bipartisan support, co-sponsored by Rep. [[aundre-bumgardner]] (D-Groton), who cited national-security concerns tied to his district's submarine-building role. Connecticut would become the second U.S. state to legislate UAP study, following New Jersey, which enacted a similar higher-education UAP center law effective January 2026. A Yale PhD student, Sri Tata, testified in favor, citing the 2024 New Jersey drone wave as a catalyst for academic interest. [[aaro]]'s 2024 report finding no evidence of alien technology is cited as background.

## Key Claims

- H.B. 5422 would direct UConn to partner with state agencies to study UAP and explore a dedicated state center (bill text)
- New Jersey passed a law establishing a UAP research center in collaboration with higher education institutions, effective January 2026 (article background)
- The 2024 New Jersey drone wave drove renewed academic and public attention to UAP (Sri Tata)
- A committee member stated he has a colleague who investigates UAPs for the DoD, likely referencing [[aaro]] (Rep. Foncello)
