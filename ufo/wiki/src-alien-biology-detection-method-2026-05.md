## Summary

UC Riverside News reports on a *Nature Astronomy* study by Gideon Yoffe ([[weizmann-institute-of-science]]) and [[fabian-klenner]] ([[uc-riverside]]) proposing a statistical [[agnostic-biosignature]] method that classifies samples as biological or abiotic by measuring the **richness** and **evenness** of [[amino-acids|amino-acid]] and [[fatty-acids|fatty-acid]] distributions. Across ~100 datasets covering microbes, soils, fossils, meteorites ([[bennu-asteroid]], Murchison), asteroids, and synthetic samples, amino acids were more diverse and evenly distributed in biological material while fatty acids showed the inverse pattern. The framework reuses ecological biodiversity statistics, requires no specialized instrument (instruments aboard current and planned missions suffice), and reportedly captured both life/non-life distinction and degrees of preservation — fossilized dinosaur eggshells retained the signature. The authors stress that no single method is decisive; multiple independent lines of evidence remain necessary. Complements the ELSI population-statistics method ([[src-panspermia-agnostic-biosignature-2026-04]]) and the surface-flux-inference approach ([[src-biosignature-gas-flux-inference-2026-04]]).

## Key Claims

- Biological amino-acid pools are more diverse and more evenly distributed than abiotic ones; fatty-acid pattern reverses (Yoffe & Klenner et al., *Nature Astronomy*).
- ~100 existing datasets (microbes, soils, fossils, meteorites, asteroids, lab synth) tested; classification was reliable across the board (Klenner).
- Method works on data from instruments already aboard current/planned missions — no new hardware required.
- Degraded biological samples (e.g., fossilized dinosaur eggshells) retain detectable statistical signature.
- "Astrobiology is fundamentally a forensic science" — inference from incomplete clues with rare, costly data (Yoffe).
- No single test will prove ET life; multiple independent lines in geological/chemical context required (Klenner).

## Notable Quotes

> "Life does not only produce molecules. Life also produces an organizational principle that we can see by applying statistics." — Fabian Klenner

> "Astrobiology is fundamentally a forensic science. We're trying to infer processes from incomplete clues, often with very limited data collected by missions that are extraordinarily expensive and infrequent." — Gideon Yoffe
