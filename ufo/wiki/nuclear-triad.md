The **U.S. nuclear triad** is the three-legged strategic nuclear deterrent architecture:

| Leg | Platform | Key Base(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Land-based ICBMs | Minuteman III | [[malmstrom-afb]], [[minot-afb]], F.E. Warren AFB |
| Submarine-launched ballistic missiles (SLBMs) | Trident II D5 | Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay, Naval Base Kitsap |
| Strategic bombers | B-52H, B-2 Spirit | [[barksdale-afb]], [[whiteman-afb]] |

Command of the triad runs through [[us-stratcom]] at [[offutt-afb]].

## Intelligence Targeting (2023–2026)

All three legs of the nuclear triad have been subject to documented Chinese intelligence collection:

- **[[malmstrom-afb]] (2023)**: Chinese spy balloon loitered 24–48 hours over 150 Minuteman III ICBM silos; reportedly maneuvered via Iridium satellite and exfiltrated data through U.S. ISPs. A 75-page exploitation report was prepared but not publicly released.
- **[[malmstrom-afb]] (2024–2026)**: Multiple unidentified drone swarm incidents over missile silos and launch control centers; no drones captured or launch points identified.
- **[[barksdale-afb]] (March 2026)**: Multi-night drone campaign targeting B-52 nuclear weapons storage areas; single drone breached perimeter March 8; 12–15 drones in four-hour waves March 9. See [[src-drone-incursions-epic-fury-2026-03]] and [[src-spy-drones-nuclear-triad-2026-04]].
- **[[offutt-afb]] (March 2026)**: Chinese student Tianrui Liang photographed aircraft at STRATCOM HQ; arrested at JFK April 9, 2026.

According to [[stephen-bryen]]'s analysis, U.S. nuclear bases lack meaningful air defenses and no drones have been shot down or traced to their operators.

## Connection to UAP

The [[nuclear-uap-correlation]] pattern — documented UAP activity near nuclear weapons sites — predates the current Chinese intelligence focus by decades, raising distinct but related questions about nuclear-site security. [[robert-salas]] claims a 1967 UAP incident at Malmstrom disabled multiple Minuteman ICBMs.
