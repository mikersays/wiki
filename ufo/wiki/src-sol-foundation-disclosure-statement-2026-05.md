## Summary

Joint press statement from [[sol-foundation]] leaders — [[tim-gallaudet]], Garry Nolan, and Peter Skafish — welcoming the May 8 [[pursue]] Release 01 document drop while calling it only a first step toward full disclosure. The statement credits the bipartisan UAP Caucus and UAP whistleblowers, notes that the Sol Foundation recently proposed new whistleblower-protection legislation, and urges Congress to pass the [[uap-disclosure-act]] co-sponsored by [[chuck-schumer]] and [[mike-rounds]]. The authors frame some UAP as "highly likely to be nonanthropogenic vehicles" and describe PURSUE records as potentially providing "further verification that humanity is not alone."

## Key Claims

- The U.S. government "possesses significant intelligence data and knowledge" about UAP, including objects "highly likely to be nonanthropogenic vehicles" (attributed to Gallaudet, Nolan, Skafish jointly)
- Congressional hearings, [[odni]], the [[department-of-war]], and *The New York Times* have confirmed military UAP encounters via advanced radar and "space-based intelligence sensors" (attributed to the statement)
- The [[pursue]] Release 01 documents "may provide further verification that humanity is not alone" (attributed to the statement)
- The Sol Foundation "recently proposed new legislation to support whistleblowers" (attributed to the statement)
- The [[uap-disclosure-act]] "faced unwarranted opposition over the last three years" and should be passed (attributed to the statement)

## Notable Quotes

> "Today's historic document release builds on growing public acknowledgements from national security, intelligence, and scientific experts that the United States government possesses significant intelligence data and knowledge about unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), including on some that are highly likely to be 'nonanthropogenic vehicles.'" — Gallaudet, Nolan, Skafish

> "Most of all, the military and intelligence whistleblowers who came forward in recent years, despite unimaginable personal risk, are owed our gratitude for their courage and patriotism." — Gallaudet, Nolan, Skafish

> "We hope that today's release will also encourage Congress to pass transparency legislation cosponsored in previous years by Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer and Senator Michael Rounds." — Gallaudet, Nolan, Skafish
