# Sample Analysis at Mars (SAM)

Instrument suite aboard [[curiosity-rover|NASA's Curiosity rover]] combining a gas chromatograph, a quadrupole mass spectrometer, and a tunable laser spectrometer. Designed to analyze the chemical composition of Mars rocks, soils, and atmosphere. Built and led in part by the team at [[nasa-goddard]], with [[jennifer-eigenbrode]] among the instrument-team co-leads.

## Major findings

- Detected chlorobenzene and thiophenes in earlier mission years.
- Tracked seasonal methane variability in Gale Crater air.
- April 2026: a one-shot **TMAH** (tetramethylammonium hydroxide) wet derivatization run on 2020 Glen Torridon mudstone yielded more than 20 [[organic-molecules-mars|organic molecules]] including a nitrogen-containing DNA-precursor-like compound and **benzothiophene** ([[src-curiosity-mars-life-molecules-2026-04]]). This was the first wet-chemistry experiment ever performed on another planet. SAM carried only ~two cups of TMAH, forcing a single high-stakes site selection.

The TMAH technique has been carried forward into ESA's [[rosalind-franklin-rover]] and NASA's [[dragonfly-mission]] (Titan).
