# PRISM MarketView: "University of Georgia Confirms: Buga Sphere Dates Back 12,560 Years"

## Summary

Promotional PRISM MarketView piece (Gerry Griffith, Oct 24 2025) restating the [[steven-greer]]/[[jaime-maussan]] claim that Accelerator Mass Spectrometry at the [[university-of-georgia]] dated organic resin embedded in the [[buga-sphere]] — a basketball-sized metallic object recovered near Buga, Colombia in March 2025 — to **12,560 years before present**, placing its creation in the Younger Dryas period. The piece frames the sphere as evidence of advanced pre-flood civilizations, likens 31 microscopic resin-anchored channels to modern fiber-optic assemblies, and groups Buga with the Baghdad Battery and Antikythera Mechanism as "out-of-place artifacts." It names no UGA personnel, methodology, or raw data. **This claim is directly rebutted by [[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]] and by Colombian astronomers cited in [[src-buga-sphere-el-pais-cali-maussan-handoff-2025-10]] and [[src-buga-sphere-infobae-true-origin-2025-10]].** Treat as **unverified, promotional, single-source**.

## Key Claims

- A UGA accelerator-mass-spectrometry lab dated organic resin from inside the sphere to 12,560 years before present (attributed to Greer's team).
- 31 microscopic precision-drilled holes contain resin-anchored thread-like structures with claimed fiber-optic-like properties.
- The sphere shows no atmospheric-entry pitting/melting, implying advanced protective tech or non-conventional descent.
- The 12,560-year date places creation in the Younger Dryas (12,900–11,700 BP), invoked as evidence of a pre-flood advanced civilization.
- The sphere is grouped with the Baghdad Battery and Antikythera Mechanism as out-of-place artifacts.
- Critics cite contamination, dating-accuracy questions, and natural-crystal explanations; Greer's team "calls for independent analysis."

## Notable Quotes

> "The University of Georgia's lab dated the resin to 12,560 years ago, placing the sphere's creation during the Younger Dryas period…"

> "The 31 microscopic holes, containing resin-anchored threads, resemble modern fiber optic cable assemblies."

> "Dr. Greer's team insists on the integrity of their methods, calling for independent analysis to confirm their findings."

## Caveats

PRISM MarketView is a financial-PR / marketing wire; the piece reads as advocacy rather than reporting. No UGA personnel, lab director, sample-prep methodology, or calibration data is supplied. The [[university-of-georgia]] has not publicly confirmed the result, and per the companion debunk source, the underlying methodology (dating organic resin to infer the age of an inorganic container) is unsound.
