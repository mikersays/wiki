# Thomas Massie

**Thomas Massie** is a Republican US Representative from Kentucky's 4th congressional district and a frequent intra-party critic of [[donald-trump]]. In the [[pursue]] context he is the principal Republican voice on record dismissing the file-release effort.

## UAP positions

- In **February 2026**, after Trump's Truth Social directive to release UAP/UFO files, Massie publicly decried the effort as "the **ultimate weapon of mass distraction**" — per [[src-al-jazeera-pursue-uap-files-2026-05]]. As of the May 8 [[pursue]] Release 01 launch, this remains the only Republican-side dismissal of PURSUE captured in this vault; the broader skeptical wave (e.g. [[src-twz-pursue-shrug-2026-05]], [[src-sciam-pursue-skeptics-2026-05]]) is driven by analysts and outlets, not lawmakers.
- His critique frames PURSUE alongside Trump's parallel JFK/RFK/MLK and [[jeffrey-epstein]] file-release patterns — i.e. as part of a broader distraction strategy rather than a substantive disclosure step.
- Massie's framing now anchors a recurring [[disclosure-as-distraction]] thesis adopted by [[matt-novak]]/[[gizmodo]] ([[src-gizmodo-find-ufos-pentagon-videos-2026-05]]) and [[aravindan-neelakandan]]/[[swarajya]] ([[src-swarajya-disclosure-distraction-2026-05]]) in commentary on PURSUE Release 01.
