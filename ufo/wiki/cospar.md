**COSPAR (Committee on Space Research)** is an international scientific committee under the International Science Council (ISC), founded in 1958. COSPAR maintains the **Planetary Protection Policy** — descended from Article IX of the 1967 [[outer-space-treaty|UN Outer Space Treaty]] — which governs forward- and back-contamination requirements for space missions.

A 2026 COSPAR policy update, surfaced at [[abscicon26]] ([[src-abscicon26-media-tips-2-2026-05]]), introduces a dedicated **icy-worlds category** with the following provisions:

- A formal definition of icy worlds.
- Empirical bounds on the driest and coldest conditions under which Earth life can survive.
- A **1,000-year biological-exploration period** during which contamination controls apply to icy-world targets.

The update constrains future missions to [[europa-clipper]], JUICE, and any Enceladus/Europa drill operation. See [[icy-worlds-protection-policy]].
