# United Kingdom

Nation-state. Closed its [[ministry-of-defence|MoD UFO desk]] in 2009; as of December 2024 maintains that [[uap-disclosure|UAPs]] pose no military threat ([[src-uk-mod-uap-no-threat-2024]]).

Hosts U.S. Air Force units at multiple Royal Air Force bases, including [[raf-lakenheath]] (F-15E/F-35A; reported U.S. nuclear-weapons storage), [[raf-mildenhall]] (KC-135 tankers), [[raf-feltwell]], and [[raf-fairford]] (strategic-bomber forward operating location). All four bases experienced UAP/sUAS incursions reported by [[usafe-afa]] in November–December 2024 — concurrent with the [[drone-incursions|broader incursion pattern]] over U.S. military and nuclear sites.

The UK's posture diverges from its closest allies: the U.S. has institutionalized UAP investigation via [[aaro]] and the [[ndaa-uap-provisions|NDAA]], [[japan]] is building a [[japan-uap-framework|whole-of-government UAP framework]], and Canada operates the [[sky-canada-project]].

## Air-defence capacity gap

A 27 November 2024 cross-party Westminster Hall debate examined UK [[ground-based-air-defence|GBAD]] gaps in the wake of the RAF base incursions ([[src-uk-airspace-defense-gap-2024-11]]). Highlights:

- [[british-army]] operates only ~6 [[sky-sabre]] systems (~25 km intercept; cannot defeat ballistic / hypersonic).
- [[royal-navy]] [[type-45-destroyer|Type 45]] destroyers provide UK's only ballistic-missile detect/track/partial-intercept — but inherently maritime.
- [[royal-air-force|RAF]] [[quick-reaction-alert]] runs from RAF Coningsby and RAF Lossiemouth.
- No terminal-phase ballistic-missile defense; long undefended target list including [[atomic-weapons-establishment]] sites and [[akrotiri-and-dhekelia]] sovereign base areas.
- [[radar-clutter-windfarms|North Sea wind-farm radar clutter]] creates detection blackouts.

The U.S. and UK are jointly establishing **common C-UAS data standards** for interoperability, led by [[jiatf-401]] ([[src-bumblebee-v2-ncr-counterdrone-2026-04]]).
