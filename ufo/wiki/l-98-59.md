**L 98-59** is a nearby [[m-dwarf-stars|M dwarf]] star ~35 light-years from Earth in the southern constellation Volans. It hosts a multi-planet system originally discovered by [[nasa|NASA]]'s TESS mission and characterized in follow-up by space- and ground-based observatories.

## Notable planets

- **[[l-98-59-d]]** — 1.6 R⊕ sulphur-rich [[magma-ocean-world]] reported by [[harrison-nicholls]], [[raymond-pierrehumbert]] et al. ([[university-of-oxford]]) in 2026 ([[src-l-98-59-d-sulphur-magma-world-2026-05]]). Proposed as a new small-planet class distinct from rocky / gaseous / water-rich categories; likely descended from a [[mini-neptune|sub-Neptune]] precursor.
