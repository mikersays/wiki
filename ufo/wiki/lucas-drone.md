**LUCAS** (Low-Cost Uncrewed Combat Attack System) is a U.S.-built one-way attack drone cloned from an Iranian variant — comparable in role to the [[shahed-drone|Shahed]] family it was reverse-engineered from. Unit cost is in the mid-to-low tens of thousands of dollars; initial production was "just dozens" of systems.

## Timeline

- **December 2025** — First launched from a littoral combat ship during a Middle East exercise.
- **March 2026** — [[centcom]] used LUCAS in combat for the first time during [[operation-epic-fury]] against Iran (per the Pentagon CTO).
- **May 2026** — Pentagon selected [[shield-ai]]'s [[hivemind]] swarm/autonomy software as LUCAS's "AI pilot"; capability demo planned for fall 2026.

The LUCAS program is a flagship example of the [[drone-dominance]] doctrine — fielding cheap, mass-produced, Iranian-derived offensive drones to close the cost-imbalance gap that motivated FY27 budget shifts ($53.6B autonomy/drones/contested logistics; $21B munitions/C-UAS/CCA).
