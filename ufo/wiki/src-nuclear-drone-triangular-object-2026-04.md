## Summary

[[liberation-times]] report on U.S. [[nuclear-regulatory-commission|Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] (NRC) records obtained via FOIA detailing 22 drone incidents at nuclear power plants between September 2022 and February 2023. The records were sent by the NRC to [[aaro]] between January 2020 and November 2025, per the FY2022 [[ndaa-uap-provisions|NDAA]] mandate. Activity concentrated at **Susquehanna Steam Electric Station** (Pennsylvania, 8 incidents) and **Columbia Generating Station** (Washington state, 9 incidents), together accounting for 17 of 22 incidents. Additional activity at Comanche Peak (Texas, 3), Monticello (Minnesota, 1), and Peach Bottom (Pennsylvania, 1).

## Key Claims

- **Triangular object at Susquehanna (Oct 3 2022):** At least four drones entered airspace from multiple directions; a fourth was described as larger, triangular in shape, carrying a large spotlight. Encounter lasted 2 hours 8 minutes (NRC records)
- **Eight-drone swarm at Susquehanna (Sep 26 2022):** Eight drones entered the plant's airspace; interaction lasted ~2 hours 45 minutes (NRC records)
- **Large drone at Columbia:** One drone estimated at 8-10 feet in diameter with white and red flashing lights. DOE's Hanford Patrol followed a drone eastward before losing it (NRC records)
- **Historical Hanford UAP:** Columbia Generating Station area has anomalous-activity history dating to the 1940s Manhattan Project era, documented by [[robert-hastings]] in *UFOs and Nukes*: 1945 radar bogey pursuit, 1949 "silvery, disc-shaped" hovering object, 1952 F-94 radar lock-on over Hanford (article)
- The article links nuclear-site incursions to March 2026 drone events at Fort McNair and [[barksdale-afb]] (article)
