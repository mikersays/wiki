# ESA Earth Explorer 13

[[esa|European Space Agency]] **Earth Explorer 13** is a forthcoming mission opportunity within ESA's Earth Explorer programme — a series of competitively selected satellites targeting specific Earth-system science questions.

[[christopher-kyba]] is principal investigator on a proposal for an Earth Explorer 13 mission **dedicated to nighttime artificial-light measurements** at higher spatial resolution and lower detection threshold than the current [[viirs-dnb|VIIRS DNB]] capability ([[src-terrestrial-technosignatures-satellites-2026-04]]). No European satellite is currently dedicated to nighttime-lights science.

The proposal motivates the mission as serving three intersecting goals: ecological-impact monitoring of [[light-pollution]], energy-policy tracking of [[nighttime-light-emissions]], and astrobiological calibration of [[terrestrial-technosignature]] models for [[seti]] and exoplanet [[direct-imaging]].

Status as of May 2026: under selection.
