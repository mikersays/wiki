**Operation Spider Web** was a 2025 Ukrainian near-field drone attack on Russian long-range aviation assets parked on flightlines deep inside Russia. The operation demonstrated that small, low-cost drones smuggled close to target bases can destroy strategic bombers at scale, transforming the U.S. domestic base-defense threat model from theoretical to concrete.

Spider Web is frequently invoked by U.S. officials including Gen. [[gregory-guillot]] as the rationale for urgency behind layered counter-small-UAS (C-sUAS) capabilities and [[flyaway-kit]] fielding. The 2026 pattern of [[drone-incursions]] over U.S. nuclear-capable bomber bases ([[barksdale-afb|Barksdale]], suspected [[whiteman-afb|Whiteman]]) is contextualized against Spider Web's demonstration of flightline vulnerability.

Per [[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04]], Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]] (Apr 16 2026) cites Spider Web as the doctrinal motivator behind expanding [[jiatf-401-marketplace]] eligibility to state/local/federal law enforcement and routing C-UAS tooling to FIFA World Cup 2026, America 250, and the 2028 LA Olympics — i.e. stateside fixed and mobile high-value targets.
