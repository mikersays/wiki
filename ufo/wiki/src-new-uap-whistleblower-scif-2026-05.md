## Summary

UFOUAP reports that the House UAP Caucus has identified a new UAP whistleblower and is pursuing classified SCIF briefings rather than public hearings — a strategic reversal from the televised [[david-grusch]] testimony of July 2023. [[eric-burlison]] confirmed the lead in a December 16, 2025 interview, while [[nancy-mace]] expressed interest in a SCIF session with Secretary of State [[marco-rubio]]. The article also details Grusch's March 2025 appointment as Special Advisor to the House Oversight Task Force, three UAP provisions enacted in the [[ndaa-uap-provisions|FY2026 NDAA]] (NORAD/NORTHCOM intercept briefings, streamlined data access, classification guide accounting), [[donald-trump]]'s February 20, 2026 directive to release UAP files with [[pete-hegseth]] as point person, and [[aaro]]'s unchanged "no verifiable evidence" position.

## Key Claims

- [[eric-burlison]] confirmed a new UAP whistleblower lead who "might go public" but is not yet comfortable doing so (Dec 16, 2025 Ask a Pol UAP interview)
- The House UAP Caucus strategy has shifted from public hearings to classified SCIF briefings first, designed to let witnesses speak freely under classification protections
- [[nancy-mace]] wants a classified SCIF briefing with [[marco-rubio]], signaling House belief that relevant information cannot surface in open settings
- [[david-grusch]] was hired as Special Advisor by Burlison in March 2025, advising the House Oversight Task Force on hearings and briefings into 2026
- FY2026 NDAA (Public Law 119-60, enacted Dec 18, 2025) embedded three UAP provisions in Subtitle E (Sections 1671-1673) amending 50 U.S.C. section 3373
- A federal UAP records framework requires agencies to review and identify UAP records within 300 days, with full disclosure within 25 years unless the president certifies an exception
- [[aaro]]'s official position remains that it has "discovered no verifiable evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity or technology" (Nov 2024 DoD release)
- Congress is now questioning whether [[aaro]] has been given sufficient data in the first place
