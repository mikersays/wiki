# Karin Austin

Managing Director of the [[john-mack-institute|John Mack Institute]] and Rice University's [[center-for-the-impossible|Center For The Impossible]]. A former participant in [[john-mack|John Mack]]'s "alien abduction" research and his personal assistant in the final years of his life, Austin helped the Mack family preserve his archives after his 2004 death and **facilitated their 2022 transfer to Harvard and Rice**. She now leads a multidisciplinary team conducting metadata analysis of experiencer materials across archival collections.

At the July 2026 [[src-breaking-the-taboo-uap-contact-symposium-2026-05|"Breaking the Taboo" symposium]] (which she co-organized), Austin argues for **rigorous, case-by-case assessment of contact testimony** — building on [[michael-bohlander]]'s legal-standard witness-credibility argument — and presents the Center's "first-of-its-kind" metadata analysis of "thousands of convergent" experiencer accounts.
