# Tom Karako

Senior fellow and director of the **Missile Defense Project** at [[csis|CSIS]]. Frequently quoted defense analyst on U.S. air- and missile-defense posture and, increasingly, [[counter-uas|counter-drone]] capability.

In April 2026 commentary on the [[jiatf-401|JIATF-401]] C-UAS surge, Karako argued the U.S. [[drone-incursions|drone problem]] had been "artificially divided" between overseas combat and homeland threats and that increased investment, while "dramatically overdue," reflects a long-anticipated requirement ([[src-counter-drone-middle-east-jiatf-2026-04]]). He suspects [[coyote-interceptor|Coyote interceptors]] and [[merops-interceptor|Merops]] systems have done "the lion's share of the drone killing" in CENTCOM.

## "Super Bowl bubbles all year round" framing (April 2026)

Per [[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04]], Karako frames the stateside C-UAS problem set as needing **ubiquitous sensing + mobile air defense across many simultaneous high-profile events** — FIFA World Cup 2026, America 250, the 2028 LA Olympics — rather than the once-a-year Super Bowl precedent:

> "It used to be that we just did the Super Bowl once a year with that kind of cap. But we need lots of Super Bowl-style bubbles over lots of things all year round."
