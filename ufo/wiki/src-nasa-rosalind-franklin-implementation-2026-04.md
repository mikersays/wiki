## Summary

[[nasa]] has approved its **Rosalind Franklin Support and Augmentation (ROSA)** project to enter the implementation phase, formalizing the agency's hardware and services contribution to [[esa]]'s [[rosalind-franklin-rover]] mission. The mission targets a **late 2028** launch on a SpaceX Falcon Heavy from Kennedy Space Center. ROSA provides the launch service, braking engines for the lander platform, radioisotope heater units for the rover, and a state-of-the-art mass spectrometer for the **Mars Organic Molecule Analyzer (MOMA)** instrument that will search for the building blocks of life in samples collected at [[oxia-planum]].

## Key Claims

- The [[rosalind-franklin-rover]] will be the first Mars rover to search for signs of past or present life **beneath** the Martian surface (NASA)
- MOMA's mass spectrometer extends the [[sample-analysis-at-mars|SAM]]/TMAH wet-chemistry heritage from [[curiosity-rover]] (NASA)
- NASA and ESA signed a Memorandum of Understanding in early 2024 expanding NASA's ExoMars role (NASA)
- Launch targeted for late 2028 on Falcon Heavy under the NASA Launch Services II IDIQ contract (NASA)

## Astrobiology Relevance

Subsurface drilling at [[oxia-planum]] targets organics shielded from cosmic-ray destruction — the same rationale underpinning the Martian ice-preservation findings ([[src-mars-ancient-life-ice-survival-2026-02]]). The 2028 launch aligns the mission with the broader Mars [[biosignatures]] pipeline that includes [[perseverance-rover]] cached samples and the eventual [[mars-sample-return]].
