**The MITRE Corporation** is a U.S. not-for-profit company that operates multiple [[ffrdc|federally funded research and development centers (FFRDCs)]] on behalf of federal sponsors, working extensively on classified defense, intelligence, aviation, and homeland-security programs.

## UAP records inquiry (May 2026)

MITRE enters the vault via Rep. [[eric-burlison]]'s May 22, 2026 records-production request. On May 26 Burlison's office announced ([[src-burlison-mitre-press-release-2026-05]]) that he had transmitted **legislative interrogatories and production requests** to MITRE seeking records on its potential custody, control, analysis, transfer, destruction, or withholding of UAP-related records — including recovered materials, "technologies of unknown origin," [[transmedium-uap|transmedium]] data, and alleged legacy [[crash-retrieval-programs|crash-retrieval or reverse-engineering programs]] — dating back to 1930.

Per DefenseScoop ([[src-burlison-mitre-uap-records-2026-05]]), the 10-page letter carries an enclosure with 40+ enumerated requests (records collections/transfers, contracts, task orders, classified/special-access programs, analysis activities, AI models, technology exploitation, health/safety data, secure facilities). A MITRE spokesperson confirmed staff are reviewing the company's archives to comply and would coordinate with responsible federal agencies on providing any assets.

Burlison's letter argues that MITRE's FFRDC-operator role places it potentially "in custody or control of government-funded records" that fall within the FY2024 NDAA [[uap-records-collection|UAP Records Collection]] framework ([[nara|NARA]] Record Group 615), and that FFRDCs "cannot become private vaults for federal records." It builds on his prior letter to [[mit-lincoln-laboratory|MIT Lincoln Laboratory]] over the Beacon Hill Study, and demands a preservation hold, a records-location index, production of unclassified records, and a classified briefing for [[task-force-declassification-federal-secrets|Task Force]] members. The inquiry names a roster of aerospace/defense contractors (Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, Raytheon/RTX, Boeing, General Dynamics, SAIC, Leidos, Battelle, The Aerospace Corporation, Booz Allen Hamilton, BAE Systems, Bigelow Aerospace) whose UAP-related work MITRE is asked to surface.
