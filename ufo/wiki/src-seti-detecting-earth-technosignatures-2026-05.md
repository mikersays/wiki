## Summary

A [[seti-institute|SETI Institute]] *SETI Live* blog post recapping a conversation between [[sofia-sheikh|Dr. Sofia Sheikh]] and [[simon-steel|Dr. Simon Steel]] (Deputy Director of the [[carl-sagan-institute|Carl Sagan Center]]) about Sheikh's "Earth Detecting Earth" study: how detectable present-day Earth would be to an extraterrestrial civilization with technology comparable to our own. Rather than speculative megastructures, the study inventories the [[technosignatures]] humanity actually emits — radio leakage, optical/laser signals, atmospheric and surface markers, and artificial objects — and estimates their detection ranges. It finds that powerful directed signals (planetary radar like the decommissioned Arecibo dish) are detectable at great distances, while everyday leakage like 4G LTE is only detectable to about 4 light-years (roughly to Proxima Centauri). The piece frames Earth itself as a calibration target for [[terrestrial-technosignature]] work and notes detectability will shift as energy use, pollution, and communication technology evolve. Though published February 2025, it complements the vault's later technosignature sources.

## Key Claims

- Earth could be detected via four channels: radio (4G LTE towers, Wi-Fi, GPS, planetary radar), optical (high-powered communication lasers), atmospheric/surface impacts (NO₂ pollution, night-side city lights), and artificial objects (satellites, lunar landers, debris). (Sheikh study, via SETI Institute)
- Powerful planetary radar bursts (e.g., the decommissioned Arecibo dish) are detectable at great distances; weaker Deep Space Network (DSN) and laser-comm signals require much closer proximity. (Sheikh / Steel)
- Low-level 4G LTE leakage could be detectable out to ~4 light-years — enough to reach Proxima Centauri. (Sheikh)
- Detectability is limited by signal strength vs. cosmic noise, signal type (narrow-band signals are clearer technology markers), directionality (radar pulses are not isotropic), and light-travel time (the 1974 Arecibo message has only traveled ~50 light-years). (Sheikh / Steel)
- Earth's technosignature profile is not static — changes in energy consumption, pollution reduction, and communication tech will alter its cosmic visibility, warranting periodic re-study. (Sheikh)

## Notable Quotes

> "[Earth's] low-level radiation from 4G LTE signals could be detectable within about four light years — enough to reach Proxima Centauri, our closest stellar neighbor." — SETI Institute (paraphrasing Sheikh's study)
