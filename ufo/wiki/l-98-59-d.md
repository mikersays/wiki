**L 98-59 d** is a ~1.6 R⊕ exoplanet orbiting the [[m-dwarf-stars|M dwarf]] [[l-98-59]] ~35 light-years from Earth in Volans. In a 2026 [[university-of-oxford|Oxford]]-led study ([[src-l-98-59-d-sulphur-magma-world-2026-05]]), the planet is proposed as the prototype of a **new small-planet class**: a persistent, global sulphur-rich silicate **[[magma-ocean-world]]** sustained by a heavy **[[sulphur-atmosphere|sulphur-bearing atmosphere]]** driving a runaway greenhouse.

## Key properties

- Radius: ~1.6 R⊕
- Density: lower than expected for a purely terrestrial composition
- Distance: ~35 ly in Volans
- Host: [[m-dwarf-stars|M dwarf]] [[l-98-59]]
- Atmosphere: heavy sulphur-bearing molecules → intense greenhouse → mantle stays molten over Gyr timescales
- Surface: uninhabitable; persistent global magma ocean
- Likely formation pathway: shrunken former [[mini-neptune|sub-Neptune]] that lost most of its primordial envelope

## Significance

- Lead author [[harrison-nicholls]] argues the result shows current small-planet taxonomy (rocky / gaseous / water-rich) is too coarse.
- Co-author [[raymond-pierrehumbert]] frames the methodology as a demonstration that coupled atmosphere–interior modelling can reconstruct deep planetary history from mass, radius, and atmospheric composition.
- Widens the planetary diversity catalogue against which [[biosignatures]] and [[false-positive-biosignatures]] are interpreted.
