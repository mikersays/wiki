# University of Georgia

The **University of Georgia (UGA)** is a U.S. public research university in Athens, Georgia. Its **Center for Applied Isotope Studies (CAIS)** is one of the world's most cited Accelerator Mass Spectrometry (AMS) [[radiocarbon-dating]] laboratories.

## UAP relevance

UGA is named — without documentation — as the source of an AMS [[radiocarbon-dating]] result of **~12,560 years** for organic resin allegedly extracted from inside the [[buga-sphere]]. The claim was promoted publicly by [[jaime-maussan]] and [[steven-greer]] at the May 8 2026 [[the-disclosure-project]] NPC event ([[src-greer-disclosure-event-npc-2026-05]]) and articulated in [[src-buga-sphere-uga-carbon-date-claim-2025-10]].

As of May 2026, the following remain unaccounted for:

- No published UGA / CAIS lab report or press release.
- No named UGA personnel, principal investigator, or sample-prep methodology.
- No calibration data, error margins, or raw measurements.
- No peer-reviewed publication.

[[src-buga-sphere-carbon-dating-debunk-2026-04]] and [[src-buga-sphere-ibtimes-ball-from-nowhere-2026-03]] argue the claim should be treated as unverified pending UGA documentation; Colombian astronomer [[marino-guarin]] explicitly notes the absence of any "official report from the university."
