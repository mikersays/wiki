## Summary

[[astrobiology-com]] ([[keith-cowing]], May 18 2026) reproduces the announcement for a two-part evening session at [[abscicon26]] on Monday May 18. The first half covers a programmatic update from [[nasa]] Headquarters on the NASA Astrobiology Program — current activities, organizational changes, and upcoming funding opportunities emphasizing interdisciplinary collaboration. The second half introduces **[[nasa-dares]]** (NASA Decadal Astrobiology Research and Exploration Strategy), a strategic framework being developed by Task Force 2. The session previews a draft strategy framework, cross-cutting themes, an upcoming public comment period, and community engagement opportunities, with a target publication date of **December 2026**.

## Key Claims

- NASA Headquarters is providing a programmatic update on the NASA Astrobiology Program at AbSciCon26, covering organizational changes and upcoming opportunities
- [[nasa-dares]] Task Force 2 is developing a decadal-scale astrobiology research and exploration strategy, with a draft framework now ready for community preview
- A public comment period on the NASA-DARES strategy is forthcoming
- The final NASA-DARES strategy document is targeted for publication in December 2026
- The session frames astrobiology as "a central pillar of NASA science and exploration in the decades ahead"
