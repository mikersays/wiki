# Jordan Flowers

Executive Director of the **[[disclosure-foundation]]**, a nonprofit advocating UAP transparency through legal action, policy, and whistleblower support. Recurring on-the-record critic of [[aaro]]'s reporting cadence and classification record.

## Statements

- **Feb 25 2026 (via [[src-uap-trump-disclosure-2026|DefenseScoop]])**: Flowers expressed cautious optimism about [[donald-trump|Trump]]'s disclosure directive but pressed AARO to release decades-old unclassified imagery, fill its vacant deputy-director role, and meet its statutory reporting obligations. He also urged Congress to enact a legislative disclosure framework distinct from executive action.

> "Trust is earned. And if AARO wants to earn the public's trust, then it should move expeditiously to release its trove of unclassified videos and photos." — Jordan Flowers

## On PURSUE Release 01 — provenance hazard (May 14 2026)

Per [[src-defensescoop-pursue-not-disclosure-2026-05]], Flowers raises the [[psyops-disinformation|1950s-era disinformation-laundering]] concern: U.S. government UAP disinformation campaigns since the 1950s — produced as smokescreens for classified weapons programs — may have generated artifacts now being re-released as authentic UAP records via [[pursue]].

> "If some of what is now being released to the public as authentic government UAP records are in fact disinformation artifacts from that era, the public deserves to know. Transparency requires not just releasing files, but accounting for their provenance and integrity."

His team will dig into provenance, integrity, and other public-accountability matters at the [[disclosure-foundation|Disclosure Foundation's June 2026 Disclosure Forum]] with legislators, national-security officials, scientists, legal experts, and journalists.
