# IBTimes Singapore: The Ball From Nowhere

## Summary

IBTimes Singapore (Mar 19 2026) frames the [[buga-sphere]] — a metallic orb reportedly recovered by [[jose-arias-restrepo|José Arias Restrepo]] near Buga, Colombia on March 2, 2025 — as a viral story whose evidentiary base has not kept pace with its headlines. A January 2026 follow-up video and a **June 2025 press conference featuring [[steven-greer]] and Rep. [[eric-burlison]]** reignited attention without producing independently verified data. The article catalogs sensational claims attributed to [[jaime-maussan]]'s *Tercer Milenio* and to a UNAM-affiliated team (concentric layers, titanium-like alloy with "unknown elements," self-healing micropores, gravity-offsetting EM field, 12,560-year resin dating, lymphocyte DNA damage in handlers) and notes that **none** have been peer-reviewed, chain-of-custody-certified, or independently reproduced. It cites [[julia-mossbridge]]'s public assessment that the object looks human-made and her call to submit it to [[galileo-project]] rigor. Historical Nuremberg (1561) and Basel (1566) sphere events are invoked as cultural parallels, not precedents. Conclusion: "not a discovery, but a very well-travelled rumor."

## Key Claims

- The sphere was reportedly recovered March 2, 2025 in Buga after a loud explosion; initial accounts describe a seamless metallic surface and weight fluctuating from ~2 to ~10 kg.
- No independent laboratory verification, [[chain-of-custody]] documentation, or peer-reviewed analysis confirms the weight, thermal, or material anomalies.
- X-ray and portable-spectrometer readings attributed to independent researchers describe concentric layers, a titanium-based alloy, and "unknown elements" — the article notes "unknown" spectrometer hits typically indicate calibration error, contamination, or misinterpretation.
- The sphere's global profile rode through [[jaime-maussan]]'s *Tercer Milenio*; Maussan's prior artifact promotions ([[nazca-mummies]]) have been debunked, prompting scientific caution.
- A **June 2025 press conference featuring [[steven-greer]] and Rep. [[eric-burlison]]** generated coverage but produced no verifiable evidence.
- The January 2026 follow-up video of a similar orb has not undergone independent forensic analysis.
- [[julia-mossbridge]] (University of San Diego) publicly stated the object's appearance suggests human fabrication and urged submission to the [[galileo-project]]'s controlled, reproducible-analysis framework.
- The article reproduces a list of UNAM-attributed findings (fiber-optic-like internal network, self-healing micropores, 18 symmetric microspheres with ~12,000-year-half-life radioactive isotopes, daily 0.1% mass gain, gravity-offsetting EM field, 12,560-year University of Georgia dating, lymphocyte DNA damage in handlers) — presented without endorsement and without UNAM having publicly confirmed any alien-origin conclusion.
- The 1561 Nuremberg and 1566 Basel sphere accounts are reread as atmospheric optics and cultural anxiety, framing Buga as a psychologically primed analog rather than evidence of historical precedent.
