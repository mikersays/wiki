## Summary

Astronomers using [[jwst]] obtained the first detailed [[transmission-spectroscopy|transmission spectrum]] of a **temperate giant exoplanet**, **TOI-199b** — a Saturn-sized world orbiting a star >330 ly from Earth with a ~100-day period and an estimated temperature of ~175 F (~80 C). The study detected **methane (CH4)** in the atmosphere and found hints of ammonia and CO2. The result confirms theoretical predictions that temperate gas giants should harbor methane, filling a gap between the frozen giants of the solar system and the well-studied hot Jupiters. Published May 20, 2026 in the *Astronomical Journal*; led by [[renyu-hu]] (Penn State / [[nasa-jpl]]) with first author Aaron Bello-Arufe (JPL).

## Key Claims

- TOI-199b is one of only a small number of known **temperate giant exoplanets** — too warm to be a frozen gas giant, too cool to be a hot Jupiter (Hu et al.)
- First time the atmosphere of a temperate gas giant has been studied in detail via [[transmission-spectroscopy]] (Hu et al.)
- **Methane detected** in the atmosphere, confirming model predictions for temperate gas-giant composition (Hu et al.)
- Hints of **ammonia and CO2** also present; additional observations needed (Hu et al.)
- The ~7-hour transit required ~20 hours of continuous JWST observation for baseline calibration (Hu et al.)

## Notable Quotes

> "Models for the composition of temperate, gas-giant exoplanets had predicted that they would contain methane, so it is good to get confirmation that our theories are accurate." — Aaron Bello-Arufe

> "This more complete picture of a temperate gas giant's atmosphere can then be used to improve our models and potentially better understand how planets and their atmospheres form and evolve, including for Earth." — Renyu Hu
