The **ROTOR Act** was a bipartisan air safety bill prompted by the January 2024 Reagan National Airport midair collision between an Army helicopter and a commercial airplane over the Potomac River.

The bill failed to pass the House after the Pentagon withdrew its support. Sen. [[ted-cruz]] criticized the [[department-of-war]] for reversing its stance and issuing what he called an "objectively false" press release attacking the legislation. The ROTOR Act's failure became a political backdrop during Senate scrutiny of the February 2026 Texas [[counter-uas|C-UAS]] incidents. See [[src-texas-counter-drone-senators-2026-03]].
