The **lighting balloon hypothesis** is [[aaro]]'s preferred prosaic-explanation pattern for several pilot UAP reports, including the [[eglin-afb-diamond-formation-2023|January 26, 2023 Eglin AFB diamond-formation incident]] (resolved by AARO as "very likely an ordinary object" — possibly a lighting balloon — with "moderate confidence").

The hypothesis has been criticized by independent skeptic [[mick-west]] as analytically convenient and under-supported by evidence: "the lighting balloon hypothesis always felt like something someone at AARO liked, but wasn't really supported by much evidence." It overlaps with [[sean-kirkpatrick]]'s broader hobbyist-balloon framing of early-2023 shootdowns including the [[lake-huron-shootdown-2023]].

Sits in tension with the witness-stigma critique advanced by Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]] and the [[uap-whistleblower-protection-act-2025|UAP Whistleblower Protection Act]] track.
