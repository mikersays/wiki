## Summary

Astrobiology.com ([[keith-cowing]]) reports on a preprint by Jian-Kang Li, Zhen-Zhao Tao, and Tong-Jie Zhang (arXiv:2603.19023) describing the first narrowband radio [[technosignatures]] search of [[3i-atlas]], the third confirmed interstellar object. Using the **Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical Telescope** (FAST) L-band multibeam receiver across four observation epochs (October 2025 – January 2026) — timed to Mars closest approach, perihelion, Earth closest approach, and departure — the team searched for frequency-drifting signals with SNR > 10 within 1.05–1.45 GHz via the BLISS pipeline. No credible technosignature was detected. The null result constrains any hypothetical transmitter on 3I/ATLAS to above 2.862 × 10⁻³ W. The authors introduce a Bayesian inference framework using physically motivated priors to bracket plausible transmitter scenarios.

## Key Claims

- No credible narrowband radio technosignature detected from 3I/ATLAS across four FAST observation windows (Li, Tao & Zhang)
- Upper-limit constraint placed on hypothetical transmitters: power must exceed 2.862 × 10⁻³ W to have been detectable (Li, Tao & Zhang)
- Novel Bayesian inference framework introduced to constrain both the existence probability and characteristic power of hypothetical transmitters (Li, Tao & Zhang)
- BLISS pipeline with cluster analysis, drift-rate cut-off, structure tensor, and PCA used for signal characterization (Li, Tao & Zhang)

## Notable Quotes

> "No credible narrowband radio technosignature are detected from 3I/ATLAS after visual inspections." — Li, Tao & Zhang
