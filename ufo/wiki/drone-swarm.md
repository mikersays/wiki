A **drone swarm** is a coordinated group of autonomous unmanned systems operating together under a single human operator in dynamic, communications-constrained environments. Vendors increasingly frame this as **collaborative autonomy** — distinguishing it from preplanned-route autopilots that cannot deviate from a programmed mission.

In May 2026 the Pentagon selected [[shield-ai]]'s [[hivemind]] software to bring swarm autonomy to the [[lucas-drone|LUCAS]] one-way attack drone — a flagship operational example under the [[drone-dominance]] / [[autonomous-warfare]] doctrine.

Distinguish from [[drone-incursions]], which captures the defensive / threat-side view of foreign drone overflights at U.S. installations.
