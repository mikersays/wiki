# Pentagon Releases "Never-Before-Seen" UAP Files — Interagency Rollout (CBS Framing)

## Summary

The Pentagon posted a first batch of declassified UAP records on May 8, 2026 to a new portal at WAR.GOV/UFO. CBS frames the release as an **interagency declassification effort** — files contributed by the [[fbi]], [[department-of-war]] (rebranded DoD), [[nasa]], and the State Department — running under [[pursue]], with [[odni]] coordinating Intelligence Community contributions. CBS's reporting supplies file counts, redaction counts, and the FBI case file at the heart of the release that the DoW press wire ([[src-dow-uap-files-release-2026-05]]) does not enumerate.

## Key Claims (CBS-specific reporting)

- **Total: 162 files** — 120 PDFs, 28 videos, 14 images.
- **108 of 162 files contain redactions** (≈67%). Officials say redactions protect eyewitness identities, the locations of government facilities, and sensitive military sites — *not* the nature/existence of any UAP encounter.
- **[[fbi-case-file-62-hq-83894]]** is a core document — covers 1947–1968, concerns unidentified objects and "flying discs," and contains 18 sub-documents including reports from **Oak Ridge, Tennessee**.
- An FBI photo composite is paired with an eyewitness sketch describing an "**apparent ellipsoid bronze metallic object**" estimated at 130–195 feet long.
- Additional FBI photographs from **New Year's Eve 1999** depict purported UAP alongside U.S. aircraft.
- **Six [[apollo-12]] / [[apollo-17]] photographs** are included; one Apollo 17 surface shot shows three dots in the sky.
- Apollo 17 voice transcripts: crew describe "very bright particles or fragments" drifting past, "big ones on my window" looking "like the Fourth of July," and "very jagged, angular fragments that are tumbling."
- Some cases remain unresolved; the government "cannot make definitive determinations."
- CBS reiterates [[aaro]]'s 2024 historical report finding **no evidence** of recovered ET tech or biologics, and notes Trump himself "has said publicly he is uncertain whether aliens exist."
