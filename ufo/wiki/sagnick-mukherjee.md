**Sagnick Mukherjee** is a 51 Pegasi b postdoctoral fellow at Arizona State University's School of Earth and Space Exploration, who began the work below as a PhD student at UC Santa Cruz (and was earlier affiliated with Johns Hopkins University).

He is the **lead author** of the *Science* (May 21, 2026) study that used [[jwst]]'s NIRISS instrument and limb-resolved [[transmission-spectroscopy]] to detect the first confirmed daily weather cycle on an exoplanet, the [[hot-jupiter]] [[wasp-94ab]] ([[src-jwst-wasp-94ab-weather-cycle-2026-05]]). His next work, with senior author [[david-sing]], will study cloud cycling across a range of exoplanets using a new large JWST program, including an eccentric gas giant in the habitable zone.

## Notable Quote

> "It was really surprising how different the two halves of the same planet are."
