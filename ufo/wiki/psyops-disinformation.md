# UAP Disinformation Campaigns (1950s-era)

A **provenance hazard** for the [[pursue]]-era disclosure effort: the alleged U.S. government practice — dating to the 1950s — of **fabricating false UAP documents** as a smokescreen to mask classified weapons-program activity from public view.

## The provenance hazard

Per [[jordan-flowers]] of the [[disclosure-foundation]] in [[src-defensescoop-pursue-not-disclosure-2026-05]]:

- 1950s-era disinformation artifacts may be **laundered into PURSUE** as authentic government UAP records.
- "If some of what is now being released to the public as authentic government UAP records are in fact disinformation artifacts from that era, the public deserves to know."
- "Transparency requires not just releasing files, but **accounting for their provenance and integrity**."

Flowers's team will dig into this at the foundation's June 2026 [[disclosure-foundation|Disclosure Forum]] with legislators, national-security officials, scientists, legal experts, and journalists.

## Why this matters

If true, the implication is that *some* UAP material on `war.gov/ufo` may be authentic surveillance of unknown phenomena, while *other* material may be deliberate Cold-War-era forgery whose original purpose was to hide weapons R&D. Without provenance chains attached to each file, the public cannot tell which is which — undermining the [[uap-disclosure|"data alone is not disclosure"]] critique articulated by [[ryan-graves]].
