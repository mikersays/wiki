**Ontological shock** refers to the transformative fracturing of worldviews triggered by disclosure of anomalous phenomena. The term was formalized by Lomas (2025) and is cited in [[src-uap-reddit-disclosure-conspiracy-2026-05]].

## Context

According to Bastos & Duarte (2026), ontological shock was observed in X/Twitter reactions to the July 2023 U.S. congressional UAP hearings featuring [[david-grusch]]'s whistleblower testimony. The concept is related to Ashby (2023)'s analysis of the implications of [[uap-disclosure]] on organized religion.

## Relevance

The concept helps explain why UAP disclosure is politically contentious — it threatens established worldviews across religious, scientific, and national-security domains. The [[transparency-effect]] documented by Bastos & Duarte suggests that structured, official disclosure may channel ontological shock into productive deliberation rather than conspiratorial anxiety.
