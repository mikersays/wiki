The **Sardinia Radio Telescope (SRT)** is a 64-metre fully steerable single-dish radio telescope located near San Basilio, Sardinia, Italy, operated by Italy's National Institute for Astrophysics (INAF). It is equipped with high-frequency receivers reaching up to ~115 GHz, making it suited to radio astronomy well above the traditional SETI L-band.

## Significance for technosignature searches

- Site of the **first high-frequency (6 GHz + 18 GHz) radio [[technosignatures|technosignature]] survey**, reported by Manunza et al. (2025). The 18 GHz portion was the first-ever [[seti|SETI]] search attempted at that frequency. The 36-hour survey of the [[galactic-center|Galactic Center]] and 72 [[tess|TESS]] targets returned a null result and set an [[eirp|EIRP]] limit of 1.8×10¹⁹ W. See [[src-sardinia-technosignature-search-2026-05]].
- The survey was led by an Italian team but included several [[breakthrough-listen|Breakthrough Listen]]-affiliated co-authors ([[vishal-gajjar]], [[steve-croft]], Andrew Siemion, Matt Lebofsky, David MacMahon), extending the BL methodology to a new dish and higher frequencies.
