## Summary

Al Jazeera reports that a drone strike on May 17, 2026 sparked a fire at an electrical generator on the perimeter of the **Barakah Nuclear Energy Plant** in the UAE's Al Dhafra region — the first nuclear power station on the Arabian Peninsula. The UAE Defence Ministry said air defences intercepted two of three drones launched from the "western border"; the third hit the generator. The [[iaea]]'s Rafael Grossi expressed "grave concern," noting one reactor temporarily relied on emergency diesel generators. The incident occurred amid ongoing Iranian attacks on the UAE since [[operation-epic-fury]] began on Feb. 28, 2026, and against the backdrop of stalled US-Iran ceasefire negotiations.

## Key Claims

- UAE Defence Ministry: air defences "successfully" dealt with two drones; a third struck a generator near the plant; drones launched from the "western border" (attributed to UAE Defence Ministry)
- IAEA: one reactor temporarily relied on emergency diesel generators; Grossi warned military activity threatening nuclear facilities is "unacceptable" (attributed to IAEA / Rafael Grossi)
- UAE nuclear regulator: all units continued operating normally; radiation levels remained normal (attributed to UAE nuclear regulator)
- UAE absorbed 2,819 Iranian missile/drone attacks Feb 28 – Apr 14 — the most of any nation in the conflict (attributed to prior reporting)
- Israel deployed Iron Dome air defence systems and personnel to the UAE (attributed to US Ambassador Mike Huckabee)
