## Summary

Malaysia restarted the search for [[malaysia-airlines-flight-mh370]], the Boeing 777 that vanished in March 2014 with 239 people. Starting December 30, 2025, Texas-based [[ocean-infinity]] would spend 55 days sweeping a 15,000 km² area of the southern Indian Ocean under a "no find, no fee" deal worth up to $70 million. The company deployed uncrewed surface vessels coordinating swarms of autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) capable of multibeam sonar and high-resolution imaging at depths of nearly 6,000 m. Prior searches (2014–2017 and 2018) covered over 220,000 km² at a combined cost exceeding $200 million without locating the main wreckage.

## Key Claims

- Malaysia contracted [[ocean-infinity]] for a 55-day sweep of a 15,000 km² zone beginning Dec 30, 2025 (Malaysian officials)
- The "no find, no fee" contract caps payment at $70 million, payable only if substantive wreckage is found
- The target zone is based on satellite "handshake" tracking data and drift reconstructions of debris fragments that washed ashore
- [[ocean-infinity]]'s upgraded AUV swarm can operate at ~6,000 m depth with higher resolution and smaller carbon footprint than traditional survey fleets
- Prior searches (2014–2017 joint Australia/Malaysia/China campaign and 2018 Ocean Infinity campaign) covered >220,000 km² at ~$155 million without finding main wreckage
- Only three confirmed pieces of wing have ever been recovered
