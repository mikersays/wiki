## Summary

A [[university-of-oxford|University of Oxford]]-led team reports that [[l-98-59-d]], a 1.6 R⊕ planet orbiting the [[m-dwarf-stars|M dwarf]] [[l-98-59]] ~35 light-years away in Volans, does not fit the rocky / gaseous / water-rich categories and instead represents a new planetary class: a persistent, global sulphur-rich silicate **[[magma-ocean-world|magma-ocean world]]**. Coupled atmosphere–interior modelling plus transit and spectroscopic data indicate a thick **[[sulphur-atmosphere|sulphur-bearing atmosphere]]** drives a runaway greenhouse that keeps the mantle molten over billions of years. The planet is lower density than expected for a terrestrial composition and likely evolved from a [[mini-neptune|sub-Neptune]] precursor that lost much of its primordial envelope. Lead author [[harrison-nicholls]] argues current small-planet taxonomy is too simple. Co-author [[raymond-pierrehumbert]] frames the work as a demonstration that interior history of distant worlds can be reconstructed from mass, radius, and atmospheric data alone. The team expects more such molten worlds to be found by next-generation telescopes ([[jwst]] and successors).

## Key Claims

- L 98-59 d is ~1.6 R⊕, lower density than terrestrial expectation, 35 ly distant in Volans, orbiting M dwarf L 98-59.
- It represents a brand-new planet class — molten, sulphur-rich, global silicate magma ocean — distinct from rocky, gaseous, and water-rich categories.
- A thick atmosphere of heavy sulphur-bearing molecules drives an intense greenhouse effect preventing crust formation.
- The magma ocean persists for billions of years (unlike early Earth, which cooled to a solid crust).
- Formation pathway: likely began as a [[mini-neptune|sub-Neptune]] that shrank and lost much of its primordial atmosphere.
- Methodology combines advanced atmosphere–interior modelling with observational data; interior reconstructed from mass, radius, and atmospheric composition.
- Implication: current small-planet taxonomy is too coarse; sulphur magma worlds are likely a broader population.
- Habitability: the surface is uninhabitable, but the result widens the diversity catalogue framing [[biosignatures]] / [[false-positive-biosignatures]] interpretation.

## Notable Quotes

> "This discovery suggests that the categories astronomers currently use to describe small planets may be too simple." — [[harrison-nicholls]]

> "What's exciting is that we can use computer models to uncover the hidden interior of a planet we will never visit … it is possible to reconstruct the deep past of these alien worlds — and discover types of planets with no equivalent in our own Solar System." — [[raymond-pierrehumbert]]
