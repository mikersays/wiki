A **federally funded research and development center (FFRDC)** is a private, typically not-for-profit organization that performs research, systems engineering, and analysis for federal sponsors under long-term contract — work that cannot easily be met by in-house government staff or commercial vendors. FFRDCs receive privileged access to federal facilities, classified information, and government programs.

FFRDCs recur across this vault as operators of sensitive government work: [[mitre|The MITRE Corporation]], the [[center-for-naval-analyses|Center for Naval Analyses (CNA)]], the [[jhuapl|Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory (JHUAPL)]], and [[mit-lincoln-laboratory|MIT Lincoln Laboratory]].

## UAP-records accountability (2026)

Because of how FFRDCs operate, they are largely protected from public disclosure by **Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) exemptions**. Some officials caution that this lets FFRDCs potentially hold government information beyond the reach of public oversight — a concern central to Rep. [[eric-burlison]]'s May 2026 records inquiry to [[mitre]] ([[src-burlison-mitre-uap-records-2026-05]], [[src-burlison-mitre-press-release-2026-05]]). Burlison argues FFRDCs "cannot become private vaults for federal records" and must comply with the FY2024 NDAA [[uap-records-collection|UAP Records Collection]] transfer mandate.

The FY2024 NDAA [[uap-records-review-board|National Archives records mandate]] and the proposed [[uap-disclosure-act]] both contemplated reaching UAP materials held by private contractors; the original Senate provision sought recovery of UAP material "provided to private entities in the past and thereby hidden from Congress" ([[src-schumer-rounds-uap-colloquy-2023]]).
