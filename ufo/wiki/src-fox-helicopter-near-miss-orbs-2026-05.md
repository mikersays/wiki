## Summary

Fox News (Morgan Phillips, May 11 2026) zooms in on a single [[fbi|FBI]] Form 302 record inside the [[pursue|PURSUE]] Release 01 trove: a senior U.S. intelligence official's first-hand account of an aerial search in which "super-hot" infrared-bright objects came within ~10 feet of a helicopter, traveled ~20 miles at speeds the helicopter could not match, then changed direction and accelerated away. Witnesses reported the objects splitting into multiple lights, with groups of four or five glowing orbs flaring into view in repeated patterns over ~30 minutes. The piece is distinct from Fox's earlier [[src-trump-uap-apollo-photos-2026-05|Apollo-photos-focused PURSUE story]]. Skeptical framing comes from former [[aaro]] director [[sean-kirkpatrick]] ("playhouse politics theater company") via Scientific American. The article also folds in [[department-of-war]] release context — [[pete-hegseth]] quote, "football-shaped" East China Sea object, Iraq/Syria lights, Apollo astronaut communications — plus standard [[drone-incursions]] / Chinese surveillance-balloon framing.

## Key Claims

- An [[fbi|FBI]] Form 302 inside Release 01 documents a "super-hot" infrared-detectable UAP approaching a US helicopter to within ~10 feet during an aerial search.
- The object traveled ~20 miles at speeds the helicopter could not match, then abruptly changed direction and accelerated away.
- The witness was a senior US intelligence official among multiple federal and state personnel in a joint operation following earlier unexplained-lights sightings.
- Objects reportedly split into multiple lights; groups of four or five glowing orbs flared in repeated patterns for ~30 minutes.
- Helicopter pilots were recording but many sightings occurred above the aircraft, outside the camera field of view.
- The released record does not identify the specific facility/location or whether the helicopter was U.S. military or partner agency.
- [[sean-kirkpatrick]]: nothing "unexpected" in the release; absent analysis and context it will fuel "speculation, conspiracy and armchair pseudoscience, particularly from the playhouse politics theater company" (via Scientific American).
- [[pete-hegseth]]: records had "long fueled justified speculation" and "it's time the American people see it for themselves"; many documents are unresolved, not confirmed.
- White House framing: "latest UAP videos, photos, and original source documents… all in one place — no clearance required."
- Release also includes a "football-shaped" East China Sea object, erratic lights over Iraq and Syria, and Apollo-era astronaut communications about strange objects and bright fragments near the moon.
