The **abiotic baseline** is the background level of abiotic processes — isotope fractionation, racemization, organic synthesis — against which any [[biosignatures|biosignature]] must be evaluated on a given world.

Higgins et al. (2026) demonstrate using [[enceladus]] as a case study that uncertainties in abiotic carbon-isotope cycling and amino acid racemization currently prevent definitive life detection. Critically, neglecting the abiotic baseline risks **false negative** life-detection claims (missed life), not just [[false-positive-biosignatures|false positives]]. Reducing this uncertainty requires complementary geophysical constraints: internal temperatures (~10-100 C), rheology, lithology, initial organic inventory, and ocean transport timescales. See [[src-ocean-worlds-biosignature-framework-2026-05]].

The concept is central to mission planning for [[europa-clipper]], [[invow|InvOW]], and any future [[enceladus]] lander.
