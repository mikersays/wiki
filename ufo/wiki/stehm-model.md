**Smaller Than Earth Habitability Model (STEHM)** — 2026 framework introduced by [[michelle-l-hill]] et al. ([[uc-riverside|UC Riverside]]) that couples atmospheric escape (Jeans escape under XUV irradiation) with interior thermal evolution (stagnant-lid cooling and volcanic outgassing) to set a **~0.8 Earth-radii lower size limit** for habitable-zone exoplanets.

## Key numbers

- ≥**0.8 R⊕** → atmosphere retained for billions of years.
- ~**0.7 R⊕** → sharp drop-off in retention.
- **0.6 R⊕** → ~400 Myr atmosphere lifetime.
- **0.5 R⊕** → ~30 Myr.

## Mechanisms

1. **Atmospheric escape** — low gravity / low escape velocity drives Jeans escape under stellar XUV.
2. **Interior cooling** — high surface-area-to-volume ratio drives rapid cooling, lithospheric thickening, and shutdown of volcanic outgassing.

## Assumptions and exceptions

- Assumes **stagnant-lid tectonics** (no plate tectonics) and a **CO₂-dominant atmosphere** — a best case for retention.
- Three rare exception channels for sub-0.8 R⊕ worlds: (1) large initial carbon budget; (2) low core-radius fraction (large mantle / no core); (3) "cold start" mantle that delays outgassing until host-star XUV declines.
- Does **not** include magnetic-field shielding as an ingredient.

## Implications

- Target-list pruning for [[habitable-worlds-observatory|HWO]] and JWST programs.
- Complements [[src-interplanetary-habitable-zone-2026|Interplanetary Habitable Zone]] framework (Scharf 2026): IHZ filters by system-scale civilizational sustainability, STEHM by per-planet atmospheric retention.
- Reinforces M-dwarf pessimism alongside [[src-oxygen-false-positive-biosignatures-2026-03]] photochemical caveats.
