**Mars caves** — lava tubes, pits, and other Martian subsurface voids — are radiation-shielded environments of high astrobiological interest. They preserve volatiles, shelter potential biosignatures from cosmic-ray flux, and offer candidate habitats for both microbial life and future human missions.

At [[abscicon26]] ([[src-abscicon26-media-tips-2-2026-05]]) the topic anchored multiple sessions:

- [[roger-wiens]] (Purdue) proposed a heli-drone concept to explore Mars lava-tube caves.
- [[jennifer-blank]] (NASA Ames/BAERI) led a session on robots specifically designed to spelunk Mars caves for biosignatures.
- A dedicated session, "Planetary Caves and Voids as Targets for Astrobiology Science I," anchored the cluster.
