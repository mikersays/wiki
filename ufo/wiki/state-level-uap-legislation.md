# State-Level UAP Legislation

**State-level UAP legislation** tracks U.S. state (subnational) efforts to establish UAP research programs, distinct from federal disclosure legislation tracked in [[uap-disclosure]].

## Enacted

| State | Law | Effective | Provision |
|-------|-----|-----------|-----------|
| New Jersey | UAP Research Center Act | January 2026 | Partners higher-education institutions with state agencies to study UAP |

## Proposed

| State | Bill | Status | Provision |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|
| Connecticut | H.B. 5422 | Appropriations Committee hearing March 12, 2026 | Directs UConn to partner with state agencies to study UAP and explore a dedicated state center |

Connecticut would become the second state to legislate UAP study if H.B. 5422 passes. The bill has bipartisan support from [[joe-hoxha]] (R) and [[aundre-bumgardner]] (D). See [[src-connecticut-uap-disclosure-bill-2026]].

## Context

State-level efforts emerged alongside the 2024 New Jersey drone wave and federal [[uap-disclosure]] momentum. They complement but do not duplicate federal programs like [[aaro]], instead focusing on localized data collection, public reporting mechanisms, and academic research partnerships.
