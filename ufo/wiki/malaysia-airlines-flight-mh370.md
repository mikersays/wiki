**Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370** was a Boeing 777-200ER that vanished on March 8, 2014 during a routine flight from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing with 239 people aboard (mostly Chinese nationals). It remains one of aviation's greatest unsolved mysteries.

## Disappearance

The aircraft lost contact with air traffic control less than an hour after takeoff. Military radar tracked it deviating westward across the Malay Peninsula and out over the Andaman Sea. Inmarsat satellite "handshake" data placed the final location somewhere along an arc in the southern Indian Ocean. Malaysia's 2018 investigative report drew no conclusion on the cause but did not rule out deliberate course deviation.

## Search Efforts

| Phase | Years | Area Covered | Cost | Outcome |
|-------|-------|-------------|------|---------|
| Joint Australia/Malaysia/China | 2014–2017 | ~120,000 km² | ~$155M | No wreckage found |
| [[ocean-infinity]] Phase 1 | 2018 | ~100,000 km² | No-find-no-fee | No wreckage found |
| [[ocean-infinity]] Phase 2 | Mar 2025 + Dec 2025–Jan 2026 | ~7,571 km² (of 15,000 km² target) | $70M no-find-no-fee | No wreckage found |

According to [[src-mh370-ocean-infinity-conclusion-2026-03]], [[ocean-infinity]] spent 151 cumulative days at sea and mapped over 140,000 km² of seafloor across all campaigns. Only three confirmed wing fragments have ever been recovered, washed ashore on east African coastlines and Indian Ocean islands.

## Families and Advocacy

[[voice370]], representing victims' families, has persistently urged Malaysia to extend search contracts. According to [[src-mh370-families-extend-search-2026-03]], the group called for a simple addendum to Ocean Infinity's contract before its June 2026 expiry.

## UAP Discourse Connection

MH370 has been a recurring subject in UAP-adjacent online communities, most notably through viral videos purporting to show the aircraft being surrounded by orbs and "teleported." These videos were thoroughly debunked by late 2023 (stock cloud textures identified as VFX source, satellite timeline inconsistencies, daylight/nighttime mismatches). The claims were promoted primarily by Ashton Forbes via social media and the 4orbs.com website. No credible evidence connects MH370's disappearance to anomalous phenomena.
