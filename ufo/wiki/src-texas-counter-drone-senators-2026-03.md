## Summary

Following a closed-door briefing on March 4, 2026, bipartisan senators including [[ted-cruz]] (R-TX) and [[maria-cantwell]] (D-WA) criticized the [[department-of-war]], [[faa]], and [[customs-and-border-protection|CBP]] for poor interagency coordination during two back-to-back [[counter-uas|C-UAS]] incidents in Texas in mid-February 2026. In the first, CBP used a military laser on-loan from the Pentagon to shoot down an object near El Paso — later identified as Mylar balloons — causing the FAA to temporarily close airspace. In the second, troops shot down a CBP drone at [[fort-hancock]] that was launched without notifying the military task force on the U.S.-Mexico border. A senior defense official confirmed that [[jiatf-401]] would conduct a high-energy laser C-UAS test with the FAA the following weekend. Senators also drew parallels to the 2024 Reagan National Airport midair collision and criticized the Pentagon's withdrawal of support for the [[rotor-act|ROTOR Act]] air-safety bill.

## Key Claims

- The two Texas incidents revealed a "lack of coordination" between DOD, FAA, and CBP on counter-drone employment (Cruz and Cantwell)
- CBP used a Pentagon-loaned military laser to shoot down an object near El Paso that turned out to be Mylar balloons, triggering FAA airspace closure (article reporting)
- U.S. troops shot down a CBP drone near [[fort-hancock]] because CBP failed to notify the border military task force (article reporting)
- [[jiatf-401]] will conduct a high-energy laser C-UAS test with the FAA "this weekend" (anonymous senior defense official)
- The Pentagon withdrew support for the ROTOR Act and issued a press release Cruz called "objectively false" (Cruz)
- Cantwell plans to send a letter to Trump or the agencies on the coordination issue (Cantwell)
- Senators expressed doubt that similar incidents would be prevented going forward; Cantwell referenced the upcoming FIFA World Cup (Cantwell and Cruz)

## Notable Quotes

> "It's clear that there were real challenges in terms of operationalizing counter-drone technology, and I think all the senators in the briefing urged the Department of War and CBP and the FAA to significantly improve their coordination." — Sen. Ted Cruz

> "Everyone wants to see hostile drones that are a threat incapacitated, but obviously we want to do so in a way that doesn't pose a threat to civilian aircraft, and it ought to be possible to do both at the same time." — Sen. Ted Cruz

> "We already can see there's a lack of coordination between the FAA and DOD with the crash in the Potomac, and we need people at a higher level." — Sen. Maria Cantwell
