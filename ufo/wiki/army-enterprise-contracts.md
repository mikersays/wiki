**Army enterprise contracts** is the Army acquisition pattern, accelerated under [[brent-ingraham]] (ASA(ALT)) through 2025–2026, of consolidating disparate vendor contracts into firm-fixed-price IDIQ vehicles with pre-negotiated terms and à-la-carte ordering. Through early 2026 the Army awarded **14 enterprise contracts in eight months**, collapsing 118 prior disparate contracts (an 88% reduction).

Anchor awards:

- **[[anduril]] — $20B / 10 years (March 2026)**: covers the proprietary, open-architecture, AI-enabled [[lattice-c2]] suite plus integrated hardware, data, infrastructure, and technical support. First task order: $87M for [[jiatf-401]] C-UAS C2 (see [[src-anduril-army-20b-counter-drone-idiq-2026-03]]).
- **[[palantir]] — $10B (August 2025)**: precedent enterprise software vehicle.

The pattern is significant for [[counter-uas]] doctrine because it shifts C2-software acquisition from project-by-project contracting to a Pentagon-wide ordering ceiling, materially reducing procurement friction for [[jiatf-401]] and other C-UAS users.
