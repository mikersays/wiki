A **confirmed conspiracy theory** is a once-speculative theory later substantiated by documentary or empirical evidence. The framework is attributed to Parker (2024) and is applied to UFO disinformation campaigns in [[src-uap-reddit-disclosure-conspiracy-2026-05]].

## UAP-Relevant Examples

Bastos & Duarte (2026) argue that several UFO-related government operations qualify:

- **[[robertson-panel]]** (1953) — CIA-convened panel that recommended debunking UFO sightings; later documented
- **[[richard-doty]] / AFOSI operations** (1980s) — acknowledged feeding of disinformation to UFO researchers
- **[[majestic-12]] document forgeries** — fabricated documents used to manipulate beliefs and deter researchers

## Broader Precedents

The paper draws parallels to other confirmed conspiracy theories:

- **Tuskegee Syphilis Experiment** — illicit medical testing shaped by racial discrimination
- **COINTELPRO** — government violations of civil rights as political repression
- **MKUltra** — CIA illicit medical testing of psychological manipulation
- **NSA Surveillance Programs** — revealed by Snowden's disclosures

## Significance

Parker (2024) argues that empirical validation of certain conspiracy theories requires academic reassessment: pathologizing all conspiracy thinking obscures legitimate critiques of institutional power, particularly when intelligence agencies are the purveyors of disinformation that later gains official acknowledgment. This feeds directly into the [[uap-stigma]] that inhibits academic research on UAP.
