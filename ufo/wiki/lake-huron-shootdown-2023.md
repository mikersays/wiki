The **Lake Huron Shootdown** of February 12, 2023 was the AIM-9X Sidewinder downing of an unidentified high-altitude object over Lake Huron by a USAF F-16C with callsign [[aesir11]]. The event came within a broader February 2023 wave of U.S./Canadian shootdowns of high-altitude objects following the Chinese spy-balloon incident.

In an April 27, 2026 presentation at the [[center-for-naval-analyses]], former [[aaro]] director [[sean-kirkpatrick]] characterized the Lake Huron object — and the broader early-2023 shootdown series — as hobbyist balloons.

The AESIR11 in-cockpit footage was previewed for [[pursue]] Release 02 ([[src-debrief-pursue-next-batch-preview-2026-05]]) and **actually released on May 22, 2026** as part of Release 02 — per [[newsweek|Newsweek]] (Castro & Robinson, [[src-newsweek-pursue-release-02-2026-05]]), the footage is framed as "**one of the clearest military engagement clips**" in the PURSUE archive, assembled by [[aaro]] in response to lawmaker requests. The release closes a long-standing [[the-war-zone|War Zone]] non-release critique ([[src-twz-pursue-shrug-2026-05]]).
