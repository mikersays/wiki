The **UAP Records Collection** is the national archive of UAP records established by the FY2024 NDAA, housed at [[nara|NARA]] as **Record Group 615**. Federal agencies — and, per Rep. [[eric-burlison]], contractors and [[ffrdc|FFRDCs]] such as [[mitre|MITRE]] and [[mit-lincoln-laboratory|MIT Lincoln Laboratory]] — are directed to identify, review, digitize, and transfer appropriate UAP records to NARA for public release ([[src-burlison-mitre-press-release-2026-05]], [[src-burlison-mitre-uap-records-2026-05]]).

It is the surviving "National-Archives-only" outcome of the [[uap-disclosure-act]] after the [[uap-records-review-board|UAP Records Review Board]] and the recovered-material / eminent-domain provisions were stripped in the FY2024 NDAA conference ([[src-schumer-rounds-uap-colloquy-2023]]). Burlison's 2026 FFRDC inquiries ([[mitre]], [[mit-lincoln-laboratory]]) operationalize the transfer mandate by extending records-preservation demands to private contractors — running parallel to the executive-branch [[pursue|PURSUE]] disclosure rollout.
