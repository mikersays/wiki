## Summary

On the 12th anniversary of [[malaysia-airlines-flight-mh370]]'s disappearance, families of the 239 people aboard urged the Malaysian government to extend [[ocean-infinity]]'s "no find, no fee" search contract before it expires in June 2026. Malaysia's Air Accident Investigation Bureau reported that two search phases covering 28 days and ~7,571 km² of Indian Ocean seabed had yielded no findings. [[voice370]], the families' advocacy group, warned that southern-hemisphere winter weather would likely prevent Ocean Infinity from resuming before the contract lapses and called for a simple addendum extending the agreement.

## Key Claims

- Two search phases (ending Jan 23, 2026) scanned ~7,571 km² of seabed with no findings (Malaysia AAIB)
- [[ocean-infinity]]'s contract operates on a "no find, no fee" basis with a $70 million payout only upon successful location of wreckage (Reuters)
- Southern-hemisphere winter makes a resumed search before the June contract expiry unlikely ([[voice370]])
- [[voice370]] urges extending the contract and opening the same terms to other exploration firms
- Malaysia's 2018 investigative report drew no conclusion on the cause but did not rule out deliberate course deviation

## Notable Quotes

> "A simple addendum extending the contract period without altering the core terms of the agreement would allow the search to continue without delay." — Voice370

> "The government remains committed to keeping the families informed and will continue to provide updates as appropriate." — Malaysia AAIB
