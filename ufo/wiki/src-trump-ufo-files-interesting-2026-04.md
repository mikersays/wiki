## Summary

NBC News coverage of [[donald-trump]]'s April 17, 2026 [[turning-point-usa]] Phoenix remarks promising imminent release of "very interesting documents" on UFOs by the [[department-of-war]]. The article corroborates Newsweek's account in [[src-trump-tpusa-ufo-tease-2026-04]] but adds three distinct elements: (1) it opens by citing [[lue-elizondo]]'s Congressional testimony that UAPs are "splitting aircraft formations right down the middle"; (2) it surfaces [[tim-burchett]]'s public thanks to Trump on X for "keeping your word"; and (3) it juxtaposes Trump's tease against a Friday-released DoD intelligence report stating the Pentagon "did not have sufficient data" on UAP spotted from 2004 to 2021, plus prior Pentagon findings (Nov 2024, March 2024) of no ET evidence and no cover-up. NBC also notes [[anna-paulina-luna]] reacting with a winking emoji and considering subpoena power after the [[src-pentagon-uap-deadline-missed-2026-04|April 14 deadline]] lapsed.

## Key Claims

- Trump told the TPUSA Phoenix crowd that DoD would release "very interesting documents" on UFOs "very, very soon."
- [[lue-elizondo]] testified at a Wednesday Congressional hearing that UAPs are in some cases "splitting aircraft formations right down the middle" (NBC framing).
- Trump in February directed [[pete-hegseth]] and other agency officials to begin reviewing files on "alien and extraterrestrial life."
- Trump's February announcement followed [[barack-obama]]'s podcast comments that aliens were real, which Obama later softened to "the odds are good there's life out there."
- Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna|Luna]]'s March letter demanded 46 UAP videos by April 14; after the deadline she said she is considering subpoena authority.
- Rep. [[tim-burchett|Burchett]] reposted Trump's clip with the caption "Thank you for keeping your word to me @realDonaldTrump."
- Pentagon Nov 2024: hundreds of UAP reports, 21 worthy of "further analysis" for "anomalous characteristics and/or behaviors"; no ET life substantiated.
- Pentagon March 2024 historical review: no credible evidence of U.S. cover-up; most sightings misidentifications.
- Friday DoD intelligence report stated insufficient data on UAP spotted 2004–2021.
- Trump has not stated he believes UAP are of alien origin (NBC characterization).

## Notable Quotes

> "As you remember, I recently directed the Secretary of War ... to begin releasing government files relating to UFOs and unexplained aerial phenomenon. This process is well underway, and we found many very interesting documents, I must say, and the first releases will begin very, very soon." — Donald Trump, TPUSA Phoenix

> "The continued lack of transparency surrounding these anomalies and the potential national security threat they pose is troubling." — Rep. Anna Paulina Luna, March letter
