# Trump Admin Releases UAP Files with Apollo 12 & 17 Photos (Fox News)

## Summary

Fox News coverage of the May 8, 2026 [[department-of-war]] UAP file release, with the lede on [[apollo-12]] and [[apollo-17]] lunar photos and an Apollo 17 voice transcript describing "jagged angular fragments" / "Fourth of July out of Ron's window." The release is identified as the first tranche of [[donald-trump]]'s [[pursue]] program. Files are hosted at WAR.GOV/UFO. Fox's framing is notably enthusiastic and credulous — full quotation of the Apollo transcript, no skeptic counter-voice — versus measured CBS coverage ([[src-uap-files-rollout-interagency-2026-05]]) and skeptical AP/WaPo framing ([[src-uap-files-public-conclusions-2026-05]]).

## Key Claims

- The release is "the first of a series" under [[pursue]].
- Photos show "strangely shaped objects" captured during the Apollo 12 and Apollo 17 missions, including a lunar-surface image with "a cluster of three tiny dots in the sky."
- Apollo 17 transcript fragments: "very bright particles or fragments… drifting by"; "a whole bunch of big ones on my window down there… looks like the Fourth of July out of Ron's window"; "very jagged, angular fragments that are tumbling." ("Ron" = Ronald Evans, Apollo 17 Command Module Pilot.)
- A separate FBI-sourced pair of photos shows UAPs alongside U.S. aircraft on **New Year's Eve 1999**.
- Background: After [[barack-obama]]'s February 2026 alien comments, [[peter-doocy]] asked Trump directly; Trump accused Obama of leaking "classified information," then announced declassification on Truth Social naming the Secretary of War as lead.
