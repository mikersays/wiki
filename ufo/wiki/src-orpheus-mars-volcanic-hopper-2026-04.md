## Summary

Phys.org profiles **Orpheus**, a proposed Mars VTOL hopper mission conceived by [[connor-bunn]] and [[pascal-lee]] of the [[seti-institute]] and presented at the 57th [[lunar-and-planetary-science-conference]]. The mission would explore volcanic fissures, pits, and cave vents in the Cerberus Fossae region of Elysium Planitia — terrain inaccessible to wheeled rovers — searching for [[biosignatures]] of extant life. The hopper builds on the flight heritage of NASA's Ingenuity helicopter and would carry a color camera, near-infrared spectrometer, ground-penetrating radar, and a dedicated biosignature detector. Its traverse ends at Cerberus Tholus 1, a small dome volcano whose summit vents — especially "Vent #5," marked by a 400-meter dark streak — represent the most recent volcanic activity and the most promising targets for subsurface material shielded from radiation.

## Key Claims

- Cerberus Fossae hosts the youngest known volcanoes and lava flows on Mars, including a pyroclastic deposit from an eruption 46,000–222,000 years ago (Bunn & Lee)
- Volcanic vents are superior astrobiology targets compared to lava-tube skylights because vents provide sustained heat and volatile circulation, including water vapor (Bunn & Lee)
- "Vent #5" on Cerberus Tholus 1 features a 400-meter uphill dark streak that may be fresh volcanic material or wind-excavated subsurface material (Bunn & Lee)
- Finding extant life on another planet is the single highest priority of astrobiology because it enables protein and genetic analyses to rule out meteorite-transfer origins (Tomaswick, framing)
- The mission has no current funding or adoption path (Tomaswick, editorial)

## Notable Quotes

> "In what might be the best naming reference for a space mission ever, Orpheus is named after the Greek hero who tamed the three-headed hound Cerberus to gain access to the Underworld." — Andy Tomaswick
