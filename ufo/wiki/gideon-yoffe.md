# Gideon Yoffe

Postdoctoral researcher at the [[weizmann-institute-of-science]] in Rehovot, Israel; first author of the May 2026 *Nature Astronomy* paper proposing a statistical [[agnostic-biosignature]] method built on amino-acid and fatty-acid distribution metrics ([[src-alien-biology-detection-method-2026-05]]). Background in statistics and data science; previously applied diversity metrics to ancient human cultures before reusing them for astrobiology.

## Key contributions

- Co-developer (with [[fabian-klenner]]) of a richness-and-evenness statistical classifier distinguishing biological from abiotic molecular pools.
- Frames astrobiology as a "forensic science" working from incomplete, rare, expensive data.

## Notable quotes

> "Astrobiology is fundamentally a forensic science. We're trying to infer processes from incomplete clues, often with very limited data collected by missions that are extraordinarily expensive and infrequent." — Yoffe, UC Riverside News (May 11 2026)
