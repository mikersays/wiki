## Summary

GovConWire reports that drone-maker **Perennial Autonomy** has received a potential **$500 million** three-year IDIQ contract from [[jiatf-401]] to support enterprise-wide counter-UAS operations. The award expands a prior **$5.2M February 2026** Bumblebee V2 agreement and covers three Perennial product lines already fielded with [[centcom]]: [[merops-interceptor|Merops]] interceptors, [[bumblebee-counter-uas|Bumblebee]] quadcopters, and **Hornet** midrange strike drones. The [[department-of-war]] frames the award as advancing low-cost drone interceptors that combine computer vision, RF sensing, and secure comms while keeping operators in the loop on lethal-force decisions. The contract is positioned as a procurement accelerator for the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]] and the broader counter-drone layered-defense doctrine.

## Key Claims

- JIATF-401 awarded [[perennial-autonomy]] a potential $500M three-year IDIQ contract for enterprise C-UAS support (attributed to [[department-of-war]]).
- The contract covers [[merops-interceptor]], [[bumblebee-counter-uas]], and [[hornet-strike-drone]] — all already in use within [[centcom]] AOR.
- JIATF-401 had previously awarded Perennial Autonomy a separate **$5.2M February 2026** agreement for Bumblebee V2 operational counter-drone evaluations with rapidly deployable forces.
- Systems include detection, tracking, and targeting via **computer vision**, **RF sensing**, and **secure communications**, with humans in control of lethal-force decisions.
- The award is meant to accelerate counter-drone procurement through the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]] and integrate sensors, effectors, and mission-command systems across the joint force.
