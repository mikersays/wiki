# Perennial Autonomy

U.S. counter-drone manufacturer founded by former Google CEO [[eric-schmidt|Eric Schmidt]]; primary contractor to [[jiatf-401]] for low-cost interceptor and short-range C-UAS effectors.

## Corporate history

Per [[src-dronexl-perennial-interceptor-doctrine-2026-05]], the company launched quietly as **White Stork** in 2023 — after 2022 meetings with Ukrainian officials to build the [[merops-interceptor|Merops]] interceptor for Ukrainian crews — was renamed **Project Eagle** in February 2024, then **Perennial Autonomy** in 2026. It recruited former Pentagon innovation chief [[will-roper|Will Roper]] plus Apple and SpaceX engineers. Merops saw heavy combat use in Ukraine (4,000+ intercepts since mid-2024) before any U.S. order. European Merops co-production runs through Munich-based [[twentyfour-industries]] (Lithuania bought 48 units in April 2026; Poland/Romania on NATO's eastern flank). Product portfolio (per [[src-perennial-autonomy-jiatf-401-500m-2026-05]]) spans three lines already fielded within [[centcom]] AOR:

- **[[merops-interceptor|Merops]]** — kinetic interceptor used against [[shahed-drone|Shahed-class]] one-way-attack UAS.
- **[[bumblebee-counter-uas|Bumblebee]] V1 / V2** — V1 kinetic effector and V2 FPV multi-rotor interceptor for follow-home / air-to-air defeat CONOPS.
- **[[hornet-strike-drone|Hornet]]** — midrange strike drone.

All Perennial systems combine **computer vision**, **RF sensing**, and **secure communications**, with humans retained on lethal-force decisions.

## JIATF-401 contract history

- **Feb 2026** — $5.2M Bumblebee V2 agreement for operational counter-drone evaluations with rapidly deployable forces.
- **May 19 2026** — Potential **$500M three-year IDIQ** for enterprise C-UAS support across the joint force; covers Merops, Bumblebee V1/V2, and Hornet; framed by [[department-of-war]] as a procurement accelerator for the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]].
