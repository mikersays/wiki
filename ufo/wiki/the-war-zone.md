**The War Zone** (twz.com) is a defense-focused news outlet known for investigative reporting on military aviation, emerging weapons, and anomalous aerial events. The outlet first documented the multi-year pattern of unexplained drone incursions over U.S. military and nuclear sites — including the December 2023 [[langley-afb|Langley]] swarm, the 2019 [[palo-verde-nuclear-plant|Palo Verde]] incursion, and the March 2026 [[barksdale-afb|Barksdale]] waves. Recurring bylines on the UAP/drone beat include **Howard Altman** (drone incursions) and **[[joseph-trevithick]]** (UAP-disclosure skepticism).

The War Zone's reporting is routinely cited by mainstream outlets (including [[src-60-minutes-drone-swarms-2025|CBS 60 Minutes]]) as the primary open-source chronicle of homeland C-sUAS events.

## Recurring critique on UAP disclosure

[[joseph-trevithick]]'s coverage of the May 8, 2026 [[pursue]] launch ([[src-twz-pursue-shrug-2026-05]]) revives a standing TWZ argument: the U.S. military releases sensor imagery quickly when narratively convenient (e.g., the 2023 China spy balloon, Iranian rocketry footage) but slow-walks UAP-related releases — pointing in particular to the **still-withheld imagery from the 2023 shootdowns of three unidentified objects over the U.S. and Canada**.
