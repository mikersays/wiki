## Summary

Primary-source U.S. Army press release announcing that **Australia, Poland, and the Republic of Korea** have signed agreements to procure [[counter-uas]] technologies through the [[jiatf-401-marketplace]]. This builds on earlier agreements with the [[united-kingdom]] and [[romania]] ([[src-jiatf-401-marketplace-romania-2026-04]]), bringing the named partner-nation total to five — halfway to the Army secretary's goal of 25 partners by end of summer 2026.

## Key Claims

- [[jiatf-401]] is described as the [[department-of-war]]'s "premier organization to synchronize C-UAS efforts across the Joint Force and interagency" (Army)
- The marketplace connects vetted C-UAS vendors with an expanding allied-partner network seeking "scalable, effective, and interoperable technologies" (Army)
- Named partner-nation count now stands at five: UK, Romania, Australia, Poland, South Korea (Army)

## Notable Quotes

> "Our mission includes working with international partners to aggregate demand for counter-drone capabilities." — Maj. [[matt-mellor]]

> "We understand that our allies and partners want to purchase American-made counter-drone technologies. The JIATF-401 marketplace helps aggregate that demand, ensuring our defense industrial base is ready to scale production and meet the growing needs of our coalition." — Brig. Gen. [[matt-ross]]
