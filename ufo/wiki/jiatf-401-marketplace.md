The **JIATF-401 Marketplace** is an Army-run procurement marketplace operated by [[jiatf-401]] that connects vetted [[counter-uas]] capability vendors with users — initially DoD, now expanding to NATO-allied partner nations.

Named partner nations (as of April 2026):

- **[[united-kingdom]]** — first partner addition.
- **[[romania]]** — second partner addition (April 2026; see [[src-jiatf-401-marketplace-romania-2026-04]]).

The Army secretary's stated goal is to expand marketplace access to **25 partner nations by end of summer 2026**. Brig. Gen. [[matt-ross]] (JIATF-401 director) and Maj. [[matt-mellor]] (lead acquisitions specialist) are the public spokesmen for the program.

The marketplace pairs with [[army-enterprise-contracts]] (e.g. the $20B [[anduril]] IDIQ for [[lattice-c2]]) as twin pillars of a federated C-UAS procurement architecture: the IDIQ scales DoD orders against vetted vendors, the marketplace scales allied access to the same capability set.

## April 2026 — Stateside-events + state/local LE dimension

Per Army Secretary [[dan-driscoll]]'s April 16 2026 House Defense Appropriations testimony ([[src-army-counter-drone-marketplace-stateside-2026-04]]):

- **Cumulative marketplace sales:** ~**$13M** of C-UAS tech moved through the site since launch.
- **Buyers:** Southern-border task force, [[centcom|CENTCOM]] (procurement channel for [[operation-epic-fury]]), and "individual services with homeland defense missions."
- **Catalog:** ~12 C-UAS systems and **>1,600 items** including spare parts; the breadth is designed to compress acquisition lead times.
- **Eligibility expanded** to **state, local, and federal law enforcement**, with purchases already on the books. 350 state/local police departments have been brought through [[joint-base-myer-henderson-hall]] to sync them in to the marketplace.
- **Shared "sight picture":** all marketplace users agree to a common sight picture for cross-jurisdiction drone monitoring; DefenseScoop notes the technical implementation is not yet disclosed.
- **Stateside high-profile events:** marketplace tooling is being routed to harden the **FIFA World Cup 2026** ($100M committed, 11 cities / 9 states), **America 250** celebrations, and the **2028 LA Summer Olympics**. [[tom-karako]] frames the new normal as "Super Bowl-style bubbles over lots of things all year round."
- The doctrinal motivation cited is [[operation-spider-web]] (2025 Ukrainian truck-launched drone attack on Russian strategic aircraft).

The marketplace now spans **three lines of effort**: (1) DoD-internal procurement (CENTCOM, southern border, homeland-defense services), (2) overseas partner-nation procurement ([[united-kingdom]], [[romania]], target 25 nations by end-summer 2026), and (3) stateside event protection / state-local-federal LE eligibility.
