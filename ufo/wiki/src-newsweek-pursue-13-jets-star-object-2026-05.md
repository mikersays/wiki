## Summary

Newsweek reports that the [[disclosure-foundation]] released more than 300 pages of [[nsa]] intelligence reports on UFO sightings obtained through a FOIA request and subsequent appeal. The documents, many formerly classified as **[[top-secret-umbra]]** (a designation for highly sensitive signals intelligence), describe objects including a star-shaped craft performing rapid vertical maneuvers, a spherical or disc-like UFO brighter than the sun, and an object emitting a 22-meter spiral luminous radiation pattern. The release follows [[donald-trump]]'s [[pursue]] file releases earlier in May 2026. The article also recaps key content from [[pursue]] Release 01, including [[apollo-11]] crew debriefings, the [[wladyslaw-krasuski]] 1944 account, and a June 2024 USNORTHCOM video of an object with a vertical pole.

## Key Claims

- The [[disclosure-foundation]] obtained 300+ pages of NSA UFO intelligence reports via FOIA request and appeal after the NSA initially denied access (Disclosure Foundation)
- Many records carried "TOP SECRET UMBRA" classification — a designation used for highly sensitive signals intelligence (Disclosure Foundation)
- One document describes 13 fighter jets scrambled to chase a UFO (released NSA files)
- A star-shaped object performed rapid vertical motions at high altitude with white-bluish luminous light and was described as "impossible to be an aircraft" (released NSA files)
- A spherical or disc-like UFO was described as brighter than the sun and about half the moon's diameter (released NSA files)
- An object emitted "luminous radiation of 22 meters extending in a spiral pattern from a black center" (released NSA files)
- The NSA's appeals authority determined the initial blanket denial was improper, leading to the release (Disclosure Foundation)
- [[hunt-willis]], the Disclosure Foundation's chief legal officer, states the NSA still asserts "considerable redactions and exemptions" on UAP material dating to the 1960s and pledges legal challenges (Hunt Willis)
