## Summary

A feature-length Christian Science Monitor piece situating the Trump administration's May 8 [[pursue|PURSUE]] file release within the broader history of American UFO belief and the debate over government transparency. The article balances the administration's "radical transparency" framing against critics who call it ironic given Trump's prior records-obstruction record. It foregrounds Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]]'s role as Task Force chair, her "interdimensional beings" claim on [[joe-rogan]]'s podcast, and Senior Chief [[alexandro-wiggins]]'s testimony about a 2023 California [[transmedium-uap|transmedium]] Tic Tac encounter. Rep. [[tim-burchett]] appears on Rogan's podcast the same day the files drop, vouching for Trump's sincerity but doubting the files will change minds.

## Key Claims

- [[pete-hegseth]] wrote on the DoW website that the 161 files "hidden behind classification, have long fueled justified speculation" (Hegseth)
- Luna told Rogan she has seen evidence of "interdimensional beings" and urged the public to read the Book of Enoch — echoing [[src-religious-republicans-uap-files-2026-05]] (Luna)
- [[alexandro-wiggins]] testified about a glowing Tic Tac-shaped object off California in 2023 that rose from the ocean and linked with three similar objects before departing (Wiggins)
- Skeptic [[joseph-uscinski]] (U. of Miami) notes that UFO belief has persisted for decades and unidentified sightings do not justify an alien conclusion (Uscinski)
- Critics frame the release as a potential distraction from the Iran war, Epstein saga, and economic headwinds — aligning with the [[disclosure-as-distraction]] thesis (article)
