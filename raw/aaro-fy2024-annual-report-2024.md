# Pentagon's AARO Unveils Annual UAP Report; New Sensor System Deployed

**By:** Brandi Vincent
**Date:** November 14, 2024
**Publication:** DefenseScoop
**URL:** https://defensescoop.com/2024/11/14/uap-aaro-chief-unveils-pentagon-annual-caseload-analysis-new-efforts/

## Key Findings from FY2024 Report

AARO Director Jon Kosloski presented the Pentagon's annual Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena analysis, revealing significant progress in managing the growing caseload. The office has received 1,600+ UAP reports total, with 757 new submissions between May 2023 and June 2024.

The office successfully resolved "hundreds of cases" to conventional explanations like balloons, birds, drones, satellites, and aircraft. However, over 900 reports currently lack sufficient scientific data for definitive analysis and remain in the active archive for future investigation.

## Notable Incident Resolutions

A significant breakthrough involved identifying multiple sightings as SpaceX Starlink satellite flares. Kosloski explained: "We found some of those correlations in time, the direction that they were looking and the location" that enabled definitive identification of previously mysterious observations.

## Flight Safety Concerns

The report documented two flight safety concerns reported by military aircrews, plus three incidents where pilots described being "trailed or shadowed by UAP."

## Gremlin Sensor System

AARO's prototype detection system, Gremlin, is conducting a 90-day baseline collection at an undisclosed national security site. The deployment aims to establish what constitutes "normal" activity near sensitive locations before expanding to other U.S. regions.

## Extraterrestrial and Adversary Assessment

Kosloski confirmed the office has "discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity or technology," nor verified indications that foreign adversaries possess breakthrough UAP capabilities.
