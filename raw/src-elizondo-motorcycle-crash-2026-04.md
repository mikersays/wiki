# UFO Whistleblower, Lue Elizondo Survives Deadly Motorcycle Crash

Luis "Lue" Elizondo, the prominent U.S. government whistleblower on UAP/UFO topics and former head of the Pentagon's Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), was involved in a serious motorcycle accident on the evening of St. Patrick's Day, March 17, 2026.

## Timeline of the Incident

The crash occurred approximately one mile from his home in Wyoming while riding home from his business at night. Elizondo encountered a doe and her fawn standing in the road. He made a split-second decision to swerve and avoid hitting the animals rather than colliding with them. He lost control of the motorcycle and crashed. He was not wearing a helmet.

Elizondo drifted in and out of consciousness by the roadside until his wife, Jennifer Elizondo, located him using the iPhone "Find My" app when he failed to arrive home. Despite his initial resistance to hospital care, she insisted on taking him to the emergency room. Initial public updates were shared around March 24, 2026, with more detailed accounts released on April 4, 2026.

## Injuries and Medical Response

The crash resulted in multiple life-threatening injuries, primarily affecting the left side of his face and body. Upon arrival at the trauma center, Elizondo was given less than a 50% chance of survival.

Key injuries included:
- Shattered rib cage with nearly all ribs broken
- Punctured and collapsed lung (one rib pierced it)
- Severed spleen requiring surgical removal
- Traumatic brain injury
- Nearly 32 facial and cranial fractures
- Dislocated hand and wrist
- Damaged/broken shoulder
- Sensory issues including hearing loss, vision changes including a "green veil," floaters, and difficulty focusing

He was life-flighted to a shock trauma center, where he spent approximately one week before being released around April 3, 2026. Surgery included spleen removal and interventions addressing the lung, fractures, and dislocations.

## Current Recovery Status

As of early April 2026, Elizondo is resting at home but reports experiencing tremendous ongoing pain, difficulty sleeping due to internal injuries and rib compression, and the sensory changes noted above. He began documenting his recovery in a video series titled "Lue Elizondo bike crash recovery diary 1," uploaded April 4, 2026, on his YouTube channel.

He has shared graphic photos of the crash scene and his immediate post-accident appearance on social media to illustrate the severity of injuries. He expresses deep gratitude for his survival, his wife's actions, and public support.

## Public Messages and Advice

Elizondo has framed the accident as a sobering reminder of how quickly circumstances can change. His core message emphasizes a strong public safety advisory regarding helmet use. He has repeatedly stressed variations of this message across social media platforms, noting that rural areas often lack helmet laws, making his choice especially risky.

"I had a choice. Make a split second decision. Do I plow into the deer and kill it or do I try to avoid it?" — Lue Elizondo

"Right now I am still [in] a tremendous amount of pain, but I was released yesterday from shock trauma." — Lue Elizondo (from YouTube recovery diary)

"for the love of God, don't be a Lue, and please wear a helmet" — Lue Elizondo (public safety advisory)

## Public and Community Reaction

The UFO and disclosure community has responded with widespread well-wishes, prayers, and support. Investigative journalist Ross Coulthart, who has closely followed Elizondo's career, publicly shared details and urged others to always wear a helmet.

An editor's note concludes: "We are relieved that Lue survived this terrible accident and is on the road to recovery. At the same time we extend our deepest admiration to Lue's wife Jennifer whose swift actions and presence of mind saved her husband's life."
