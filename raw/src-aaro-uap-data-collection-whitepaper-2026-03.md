# 2025 UAP Workshop: Narrative Data, Infrastructures, and Analysis

**Publisher:** All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)
**URL:** https://www.aaro.mil/Portals/136/PDFs/Information%20Papers/2025_UAP_Workshop_Paper.pdf
**Length:** ~17 pages
**Published:** Early 2026 (February or March 2026 per secondary outlets; surfaced publicly mid-March 2026)
**Workshop dates:** August 5-6, 2025, Washington, D.C. area
**Organizers:** Dr. Gretchen Stahlman and Dr. Tim Souck, Associated Universities Inc. (AUI)
**Sponsor:** AARO
**Attendance:** ~40 researchers from government, academia, and independent organizations; attendees covered their own travel costs; no public announcement before the event
**Governance:** Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval governed data collection and security for the workshop

## Document Title (as posted on aaro.mil)

"2025 UAP Workshop: Narrative Data, Infrastructures, and Analysis"

- **Gretchen Stahlman** (AUI) — moderator of the qualitative/quantitative panel

## Workshop Objectives

- Explore best practices for collecting, storing, and managing narrative UAP data
- Discuss methods for integrating data from disparate organizations
- Identify applications of advanced analytical tools, including AI, for large-scale UAP datasets
- Develop approaches for improving transparency and scientific rigor in UAP data handling
- Assess current UAP reporting systems and data repositories, and identify gaps in standardization/accessibility
- Build trust between government and civilian researchers
- Develop robust UAP data infrastructure

## Cross-Cutting Findings (quoted/paraphrased from the whitepaper)

### 1. Standards and reporting templates

"Effective progress requires clear standards and common reporting templates, with robust metadata" capturing time, location, provenance, morphology, and contextual/environmental details. Current reports arrive from "military logs, pilot records, civilian testimony, and social media — none of it formatted in a way that allows for meaningful comparison."

### 2. Data integration across military and civilian sources

"Linking across datasets — military and civilian, to include archival, environmental, and technical — must balance interoperability with privacy, ethical, and classification constraints."

### 3. Credibility assessment

"Credibility is best assessed through corroboration, but for efficiency there is a need for automated methods to filter reports and surface the most promising for investigation." Manual assessment does not scale to AARO's 2,000+ case backlog (approximately 1,000 of which reportedly lack sufficient data for analysis).

### 4. AI and machine learning

AI presents both opportunities and risks. AI can assist with "transcription, clustering, and large-scale pattern detection," but the workshop endorsed a "hybrid human-AI model with human oversight" to mitigate bias and hallucination.

### 5. Community engagement

AARO seeks a "multi-disciplinary and community-engaged approach to UAP narrative data."

## Analytical Approaches

The whitepaper concludes that "UAP narratives cannot be reduced to a single analytic approach." It distinguishes:

- **Corpus-level methods** — time/space clustering, keyword trends, statistical correlation, graph analysis; "useful for pattern detection and hypothesis generation"
- **Narrative/experiential methods** — phenomenology, discourse analysis; "useful for preserving meaning, cultural context, and witness voices"

"Infrastructures should allow these modes to coexist."

## Recommendations

**Standardized metadata templates** that:
- combine human expertise with AI tools
- leverage existing infrastructure
- support case triage
- integrate interviews and historical reports
- prioritize new high-quality data
- record time, location, morphology, provenance, and environmental conditions

**Specific data collection field recommendations** (examples cited by The Debrief / DefenseScoop):
- Ask witnesses to explain how they estimated size, distance, or speed
- Capture whether similar sightings have happened before and how often (recurrence)
- Record approximate numbers of witnesses rather than yes/no answers
- Include a field for whether the object seemed to react to observer presence

**Other recommendations:**
- Standardize reporting templates
- Improve military-civilian data sharing
- Deploy AI for processing large report volumes
- Preserve human oversight at every analytical stage

## Sensor Deployment Implication

The whitepaper notes that workshop findings "may influence how and where technical sensors are deployed" going forward — i.e., narrative-data pattern analysis could inform where to place quantitative sensors. Critics (quoted in UFONews coverage) flag this as meaning "a closed government meeting could determine what UAP evidence gets recorded."

## Context

- First known AARO effort to formally engage non-government researchers in shaping UAP research methodologies
- Published whitepaper is the most detailed public document AARO has released on its analytical challenges and approach to narrative data
- Event remained undisclosed for ~7 months until DefenseScoop's Brandi Vincent broke the story on 2026-03-16
