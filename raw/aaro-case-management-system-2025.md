# Pentagon's UAP Investigators Exploring New Options to Better Track and Manage Confidential Reports

**By:** Brandi Vincent
**Date:** May 21, 2025
**Publication:** DefenseScoop
**URL:** https://defensescoop.com/2025/05/21/ufo-uap-pentagon-aaro-exploring-new-options-track-manage-reports/

## Overview

The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) is seeking contractors to develop and maintain a secure, custom case management system designed to track unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) investigations on a classified network.

## The Solicitation

AARO released a sources sought notice this week requesting vendors capable of producing software for "a new effort" to manage UAP cases. A defense official confirmed this marks the first such solicitation since AARO's 2022 establishment. The system will operate on JWICS (Joint Worldwide Intelligence Communications System), the Department of Defense's top secret and sensitive compartmented information network.

The official stated: "AARO currently uses a variety of tools for its mission" and aims to "integrate them" through this unified platform.

## Technical Requirements

The proposed case management system must include several core capabilities:

- Categorizing UAP cases by type, severity, and priority
- Converting documents into structured data with auto-generated identifiers (weather, speed, location)
- Maintaining case action histories and status tracking from initiation through resolution
- Generating automated responses for case submissions
- Encrypting data according to sensitivity levels
- Offering customizable dashboards for different user roles

## AARO's Mission and Caseload

AARO is responsible for detecting, identifying, and attributing "spaceborne, airborne, and maritime objects of interest in or near national security areas." The office collects and manages reports from government officials and the general public about potential UAP encounters.

As of November 2024, AARO's caseload exceeded 1,600 reports, demonstrating substantial growth from earlier years when the office managed approximately 650-800 cases.

## Implementation Timeline

Interested contractors with active top secret clearances must submit capability statements by June 9. If the Pentagon proceeds with full acquisition, the project would involve:

- Software development services
- Fielding and certification on the AARO JWICS domain
- Personnel training
- Sustainment and ongoing functionality development

The performance period would cover one base year with options for four additional years. The defense official declined to disclose cost estimates.

## Significance

This acquisition supports AARO's expansion, particularly as the office prepares to launch a public UAP reporting mechanism. The structured case management system will enable better tracking of the office's "continuously expanding caseload" while meeting government records management requirements.
