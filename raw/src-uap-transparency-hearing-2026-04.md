# Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection

The House Oversight and Government Reform Committee's Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets held a hearing on September 9, 2025, titled "Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection." The hearing was chaired by Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-FL) and was the task force's first UAP-focused hearing. It began at 10:00 a.m. ET.

The hearing examined transparency issues surrounding the Department of Defense and the intelligence community, evaluated the effectiveness of the congressionally mandated All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), and analyzed ways Congress can better protect whistleblowers who come forward with information on UAPs.

## Witnesses

- **Jeffrey Nuccetelli** — U.S. Air Force veteran, former military police officer with 16 years of service
- **Dylan Borland** — U.S. Air Force veteran and UAP witness
- **Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins, U.S. Navy** — Active-duty Navy official with 23 years of service (the first active-duty Navy official to testify publicly before Congress about UAP)
- **George Knapp** — Investigative journalist covering UAP for decades
- **Joe Spielberger** — Senior policy counsel, Project on Government Oversight (POGO)

## Testimony Summaries

### Jeffrey Nuccetelli (Air Force Veteran)

Nuccetelli described multiple possible UAP incursions between 2003 and 2005 near Vandenberg Air Force Base in California, occurring during high-stakes National Reconnaissance Office launches. He described massive objects that appeared "like flying buildings" and moved in unusual patterns. He stated that "each incident was witnessed by multiple personnel, documented, investigated and reported up the chain." He called on lawmakers to fund independent research, end over-classification, and strengthen protections for witnesses: "Transparency is the foundation of truth. Without it, witnesses like us are dismissed. Protect the witnesses. Many stay silent out of fear for their careers, reputations, and the safety of their families. Protect them and you will embolden others to join this cause."

### Dylan Borland (Air Force Veteran)

Borland testified about facing severe retaliation after disclosing an alleged UAP experience at Langley Air Force Base in 2012. He claimed sustained reprisals spanning more than a decade: "Because of my direct knowledge of the reality of certain legacy UAP programs, my professional career was deliberately obstructed."

### Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins (Active-Duty U.S. Navy)

Wiggins described a 2023 incident aboard the USS Jackson in which a self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped object emerged from the ocean before linking up with three other similar objects. The objects disappeared with "high, synchronized, near-instantaneous acceleration" and left no sonic boom or conventional propulsion signatures.

"What I observed and what our crew recorded was not consistent with conventional aircraft or drones. A self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped object emerged from the ocean before linking up with three other similar objects."

He urged Congress to implement sensor standardization, personnel training, and reinforced witness protections, stressing: "Sailors need to know that reporting UAP encounters will not harm their careers."

He outlined three priorities for the committee: (1) aviation and maritime safety — objects maneuvering beyond known profiles near ships and aircraft are first and foremost a safety issue; (2) standardized checklists and training to ensure best possible sensor data capture in real time; and (3) reporting without stigma or retribution.

### George Knapp (Investigative Journalist)

Knapp called for continued congressional investigations, noting: "The secret is out. I mean, how many videos of UAP have there been?" He emphasized that the public has been told since the late 1940s there is "nothing to worry about here," while mysterious craft have been seen by millions.

### Joe Spielberger (Project on Government Oversight)

Spielberger advocated for strengthened disclosure channels and whistleblower protections, noting that exposed national security misconduct "often have a direct impact on public safety, civil liberties and the security of our country." He called on Congress to continue pushing for investigations and legislation to increase transparency.

## Key Hearing Developments

Rep. Eric Burlison (R-MO) unveiled video footage publicly for the first time depicting a U.S. military MQ-9 drone firing a Hellfire missile at a high-speed orb off Yemen's coast in October 2024. The object appeared to be struck but remained intact and continued on its course. Burlison stated: "It kept going, and it looked like the debris was taken with it," questioning why the information had remained restricted.

Rep. Luna emphasized the need for enhanced protections: "In general, we need more, stronger protections for everyone. But there is a different case in point for UAP whistleblowers specifically." She also stated: "The American people deserve maximum transparency from the federal government on sightings, acquisitions, and examinations of UAPs and whether they pose a potential threat to Americans' safety."

## Legislative Context

The hearing followed the introduction of the UAP Disclosure Act of 2025 by Rep. Eric Burlison on August 29, 2025, as an NDAA amendment, seeking government record preservation, National Archives access, and an independent declassification board.

The hearing also followed the November 2024 session where Rep. Nancy Mace introduced the *Immaculate Constellation* document into the Congressional Record, and the April 2025 public confirmation by Pentagon contractor Matthew Brown that he authored that covert reconnaissance report.

AARO, established in 2022, achieved full operational capacity in 2024 and maintains dozens of open investigations with limited public disclosure. The hearing examined whether AARO is adequately fulfilling its congressional mandate.
