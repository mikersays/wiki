## Overview

Throughout 2025, a sustained legislative and advocacy campaign has advanced UAP transparency efforts in Congress, culminating in new provisions within the **FY2026 National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA)** and laying the groundwork for dedicated whistleblower protection legislation. This summary consolidates reporting from multiple DefenseScoop articles covering the key developments across the year.

## Capitol Hill Advocacy -- March 2025

In late March 2025, former defense officials **Luis "Lue" Elizondo** and **Christopher Mellon** traveled to Capitol Hill for closed-door meetings with lawmakers. Elizondo, the former director of the Pentagon's **Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP)**, led a delegation that included himself, Mellon, two attorneys, and a security representative.

The group met with at least one senator and several House representatives, including **Anna Paulina Luna (R-FL)**, **Tim Burchett (R-TN)**, and **Eric Burlison (R-MO)** -- three lawmakers who have emerged as the leading congressional champions of UAP transparency.

### Key Arguments Made

Elizondo framed the UAP issue in the context of emerging drone technology threats, highlighting the difficulty of distinguishing modern drones from UAP sensor signatures. He noted: **"Drones can do now what in 2017 we could only dream of,"** citing advances in battery technology, lighter materials like carbon fiber, and improved motors.

He also questioned the resolution of recent mystery drone incidents: **"Why is it we haven't recovered a single one? Why is it we haven't found a single source, a single person [on the record] that is actually flying one of these things?"** This rhetorical question was designed to demonstrate that some unexplained aerial incidents resist conventional explanation even in an era of advanced drone proliferation.

Elizondo emphasized that transparency efforts require patience: **"Disclosure and transparency is a process -- it's not an event -- meaning you're going to be sadly disappointed if you think all the revelations are going to be provided all at once."** Despite this measured framing, he expressed optimism: **"I'm confident that next steps are not only going to follow, but I think we're going to see potentially even more progress this year than we have in the last seven."**

The advocates pushed for **administrative mechanisms** to compel intelligence and defense communities to share both classified and unclassified UAP-related information with appropriate congressional committees, as well as potential new legislation to advance records-release initiatives.

## New Legislation in Development -- May 2025

By May 2025, the three leading House Republicans -- **Burlison, Luna, and Burchett** -- announced they were engaged in **bipartisan, bicameral legislative efforts** to draft new UAP-related proposals. Their stated goal was to institutionalize greater accountability and transparency from federal agencies on UAP investigations.

Burlison characterized the effort as systemic rather than episodic: **"This is not a one-time thing... we're working on legislation that will put that into practice."**

### Planned Congressional Hearings

Luna announced **two upcoming hearings**:

1. A hearing focused on **government accountability**, featuring government appointees, military officials, and former military personnel
2. A hearing featuring a witness described as **"someone that wants to come forward in regards to a crash retrieval program"** -- a potentially explosive development suggesting a new whistleblower was prepared to testify about alleged crash recovery operations

### Expert Briefing (May 1, 2025)

A congressional briefing on May 1, 2025, featured presentations from several notable figures:

- **Professor Avi Loeb** (Harvard University) -- known for his work on the Galileo Project and investigation of anomalous interstellar objects
- **Christopher Mellon** -- former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Intelligence
- **Rear Admiral Tim Gallaudet (Ret.)** -- former head of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)

Gallaudet made the notable claim that **military personnel report numerous UAP sightings** and that **the Navy possesses substantial classified video evidence** that may be suitable for declassification. This claim from a retired flag officer carried particular weight given his former senior positions within both the Navy and NOAA.

### Whistleblower Protection Legislation

A key legislative priority was the creation of **codified protections for whistleblowers** who disclose information about government-funded UAP research programs. Existing whistleblower statutes have proven inadequate for personnel involved in classified special access programs, where the act of disclosure itself can constitute a criminal violation regardless of the whistleblower's intent. The proposed legislation would create distinct legal protections specifically for disclosures related to **"unidentified anomalous phenomenon material"** and associated government programs.

## FY2026 NDAA Provisions -- December 2025

The conferenced **fiscal year 2026 National Defense Authorization Act** included three significant UAP-related provisions, representing the most concrete legislative achievements of the year:

### 1. Congressional Briefing Requirement

The Pentagon is now required to **brief lawmakers on all military operations since 2004** involving UAP intercepts conducted by:
- **North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD)**
- **U.S. Northern Command (NORTHCOM)**

The director of the **All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)** must provide detailed information on the **"number, location, and nature"** of intercepts, including operational procedures, protocols, and all collected data. The 2004 start date is significant as it encompasses the famous **USS Nimitz "Tic Tac" encounter** and all subsequent military UAP incidents.

### 2. Classification Review

A directive requires AARO to **account for all UAP-related security classification guides**, addressing longstanding concerns about:
- **Overclassification** of UAP data
- **Improper restrictions** on the release of imagery and sensor data
- Potential use of classification authorities to prevent legitimate congressional oversight

This provision targets the specific mechanism by which UAP information has allegedly been kept from lawmakers and the public -- the classification system itself.

### 3. Streamlined Reporting

The third provision **eliminates duplicative reporting requirements**, streamlining processes by which federal agencies provide data to AARO's investigations. This administrative reform was designed to improve the efficiency of UAP data collection and reduce bureaucratic barriers to information sharing.

### Reaction

**Jordan Flowers**, Executive Director of the **Disclosure Foundation**, characterized the NDAA provisions as **"a meaningful, if incremental, step toward transparency and oversight."** This assessment reflected the view of many transparency advocates that while the provisions fell short of the more ambitious UAP Disclosure Act proposed in earlier congressional sessions, they nonetheless established important new oversight mechanisms.

## Elizondo's Upcoming Book

Throughout 2025, it emerged that **Luis Elizondo** has a new book in preparation titled **"Reckoning: The Unspoken Truth about UFOs and the Urgency of Now,"** which quietly appeared on publisher and retail sites with an expected **2026 release date**. Elizondo's first book, "Imminent," published in 2024, alleged that the U.S. military has been running UAP retrieval and reverse engineering programs and had recovered nonhuman specimens. The forthcoming book is expected to expand on these claims.
