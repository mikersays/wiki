# Explosive New Whistleblower Confirms "Immaculate Constellation" UAP Program
**Source:** https://www.abovethenormnews.com/2025/04/30/explosive-new-whistleblower-confirms-immaculate-constellation-uap-program/
**Author:** Above The Norm News staff
**Date:** April 30, 2025

## Overview

In April 2025, former Pentagon intelligence analyst **Matthew Brown** became the first government insider to publicly confirm the existence of a classified UAP surveillance program allegedly codenamed **"Immaculate Constellation."** Brown appeared on-camera in a multi-part interview series produced by investigative journalists **Jeremy Corbell** and **George Knapp**, marking a significant escalation in the ongoing UAP disclosure saga. The interview, titled "Immaculate Constellation -- UFO Whistleblower Goes Public," was released on YouTube and represented the first time a named, credentialed government official went on record about the program.

## Matthew Brown's Background and Credentials

Brown held multiple senior advisory positions within the U.S. national security apparatus, lending significant weight to his claims:

- **Policy Advisor** for the Office of the Secretary of Defense
- **Technical Advisor** for the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence & Security (OUSD(I&S))
- **Program Advisor** for the Department of State
- Holder of **Top Secret/Sensitive Compartmented Information (TS/SCI)** security clearance

These credentials place Brown within the upper echelons of Pentagon intelligence circles, with direct access to classified programs and compartmented information systems.

## The Immaculate Constellation Program

According to Brown and prior reporting, Immaculate Constellation was established as an **unacknowledged Special Access Program (SAP)** in the wake of the 2017 public exposure of the **Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP)** by former AATIP director **Luis Elizondo**. When Elizondo went public through a landmark New York Times article in December 2017, the Department of Defense allegedly created Immaculate Constellation as a successor or parallel effort to continue UAP data collection under tighter secrecy.

The program's core mission was described as a **consolidation effort** -- gathering UAP data from across the military and intelligence community's various collection platforms, both "tasked" (intentionally directed) and "untasked" (incidental captures). The program allegedly served as a **central or parent Unacknowledged Special Access Program (USAP)** that aggregated observations and distributed relevant intelligence to authorized officials.

The U.S. military and intelligence community database associated with the program reportedly includes videos and images captured using:

- Infrared sensors
- Forward-looking infrared (FLIR) systems
- Full-motion video (FMV)
- Still photography

## How Brown Discovered the Program

Brown described a remarkable chain of events leading to his awareness of the program. While managing files on a Pentagon server, he reportedly **accidentally encountered a classified 2018 Schriever War Game document** that contained program details and imagery related to Immaculate Constellation. The Schriever War Games are real, well-documented U.S. Space Command exercises designed to explore future space warfare scenarios, lending plausibility to the claim that such a document might exist within Pentagon file systems.

## Key Claims and Evidence

The discovered document allegedly contained several extraordinary elements:

- **Russian naval awareness of UAP:** References to Russian naval vessels near the **Kamchatka Peninsula** that appeared to anticipate the arrival of a "large black triangle" craft, suggesting that foreign powers possessed foreknowledge of UAP activities and movements.
- **Global UAP imagery:** The document allegedly included imagery of UAP incidents from locations worldwide, captured by multiple sensor platforms.
- **Cross-national pattern:** The implication that both U.S. and Russian military forces were tracking and studying the same phenomena independently.

Brown was careful to note the limits of what the evidence proved: "Nothing in those videos proves extraterrestrial or non-human... certainly anomalous, exotic, and unexplainable." This measured stance distinguished his claims from more sensational allegations, as he acknowledged the phenomena while declining to attribute them to any specific origin.

## Personal Risk and Motivation

Brown acknowledged the extreme personal risk of going public, stating: "My hope is that the stakes are not paid out. But they are life imprisonment and the possibility of execution." This reference to penalties under the Espionage Act and other classified information statutes underscored the seriousness of his decision.

He also expressed broader concerns about the implications of continued secrecy: "Look out at the world... We are not headed to a good future." This suggested his motivation extended beyond mere transparency to a belief that the information had significant implications for humanity's trajectory.

## Official Response

The **Department of Defense** has consistently denied the program's existence. Spokesperson **Sue Gough** stated: "The Department of Defense has no record, present or historical, of any type of SAP called 'IMMACULATE CONSTELLATION.'" This denial follows a pattern seen with other alleged classified programs, where official channels deny existence while insiders and documents suggest otherwise.

## Congressional Involvement

A report on Immaculate Constellation was allegedly submitted to Congress, and the program was referenced in documents associated with a **November 2024 House hearing** on UAP. Journalist **Michael Shellenberger** had previously presented information about the program at congressional proceedings, asserting that the U.S. executive branch had been managing UAP intelligence without proper Congressional oversight.

The **Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI)** released a FOIA-related document (DF-2025-00021) pertaining to Immaculate Constellation, described as originating from unclassified press materials dated October 22, 2024, further indicating that the intelligence community has at minimum acknowledged inquiries about the program's existence.

## Connection to Broader Disclosure Efforts

Brown's testimony aligns with and reinforces the claims made by **David Grusch**, the former intelligence official who testified before Congress in July 2023 about a "multi-decade UAP crash retrieval and reverse engineering program." While Grusch spoke about retrieval programs, Brown's claims focus on the surveillance and data-collection apparatus, suggesting a larger ecosystem of classified UAP-related activities within the U.S. government.

The interview was part of a broader investigative effort by Corbell and Knapp, who have been at the forefront of UAP journalism and have cultivated sources within the military and intelligence communities over many years.
