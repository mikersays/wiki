**TRAPPIST-1** is an ultra-cool red dwarf (M dwarf) star located approximately 39 light-years from Earth in the constellation Aquarius. It hosts seven known Earth-sized rocky planets — three of which ([[trappist-1-e]] among them) lie in the habitable zone — making it one of the most studied systems in the search for extraterrestrial life.

## Astrobiology Context

- TRAPPIST-1 planets are prime candidates for [[jwst]] atmospheric characterization via [[transmission-spectroscopy]].
- Wogan et al. (2026) used a synthetic TRAPPIST-1 e spectrum to demonstrate [[surface-flux-inference]] for biosignature detection (see [[src-biosignature-gas-flux-inference-2026-04]]).
- Despite the habitable-zone planets, [[caleb-scharf]]'s [[interplanetary-habitable-zone]] model finds advanced civilizations in the TRAPPIST-1 system go extinct within ~45 years due to severe radiation from the active host star (see [[src-interplanetary-habitable-zone-2026]]).
- As an M dwarf, TRAPPIST-1 is also a high-risk environment for [[stellar-plasma-scattering]] of narrowband radio [[technosignatures]] (see [[src-plasma-technosignature-scattering-2026]]).
