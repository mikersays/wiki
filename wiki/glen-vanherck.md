**Glen VanHerck** is a retired U.S. Air Force four-star general who served as commander of [[norad]] and [[usnorthcom]]. VanHerck ordered the February 2023 shoot-down of the Chinese high-altitude balloon and was in command during the December 2023 [[langley-afb|Langley]] [[drone-incursions|drone swarm]]. Predecessor to [[gregory-guillot]].

On camera with CBS [[src-60-minutes-drone-swarms-2025|60 Minutes]] (March 2025), VanHerck publicly rejected the Biden administration's "hobbyist" framing of the Langley incursions and warned the activity "could have a foreign nexus." He frames U.S. homeland defense as still structured around a Cold War "fortress America" assumption that no longer holds.

## Key Public Positions

- NORAD radars cannot detect low-flying drones.
- Langley drones were not hobbyists; pattern suggests deliberate surveillance of U.S. critical infrastructure.
- Homeland threat-model assumptions are outdated.
