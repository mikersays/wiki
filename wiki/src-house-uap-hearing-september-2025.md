# Military Whistleblowers Share New Evidence at September 2025 House UAP Hearing

## Summary

On September 9, 2025, the **House Oversight and Government Reform Committee's Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets** (chaired by [[anna-paulina-luna]]) convened its first UAP-focused hearing. The hearing featured testimony from military officials and included the first public showing of military footage depicting a weapons engagement with a UAP. It marked several firsts: first active-duty Navy testimony on UAP before Congress, and first public UAP engagement footage.

## Key Witnesses

### Jeffrey Nuccetelli (Air Force Veteran)

- 16 years of service; testified about UAP incidents between 2003-2005 near **Vandenberg Space Force Base**
- Observed massive objects "like flying buildings" during NRO satellite launches
- "Each incident was witnessed by multiple personnel, documented, investigated and reported up the chain of command."

### Dylan Borland (Air Force Veteran)

- First public disclosure of a **2012 UAP incident at Langley Air Force Base**
- Described **sustained harassment and career obstruction spanning over a decade** after reporting through official channels
- His testimony underscored the need for whistleblower protections in UAP disclosure

### Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins (Active-Duty U.S. Navy)

- **First active-duty Navy official to testify publicly about UAP** before Congress
- 23 years of active service
- Described a **2023 incident aboard the USS Jackson**: a "self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped" object emerged from the ocean, linked up with three similar objects, then disappeared with "high, synchronized, near-instantaneous acceleration"
- Key observables: multiple sensor detection (including radar), no sonic boom, no conventional propulsion signatures, self-luminous, **trans-medium travel**
- The "Tic Tac" description echoes the famous 2004 USS Nimitz encounter

## Unprecedented Video Evidence

Representative Eric Burlison unveiled footage of a **U.S. military MQ-9 Reaper drone firing a Hellfire missile at a high-speed orb** off the coast of Yemen (October 2024). The missile appeared to impact the object, yet the orb **continued moving without apparent damage**. This was the first publicly shown footage of a U.S. military engagement with a UAP.

## Supporting Testimony

- **George Knapp** provided historical context on government UAP secrecy
- **Joe Spielberger** from the **Project on Government Oversight (POGO)** addressed institutional barriers to transparency

## Legislative Priorities

[[anna-paulina-luna]] emphasized:
- Stronger **whistleblower protections** for UAP disclosures
- **Confidential reporting channels** for military personnel
- **Declassification initiatives** for UAP-related data

## Significance

The hearing demonstrated that congressional UAP oversight survived the transition from the 118th to 119th Congress, with new institutional structures continuing the work. The combination of credentialed military witnesses, sensor-corroborated evidence, and engagement footage marked an escalation in the quality of congressional UAP testimony.
