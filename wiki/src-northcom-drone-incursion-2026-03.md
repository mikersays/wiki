## Summary

[[gregory-guillot|Gen. Gregory Guillot]], commander of [[usnorthcom]] and [[norad]], disclosed in written Senate Armed Services Committee testimony that a [[usnorthcom]] [[flyaway-kit|Flyaway Kit (FAK)]] detected and defeated a small unmanned aerial system (sUAS) over an undisclosed "strategic" U.S. military installation in the opening hours of [[operation-epic-fury]], the U.S.-led, Israel-coordinated military campaign against Iran that [[donald-trump|President Trump]] initiated Feb. 28, 2026. The term "strategic" in military parlance often denotes assets tied to the U.S. nuclear arsenal, though NORTHCOM would not confirm a nuclear link or name the base. The incident illustrates the rising threat of [[drone-incursions]] over U.S. military sites and critical infrastructure — a pattern driving the [[ndaa-uap-provisions|FY2026 NDAA]] UAP intercept briefing mandates on [[norad]] and [[usnorthcom]]. Guillot also cited 100+ sUAS mitigations by Joint Task Force-Southern Border and an October 2025 FAK deployment to [[minot-afb]] during [[us-stratcom|STRATCOM]]'s Global Thunder exercise.

## Key Claims

- A deployed FAK successfully detected and defeated sUAS operating over a "strategic" U.S. installation in the opening hours of [[operation-epic-fury]]
- NORTHCOM declined to name the base, confirm nuclear-enterprise ties, or identify the drone's origin/brand, citing operational security
- FAKs are an emerging rapid-response counter-small unmanned aerial system (C-sUAS) capability for defending domestic bases
- A tested FAK configuration includes the Heimdal mobile sensor trailer, [[anduril]] Anvil drone interceptors, the Pulsar electronic-warfare tool, and the Wisp wide-area infrared system
- Joint Task Force-Southern Border has mitigated 100+ sUAS tracking patrols over the past year
- NORTHCOM's FAK team deployed to [[minot-afb]] in October 2025 for Global Thunder, certifying the kit, operators, and processes; Minot hosts nuclear-capable bombers and ICBMs
- Additional FAKs are due in late Spring 2026, deployable anywhere in the NORTHCOM AOR
- Work proceeds in cooperation with Joint Interagency Task Force 401 (JIATF-401)
- Guillot credited lessons from Ukraine for the need for layered, multi-medium drone defeat mechanisms

## Notable Quotes

> "In the early hours of Operation EPIC FURY last month, a deployed FAK successfully detected and defeated sUAS operating over a strategic U.S. installation." — Gen. Gregory Guillot

> "We will not name the base where our Flyaway Kit is deployed in order to preserve operational security." — NORTHCOM spokesperson

> "You can't have just one or two different defeat mechanisms. They have to be across multiple mediums... And we wouldn't have known that without Ukraine." — Gen. Gregory Guillot
