## Summary

Rep. [[anna-paulina-luna]] (R-FL) publicly criticized the Pentagon on April 14-15, 2026 after it missed her April 14 deadline to hand over 45+ UAP videos she had requested in a March letter to Defense Secretary [[pete-hegseth]]. The requested footage reportedly includes spherical, cigar-shaped, and Tic Tac-like objects recorded by U.S. military platforms over war zones, oceans, and restricted airspace, including incidents near Iran, Syria, U.S. bases and airports, and a 2023 Lake Huron event. Luna, who chairs the Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, posted on X that "no one from the Pentagon had responded until we reached out" and that her letter appeared not to have been routed to the "appropriate authorities." The episode extends tensions following [[donald-trump|President Trump]]'s February 2026 UAP disclosure directive ([[src-uap-trump-disclosure-2026]]).

## Key Claims

- Luna's March letter requested 45+ military UAP videos "as soon as possible but no later than April 14"
- Requested videos include spherical, cigar-shaped, and Tic Tac-like objects from over war zones, oceans, and restricted airspace
- Incident set includes events near Iran, Syria, U.S. bases and airports, plus a 2023 Lake Huron incident
- Pentagon failed to respond until Luna's office re-initiated contact; Luna suggests the letter was not routed to "appropriate authorities"
- Luna asserts Trump authorized release and rejects any "future briefing" substitute
- [[pete-hegseth]] said in February he had no timeline but would be "in full compliance with that executive order"
- [[barack-obama]]'s remarks on the Brian Tyler Cohen podcast ("they're real, but I haven't seen them") preceded Trump's February directive
- Pew 2021: ~two-thirds of Americans believe intelligent life likely exists on other planets
