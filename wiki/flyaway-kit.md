A **Flyaway Kit (FAK)** is a NORTHCOM transportable counter-small-unmanned-aerial-system (C-sUAS) capability package. It combines sensors and effectors that can be rapidly deployed to any U.S. base in the [[usnorthcom]] area of responsibility.

## Tested configuration

Per [[src-northcom-drone-incursion-2026-03]]:

- **Heimdal** mobile sensor trailer
- **Pulsar** electronic-warfare tool
- **Wisp** wide-area infrared system
- **Anvil** drone interceptors by [[anduril]]

## Operational history

- **Oct 2025** — First kit, operators, and processes certified at [[minot-afb]] during [[us-stratcom]]'s Global Thunder exercise
- **Feb/Mar 2026** — First operational defeat: FAK detects and defeats a drone over a "strategic" U.S. installation in the opening hours of [[operation-epic-fury]]
- **Late Spring 2026** — Additional FAKs due for deployment

Development proceeds in cooperation with Joint Interagency Task Force 401 (JIATF-401). Gen. [[gregory-guillot]] credits lessons from Ukraine for the need for layered, multi-medium defeat mechanisms.
