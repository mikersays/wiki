# UAP Reverse Engineering and Non-Human Technology Claims: 2025 Overview

## Summary

As of 2025, multiple whistleblowers, congressional investigations, and journalistic efforts have expanded allegations that the U.S. government has recovered and attempted to reverse-engineer craft of [[non-human-intelligence]] origin. This source synthesizes the state of [[uap-reverse-engineering]] claims, official responses, and the credibility landscape.

## The David Grusch Foundation

[[david-grusch]], former USAF intelligence officer and NRO representative to the UAP Task Force, remains the most prominent whistleblower. His key claims (from June 2023 onward):

- The U.S. government operates a **"multi-decade UAP crash retrieval and reverse engineering program"** ([[crash-retrieval-programs]])
- Recovery of **"non-human origin technical vehicles"** -- intact and partially intact craft
- **"Non-human biologics"** recovered from crash sites (testified under oath, July 2023)
- Programs deliberately hidden from congressional oversight for decades
- **"Quite a number"** of craft recovered; both the U.S. and other nations possess materials

The Intelligence Community Inspector General found his complaint **"credible and urgent."**

### Grusch in 2025

Continued public appearances including **The Megyn Kelly Show**, addressing crash retrievals, legacy programs, and disinformation campaigns. [[lue-elizondo]] publicly vouched for him: "a patriot... a damn fine human being."

## The Kona Blue Program

[[kona-blue]] was a proposed DHS program specifically to **reverse-engineer any alien technology recovered by the government**. Unlike many alleged programs, its existence has been **officially acknowledged** by the DoD.

The DoD states Kona Blue **"never got off the ground"** because **"no such technology was ever located."** Proponents argue it may have been a cover story or that materials are held in more deeply classified programs.

## Bob Lazar's Claims

[[bob-lazar]] alleged in 1989 that he worked at **S-4 near Area 51** on [[uap-reverse-engineering]]:

- Personally observed **nine UFOs** undergoing reverse engineering at S-4
- Craft used propulsion capable of producing **gravity and warping space**
- Propulsion relied on **Element 115** (moscovium, later synthesized in 2003)
- Researchers unable to fully understand or replicate the technology

Lazar's claims remain deeply controversial due to unverifiable employment and education records.

## Alleged Derivative Technologies

Some proponents claim modern technologies derived from reverse engineering (fiber optics, microchips, lasers, night vision, Kevlar). These claims are **not supported** by mainstream historical evidence; documented development histories trace each to human researchers and incremental progress.

## AARO Report and Official Denial

[[aaro]] published a historical review concluding:
- **No evidence** the U.S. has recovered alien material
- **No evidence** of reverse-engineered UAP technology
- Many cases attributable to misidentifications

Critics including congressional members argue AARO lacked access to the most sensitive compartmented programs.

## The Arms Race Dimension

[[chris-mellon]] and others allege recovered UAP technology has fueled a **"secretive arms race"** among nations. Secrecy is driven not by public panic concerns but by the strategic value of potential technological breakthroughs.

## Credibility Landscape

| Supporting Factors | Undermining Factors |
|---|---|
| Multiple independent whistleblowers with consistent claims | No physical evidence publicly presented |
| Congressional willingness to legislate | [[aaro]] finding of no evidence |
| ICIG found Grusch's complaint "credible and urgent" | Claims rely primarily on testimony |
| Official acknowledgment of [[kona-blue]] | Scientific community considers claims unproven |
| Multiple sensor-corroborated military encounters | Historical precedent of conventional explanations |
| Growing number of military witnesses | Some key figures have credibility issues |

## Current Status

The debate has shifted from whether UAP deserve investigation (broadly accepted) to whether the U.S. possesses recovered [[non-human-intelligence]] materials (deeply contested). Next inflection points: NDAA-mandated briefings on NORAD/NORTHCOM intercepts and potential [[crash-retrieval-programs]] whistleblower testimony.
