# Military Whistleblowers Share New Evidence of Alleged UAP at Transparency Hearing

**Source:** DefenseScoop, September 9, 2025
**Author:** [[brandi-vincent]]

## Summary

> **Follow-up impact:** This hearing's whistleblower testimony is the evidentiary basis [[anna-paulina-luna]] cites in her March 31, 2026 letter to SecWar [[pete-hegseth]] demanding 46 specifically named UAP videos ([[src-luna-46-uap-videos-demand-2026-03]]). The primary-source URL `oversight.house.gov/hearing/restoring-public-trust-through-uap-transparency-and-whistleblower-protection/` refers to **this September 2025 hearing**, not any separate 2026 event.

On September 9, 2025, the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee's Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets held its first UAP-focused hearing, titled "Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection." Chaired by Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-FL), the hearing featured testimony from current and former military officials who described firsthand encounters with unidentified anomalous phenomena. The hearing also showcased previously unseen video evidence of a U.S. military engagement with an unidentified object and advanced proposed legislation for [[uap-whistleblower-protections]].

## Key Witnesses

### Jeffrey Nuccetelli (Air Force Veteran)

- Testified about encounters near **Vandenberg Air Force Base** (2003-2005)
- Described massive objects "like flying buildings" that pulsed with light and moved in irregular patterns
- Multiple personnel witnessed and documented the incidents
- Received "no guidance down on how to handle these events"
- Highlights the lack of clear reporting chains for military UAP encounters

### Dylan Borland (Air Force Veteran)

- First public testimony about a 2012 UAP incident at **Langley Air Force Base**
- Alleged sustained retaliation after reporting: "my professional career was deliberately obstructed"
- Described workplace harassment and undermining of his classified work eligibility
- Testimony serves as a case study for why specific [[uap-whistleblower-protections]] are needed

### Senior Chief Petty Officer Alexandro Wiggins (U.S. Navy, Active Duty)

- First active-duty Navy official to testify publicly about a UAP incident before [[us-congress-uap-caucus|Congress]]
- 23 years of active service
- Reported a 2023 incident aboard the **USS Jackson**: "a self-luminous Tic Tac-shaped [object] emerged from the ocean"
- Object linked with three similar objects; all four disappeared with "high, synchronized, near-instantaneous acceleration"
- Key observables: multiple sensor detection (including radar), no sonic boom, no conventional propulsion signatures, self-luminous, **trans-medium travel**
- The "Tic Tac" description echoes the famous 2004 USS Nimitz encounter

## Video Evidence

Rep. Eric Burlison (R-MO) unveiled previously unseen footage of a U.S. military **MQ-9 Reaper drone** firing a **Hellfire missile** at a high-speed orb off the coast of Yemen (October 2024). The object appeared to be struck but was not destroyed: "It kept going, and it looked like the debris was taken with it." This represents one of the most significant pieces of visual evidence presented in a congressional hearing, showing direct military engagement with an unidentified object and the apparent failure of a kinetic weapon to neutralize it.

## Whistleblower Protection Legislation

The hearing advanced the **UAP Whistleblower Protection Act (H.R. 5060)**, introduced by Reps. Tim Burchett and Anna Paulina Luna. The bill would codify distinct protections for individuals disclosing information about government-funded UAP research programs to Congress. Joe Spielberger of the Project on Government Oversight testified that whistleblower protections are essential because "misdeeds that they expose can often have a direct impact on public safety, civil liberties and the security of our country." See [[uap-whistleblower-protections]] and [[ndaa-uap-provisions]].

- [[aaro]] achieved full operational capacity in 2024 and manages dozens of open investigations
- Government officials maintain secrecy regarding specific projects, caseloads, and findings
- The hearing reflected bipartisan congressional frustration with the pace of [[uap-disclosure]]
- The hearing demonstrated congressional UAP oversight survived the transition from the 118th to 119th Congress
