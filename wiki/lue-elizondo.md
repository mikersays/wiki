# Luis "Lue" Elizondo

**Luis "Lue" Elizondo** is the former director of the Pentagon's **Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program ([[aatip]])** and a leading figure in the UAP disclosure movement.

## Background

- Former director of [[aatip]]
- Went public in December 2017 through a landmark New York Times article, exposing the Pentagon's UAP investigation program
- His 2017 disclosure triggered the creation of [[immaculate-constellation]] as a successor/parallel program under tighter secrecy

## Advocacy Work

### Capitol Hill (2025)

According to [[src-uap-legislation-disclosure-efforts-2025]], Elizondo traveled to Capitol Hill in March 2025 with [[chris-mellon]] for closed-door meetings with lawmakers including [[anna-paulina-luna]], [[tim-burchett]], and Eric Burlison.

Key statements:
- "Drones can do now what in 2017 we could only dream of"
- "Disclosure and transparency is a process -- it's not an event"
- Expressed confidence: "I think we're going to see potentially even more progress this year than we have in the last seven"

### Support for Other Whistleblowers

Publicly vouched for [[david-grusch]]: "a patriot... a damn fine human being."

## Publications

- **"Imminent"** (2024) -- alleged the U.S. military runs [[uap-reverse-engineering]] programs and recovered [[non-human-intelligence]] specimens
- **"Reckoning: The Unspoken Truth about UFOs and the Urgency of Now"** -- forthcoming (expected 2026)

## Significance

Elizondo's 2017 disclosure is widely regarded as the catalyst for the modern UAP transparency movement. His ongoing advocacy has been instrumental in shaping legislation and sustaining congressional attention on UAP issues.
